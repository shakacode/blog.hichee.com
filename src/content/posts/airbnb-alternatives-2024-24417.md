<h2 class="wp-block-heading has-text-align-center" id="h-airbnb-alternatives-that-are-better-for-short-stays"><strong>Airbnb alternatives that are better for short stays</strong></h2>



<p>Airbnb doesn&#8217;t list hotels, but I mention hotel websites here because there are times and cases when staying at a hotel can be a better option than staying at an Airbnb or other short-term rental (STR).</p>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-short-breaks">Short breaks</h4>



<p>If you stay somewhere for just one or two nights, then a hotel may be your best option. A hotel&#8217;s business model is set up around short stays. They have full-time cleaning staff, whereas short-term rentals like Airbnbs pay their cleaners on a per-cleaning basis. This can make the cost of a one or two night stay at a STR disproportionately expensive.</p>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-city-breaks">City breaks</h4>



<p>Hotels are generally situated in the heart of the city. If you are in the center of town then you won&#8217;t need to get taxis or public transport to see the sights. This can be a big time and money saver over a few days. So, even if a hotel is a little more money than a rental apartment, you may be better off in the long run.</p>



<p>If you plan on a city break and take in the city&#8217;s bars, cafes, and restaurants, you may not need a kitchen if you will be eating out all the time. </p>



<p>Hotels have bag drops which can be super handy if you have to check out mid-morning but don&#8217;t fly out until later in the day. You can drop off your bags and spend the day sightseeing. You&#8217;ll have to drag your luggage around if you check out of an Airbnb-type rental. That&#8217;s never a great day out.</p>
