<p>One of the most important aspects of managing a holiday let is communicating with potential and existing guests. The emails and messages that you send demonstrate to guests that you are an attentive host who cares.</p>



<p>However, writing emails to keep every guest informed and answering the same questions over and over can be one of the most time-consuming tasks. But by utilising email templates or the auto-messaging tool as part of your holiday rental software, you can save time by sending out specific information via automated messages throughout the booking process.</p>



<p>From booking confirmations to payment reminders, here’s a list of essential emails to send guests throughout their booking.</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>How to write a great email template</strong></h2>



<p>The key to writing a great email is to customise the template based on the individual guest so it doesn’t sound like a generic response. You want the message to include all the relevant information while inserting a friendly, personalised tone.</p>



<p>Start with the name of the guest and include answers to any specific questions asked, ideally with links to blog posts with further information. For example, if someone asks if your holiday cottage is suitable for dogs, tell them about your dog – the walks you go on, your secure outdoor space, the doggie daycare right around the corner you use and the pet-friendly restaurants and attractions you recommend.</p>



<p>Your guest will probably be reading your reply on a mobile device so keep your emails short and to the point. Make paragraphs digestible, use bullets to highlight key points and link to your website for further information.</p>



<p>Add your email signature and telephone number to the end of all emails so guests can easily contact you if necessary.</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>The enquiry reply email</strong></h2>



<p>The enquiry is your first email contact with potential holiday let guests and the one that has the potential to convert the enquiry into a booking. Send this as soon as possible after you receive the enquiry. Holidaymakers usually contact several holiday lets at once to see who’s available for their chosen dates so the quicker off the mark you are, the more likely you are to bag the booking.<br><strong><br></strong><strong>Tips to increase bookings</strong><strong>&nbsp;</strong></p>



<p>Always include your property name in the subject so they know who the reply is from and the dates. For example, “Booking enquiry for Fellview Cottage 7-14 September”.</p>



<p>This email should confirm if your property is available for the requested dates – include the dates in the email (and the number of nights) so there’s no room for confusion. Confirm your rate, ideally a fully inclusive rate, if not then breakdown any extra charges for dogs etc.</p>



<p>It’s also a good idea to list your property’s unique selling points as it relates to them specifically and show the extra value you provide over your competitors. For example, any special offers, hot tub, welcome pack or discounts at local attractions.</p>



<p>Include a link to your&nbsp;<a href="https://www.schofields.ltd.uk/blog/3839/create-a-holiday-rental-website/">holiday rental website</a>&nbsp;where you can further convince holidaymakers that your rental is the right one for them. Your website reinforces the information guests saw in your advert and builds trust.</p>



<p>Have a clear call to action – tell them how to book and make it clear and easy. You could offer an incentive to book with you (e.g. a free welcome pack) if they book within 48 hours. Also, create a sense of urgency to convert the booking if you have limited availability, these can seal the booking and are used with great success on travel sites e.g.</p>

<p>Don’t forget to include a link to your&nbsp;<a href="https://www.schofields.ltd.uk/blog/4681/booking-terms-conditions/">terms and conditions and cancellation policy</a>.</p>



<p>How do you reply to a request for a discount? There are certain times when it’s a&nbsp;<a href="https://www.schofields.ltd.uk/blog/3910/holiday-home-special-offers/">good idea to offer a discount or special offer</a>. When it’s not (usually because the dates are popular), simply explain to the enquirer that the dates are likely to get booked so you are unable to offer a discount. Suggest other dates (to fill a booking gap) or recommend that they re-check neared the time – but the dates are likely to get booked up.</p>



<p><strong>Top tip:</strong>&nbsp;If they use their first name in their enquiry then use it in your replies to make it personal. Make your replies friendly and courteous, professional yet chatty.</p>



<p>Ask a question in your reply to build rapport and prompt further dialogue e.g. if they are walkers then ask them if they have done a specific local walk or suggest a few and link to them on your website.</p>



<p>Use your email signature to point them to reviews of your property as well as any social media accounts. Also, include your phone number so they can call with any further questions.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Booking chaser</strong></h2>



<p>Emails sometimes go astray or into spam folders. Many people also send enquiries to several different properties and often just need a gentle nudge to remind them to book before it’s too late.</p>



<p>If you don’t hear back within a couple of days then follow up to make sure the traveller received your reply, and offer to answer any further questions. A simple chaser email can secure a booking, but only chase once and move on if you don’t receive a reply.</p>



<p><strong>Top tip:</strong>&nbsp;If you have a contact number you could also give the enquirer a call. Some owners prefer talking to guests over the phone as it allows them to discuss the property and answer questions.</p>



<p><strong>Booking chaser email</strong></p>



<p>Subject: Reminder: Your enquiry for (property name &amp; dates)</p>



<p><em>Dear (guest name)</em></p>



<p><em>You recently enquired about staying at (property name and website where they found you).</em></p>



<p><em>Since email sometimes goes astray, I’m writing again . . . Have you already found a rental? If not, please let me know if you have any additional questions.</em></p>



<p><em>(Include details on how to book your property).</em></p>



<p><em>If you have already completed your accommodation arrangements, please accept our best wishes for your upcoming travels.</em></p>



<p><em>Regards</em></p>



<p><em>(your name)</em><em><br>(signature with phone number, link to&nbsp; website, social medial profiles and reviews)</em></p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>The property is unavailable</strong></h2>



<p>Don’t simply delete enquiries if you are already booked for the requested dates. If your property isn’t available then offer alternative dates. The enquirer may be flexible or bookmark your property for a future stay.</p>



<p>You can also use the unavailable email for those&nbsp;<a href="https://www.schofields.ltd.uk/blog/3918/refuse-booking-request/">bookings that you want to refuse</a>&nbsp;because they are likely to be more hassle than it’s worth.</p>



<p><strong>Top tip:</strong>&nbsp;If you are booked up then suggest similar properties in your area if you have a referral arrangement with local owners.</p>



<p><strong>Sample unavailable email</strong></p>



<p><em>Subject: Re: your enquiry for (property name &amp; dates)</em></p>



<p><em>Dear (guest name),</em></p>



<p><em>Thank you for enquiring about (property name). Unfortunately, our holiday let is booked during the period you requested. Are your dates flexible by any chance? I keep an up-to-date calendar on my website (link to availability calendar).</em></p>



<p><em>If you’re flexible, please let me know – or feel free to resubmit your request on our website (website).</em></p>



<p><em>Regards,</em></p>



<p><em>(your name)</em><em><br>(signature with phone number, link to&nbsp; website, social medial profiles and reviews)</em></p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Holiday let booking confirmation&nbsp;</strong><strong>template</strong></h2>



<p>The holiday let booking confirmation is probably the most important email because it contains all the important information you and the guest need to be clear about.</p>



<p>The holiday home booking confirmation form should include:</p>



<p><strong>Booking number<br></strong><strong>Owner information</strong>&nbsp;– name, address, contact details.<br><strong>Guest(s) details –</strong>&nbsp;name, address, contact details.<br><strong>Dates</strong>&nbsp;– confirm the date of arrival/departure, the number of nights, check-in and check-out time.<br><strong>Number of guests</strong>&nbsp;– confirm the number of people who will be staying.<br><strong>Cost&nbsp;</strong>– confirm the exact payment required for the stay including any extras such as the cleaning or dog fee.<br><strong>Security deposit</strong>&nbsp;(if applicable).<br><strong>Payment schedule</strong>&nbsp;– list the dates when payment(s) are due. Include your bank details if the guest is paying via bank transfer.</p>



<p><strong>Top tip:</strong>&nbsp;Think of this booking confirmation email almost as a contract – make sure all the details are crystal-clear with a copy of your&nbsp;<a href="https://www.schofields.ltd.uk/blog/4681/booking-terms-conditions/">terms and conditions</a>.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Tips and ideas</strong></h2>



<p>Around four weeks before your guests are due to arrive, send over some helpful tips for how they can make the most of their time at your property.</p>



<p>Send them your recommendations (<a href="https://www.schofields.ltd.uk/blog/4926/create-blog/">blog posts</a>) of things to see, do and where to eat in your local area. For those guests visiting during peak season, recommend that they book in advance.</p>



<p>For example:<br>-Top 10 restaurants<br>-Best beach guide<br>-Weather guide and what to do on a rainy day<br>-An&nbsp;<a href="https://www.schofields.ltd.uk/blog/3078/self-catering-cottage-inventory-list/">inventory list</a>&nbsp;of what items are provided</p>



<p>Include information about any question that has previously been asked more than twice and a copy of your&nbsp;<a href="https://www.schofields.ltd.uk/blog/3505/guest-information-book/">guestbook</a>.<strong>&nbsp;</strong></p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Check-in and directions</strong></h2>

<p>Provide them with:</p>

<li>Directions to your property (include a GPS reference and/or Google map coordinates).</li>

</ul>



<p><strong>Top tip:</strong>&nbsp;It’s also a good opportunity to share some key information that they may need quick access to upon their arrival like the Wi-Fi details, heating instructions, etc.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Check-up message</strong></h2>



<p>The day after check-in send an email or text message asking how they have settled in, if they have everything they need or do they have any questions. This can help to protect you from negative reviews as some guests may not inform you of any issues. A quick check-up message gives them a chance to bring any problems or questions to your attention.</p>



<p>You could say something like:</p>



<p><em>Hi (guest name),</em></p>



<p><em>I just wanted to make sure you are enjoying your holiday and you settled in ok.</em></p>



<p><em>If there is anything I can do to make your stay more enjoyable, or if you have any questions please don’t hesitate to ask.</em></p>



<p><em>Regards,</em></p>



<p><em>(your name)</em><em><br>(signature with phone number, link to&nbsp; website, social medial profiles and reviews)</em></p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Checkout Instructions</strong></h2>



<p>Leaving clear checkout instructions lets your guests know what you expect from them at the end of their stay so there is no room for misunderstanding. This makes the job of your cleaner much easier, allowing them enough time to take care of everything before your next guests arrive.</p>



<p>Send this email before they arrive and again a couple of days before your guests leave.</p>



<p>State anything the guests need to do before leaving the property, such as:</p>



<p>– Confirm the checkout time they have to leave by<br>– Remind guests to turn off all the lights<br>– Set the thermostat back to a specific temperature<br>– Close all windows and lock doors before leaving<br>– Dispose of any food they brought with them<br>– Washup/load the dishwasher<br>– Empty the bins<br>– What the guests should do with the keys when they leave<br>– Inform you of any damage, missing items or problems encountered during their stay.</p>



<p>If have had issues in the past you may want to consider adding a late checkout fee in the booking terms and conditions/house rules. Explain why it’s so important that guests check out on time so your guests understand why you are enforcing it.</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>The follow-up email</strong></h2>



<p>Within days of your guests’ departure, send them an email thanking them for being your guests. You can also use this email to encourage loyalty by turning first-time guests into repeat bookers.</p>



<p>Things to include:</p>



<ul>
<li>Offer a repeat friend and family booking discount.</li>



<li>A request to follow or like your social media profiles.</li>



<li>Communicate details of the security deposit – when you’re returning it, and how much.</li>



<li>Ask them to subscribe to your newsletter to hear about your special offers and news about your area.</li>
</ul>

<p><em>Subject: Your recent stay at (property name)</em></p>



<p><em>Dear (guest name)</em></p>



<p><em>We do hope you enjoyed your holiday in (property name) and that you have happy memories of your stay.</em></p>



<p><em>If you have any comments about our holiday home or suggestions about how we could improve it, we would love to hear them.</em></p>



<p><em>We hope that we may be welcoming you back to our holiday home in the future. As an extra way to say ‘thank you’ for choosing us, if you or your friends wish to book again you are entitled to X% off the rental rates. Simply add (code) when you book online through our website.</em></p>



<p><em>Regards,</em></p>



<p><em>(your name)</em><em><br>(signature with phone number, link to&nbsp; website, social medial profiles and reviews)</em></p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Ask for a review</strong></h2>



<p>Reviews are essential for building trust in prospective guests and improving your guest experience. But unless they are asked, guests are unlikely to leave a review about their stay.</p>



<p>It’s important that you encourage guests to tell other people why they enjoyed their stay so much. Sending them a link to the place where they can leave their review and remind them of their returning guest discount.</p>



<p><strong>Sample review request email</strong></p>



<p><em>Subject: We would love your feedback on (property name)</em></p>



<p><em>Hi (name),</em></p>



<p><em>We wanted to thank you again for choosing to stay with us and hope that you’ll come back and stay with us again.</em></p>



<p><em>Please could you take a few seconds to write a review about our (property name) at: (insert website where they can write a review, e.g. your website or the site where they found you/booked).</em></p>



<p><em>It only takes a few minutes and helps other guests benefit from your experience and advice.</em></p>



<p><em>Any feedback you have is greatly appreciated.</em></p>



<p><em>Regards,</em></p>



<p><em>(your name)</em><em><br>(signature with phone number, link to&nbsp; website, social medial profiles and reviews)</em></p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Booking cancellations and r</strong><strong>efund requests</strong></h2>



<p>Hopefully, booking cancellations will be rare. It’s a difficult situation to deal with and each owner&nbsp;<a href="https://www.schofields.ltd.uk/blog/3888/holiday-rental-refund-2/">deals with cancellations</a>&nbsp;differently depending on the reason for cancelling and the&nbsp;<a href="https://www.schofields.ltd.uk/blog/4681/booking-terms-conditions/">cancellation terms in the booking contract</a>.</p>



<p>Recommend that guests take out travel insurance and that it’s not your responsibility to refund if they choose not to take out insurance.</p>



<p>The following email may be useful, but tailor it to the individuals circumstances and whether you are able to re-advertise the dates or not. Try to be as understanding with your guests as possible and add condolences if they’ve had to cancel due to family bereavement.</p>

<p><em>Subject: Cancellation of your holiday at (property name and dates)</em></p>

<p><em>If we are unable to re-let the dates, we will refund any costs which we will not incur due to your cancellation, being (the cleaning and estimated utility costs).</em></p>



<p><em>If you would like us to supply any paperwork to support a travel insurance claim then please let us know.</em></p>



<p><em>We will let you know if we manage to re-let the dates and confirm any refund due.</em></p>



<p><em>Regards,</em></p>



<p><em>(your name)</em><em><br>(signature with phone number, link to&nbsp; website, social medial profiles and reviews)</em></p>
