<p><strong>Airbnb Becomes AirAnB &#8211; It’s Now A Two Tier System That May Kill Millions Of Host Businesses</strong></p>
<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-at-a-glance">At a glance</h2>
</p>
<p><strong>Airbnb has introduced a Guest Favorite badge, which splits all listings into haves and have-nots. Only 2 million properties have the badge. That&#8217;s around one-quarter of Airbnb listings.<br /></strong><br />But here&#8217;s the kicker, Airbnb&#8217;s CEO <strong>Brian Chesky says, “he can’t imagine that there are a lot of use cases where you (a traveler) wouldn’t want to book a guest favorite. “ <br /></strong><br /><strong>Of the location search results I checked, guest favorite badged listings account for 60% of the listings displayed. </strong></p>
<p><strong>Those listings without a badge make up less than 9% of the displayed properties.</strong></p>
<p><strong>Your property will struggle for exposure if you don&#8217;t have the new badge.</strong></p>
<p>This article looks at the full details.</p>
<div class="wp-block-qubely-divider qubely-block-c3d401">
<div class="qubely-block-divider ">
<div class="qubely-block-divider-style-dot"></div>
</div>
</div>
</p>
<p>You may have read about Airbnb’s winter release, as there’s been a fair amount written about it, but I did a bit of digging, and it may affect you much more than you thought.</p>
<p>The winter release changes are already in place, and this article will take a deeper dive into the new ‘Guest Favorite’ badge that could destroy half or more of&nbsp;Airbnb host&#8217;s businesses.</p>
<figure class="wp-block-image"><img decoding="async" src="https://lh7-us.googleusercontent.com/uU8r56_VSyjC0AMEIvwFH9LS1VBOQixb1D18XS2g65AM1G-sNLPDoMKjfiDRv5J_MP5WH6a4q-5jGtqEh2wMCTM-sGpXaBoWiyVdHPVTE9U_nZFAlx77WIzbcVNypFdGXOz2ZKumPBSbLoB6s7LWWQ" alt=""/></figure>
</p>
<p>For those who don’t know, the guest favorite badge is awarded to individual listings depending on several criteria.</p>
<p>The badge is based on a combination of high guest ratings (above 4.9 stars on average), a significant number of positive reviews (at least five), and a track record of reliability (with Host cancellations and quality-related issues being very low, around 1% on average).</p>
<p><a href="https://news.airbnb.com/en-uk/airbnb-introduces-guest-favourites/">https://news.airbnb.com/en-uk/airbnb-introduces-guest-favourites/</a></p>
<p>To cut a long story short, 2 million listings have been awarded the guest favorite badge.&nbsp;</p>
<p>This badge also trumps the Superhost badge &#8211; awarded to individual hosts instead of individual properties (no matter how many listings a host may manage).</p>
<p>So, Airbnb is now operating a two-tier system—listings with the guest&#8217;s favorite badge and those without.&nbsp;</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-the-airbnb-superhost-badge">The Airbnb Superhost badge</h2>
</p>
<figure class="wp-block-image size-medium is-resized"><img decoding="async" width="300" height="290" src="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/12/Screenshot-2023-12-14-at-17.55.37-300x290.png" alt="Airbnb Superhosts" class="wp-image-82065" style="width:300px" srcset="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/12/Screenshot-2023-12-14-at-17.55.37-300x290.png 300w, https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/12/Screenshot-2023-12-14-at-17.55.37.png 516w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></figure>
</p>
<p>The&nbsp;<a href="https://www.airbnb.com/d/superhost">Superhost program</a>&nbsp;was created in 2014 to celebrate and reward the top-rated and most experienced Airbnb Hosts.&nbsp;Earlier this year, Airbnb announced that there are now over 1 million Superhosts worldwide.</p>
<p>But here&#8217;s the shocker&#8230;</p>
<figure class="wp-block-pullquote has-theme-palette-9-color has-text-color has-background has-link-color wp-elements-9c3119b4e6e393b98364248a85590300" style="background-color:#eb4c60">
<blockquote>
<p>T<strong>he typical Superhost earns 64% more than a regular Host</strong>.</p>
</blockquote>
</figure>
</p>
<p><a href="https://news.airbnb.com/airbnb-celebrates-1-million-superhosts/#:~:text=Airbnb%20hits%20a%20new%20milestone,income%20from%20hosting%20on%20Airbnb." target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">This statistic is from Airbnb themselves</a></p>
<p><strong>As you can see from the above statistic, badges make a difference.</strong>  That&#8217;s the point of having badges. </p>
<p class="has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-f3d8eee843bd42be9f786b8d0db26d41" style="color:#f30707"><strong>But the downside is that badges divert attention away from non-badged listings.</strong></p>

<p><strong>The guest favorite badge is meant to indicate rentals with hotel reliability.</strong></p>

<p>Airbnb has around 7.8 million listings. He didn’t say they took these signals and determined the bottom 5.8 million homes. Because, like it or not, that’s the other side of the coin. </p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-conclusion"><strong>Conclusion</strong></h2>
</p>
<p>Airbnb in now a two tier system with the haves (exposure and bookings) and the have-nots.&nbsp;</p>
<p>Airbnb has a filter that literally points out their two-tier system. If they have a top tier, and you&#8217;re not in it &#8211; That makes you a bottom-tier host.</p>
<figure class="wp-block-image"><img decoding="async" src="https://lh7-us.googleusercontent.com/2J-cGJxbygpG7Fyju1mYtd2S2y1QctQY4RxgMFvzoyEj4t21Ask0uisUj_sCXyRkk5LHBpcgRWkJRjBBuXhOg8CCUTX0T8tweRdNAhNjpidgEsdfuVuYLz-on44yfiMbC8G6odC2t_p1v0PplhK3nQ" alt=""/></figure>
</p>
<p>In the past, the company had an upper level, a select category called Airbnb Plus. They now have, in effect, Airbnb Minus. If you aren’t a guest favorite, you will be buried by the algorithm, struggle with exposure, and miss out on bookings.</p>
<p>When Chesky says that “he can’t imagine that there are a lot of use cases where you wouldn’t want to book a guest favorite” (property), he’s also saying that he can’t imagine people wanting to book non-guest-favorite listings.</p>
<p>Chesky doesn’t have to answer to hosts; he only has to answer to board members and investors. It looks like he has abandoned around 4 million hosts because he wants an Airbnb experience to be more aligned with a hotel experience than an actual Airbnb experience.</p>
<p>If you are a host and you don’t have the guest favorite badge, you can check your listing views&nbsp;<a href="https://www.airbnb.com/help/article/687" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">here</a>. </p>
<p>The gig is up for many in this particular gig economy.</p>
