<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Why do vacation rental hosts and managers have cancellation policies?</strong></h2>



<p>In order to answer this question, we need to look at things from a host or manager&#8217;s perspective. Let’s imagine that a guest has booked two weeks in high season, but they cancel just days before they are due to arrive. These are two of the most expensive weeks of the year, and the chances of the host rebooking these dates at such short notice are very slim.</p>



<p>This host will, in effect, be thousands of dollars (or whatever currency) out of pocket. This is a huge financial hit, and the property will be sitting empty. The pool guy and the cleaning staff have all been booked and still need to be paid.<br><br><strong>This is a host or manager&#8217;s worst nightmare.<br></strong><br>So, to offset the risk of this happening, hosts and managers have clearly stated cancellation policies that mitigate the pain when this happens.<br><br>For example, some operators will refund the guest in full if they cancel 60 days before arrival but will only refund the guest 50% of the booking if they cancel 30 days before the due arrival date.</p>



<p>The thing is, there are a myriad of cancellation policies depending on how, where, and when you book.</p>



<p>The three biggest booking platforms, Airbnb, Booking, and VRBO, all have numerous options that the host can choose from, so let’s look at those first…</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">What happens if the host cancels an Airbnb booking?</h2>



<p>There may be occasions when a host has to cancel your booking. These could include things like double bookings, the property has been damaged, earthquakes, hurricanes, a pandemic, etc.</p>



<p>Here&#8217;s what Airbnb will do if a host cancels a booking&#8230;</p>



<figure class="wp-block-pullquote has-small-font-size" style="font-style:italic;font-weight:400"><blockquote><p><br><br>While it’s rare, sometimes a Host may need to cancel a reservation. Rest assured that every booking made on Airbnb comes with <a href="https://www.airbnb.com/aircover">AirCover</a>, protection for guests against significant issues, including Host cancellations within 30 days of check-in. <br>In the unlikely event a Host needs to cancel your booking within 30 days of check-in, you’ll automatically get a refund and we can help you find a similar or better home to rebook.<br><br></p></blockquote></figure>
