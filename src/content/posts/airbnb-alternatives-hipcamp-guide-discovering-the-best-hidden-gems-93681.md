<p>Airbnb Alternatives &#8211; Hipcamp &#8211; If a tree falls in a forest, and there’s no one around to hear it, does it make a sound? If you have a great vacation and no one wants to hear about it, did it really happen?</p>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p>Read on to find out&#8230;</p>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p>There are so many alternatives to Airbnb these days that writing a list would take up a whole vacation. But most of them are homogeneous. They all offer the same old, same old listings, and that&#8217;s a problem. </p>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p>There&#8217;s a lot more to a vacation than going on vacation. There&#8217;s the anticipation, the experience,  the memories, and the stories that you can share.</p>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p>In the early days of Airbnb, part of the attraction, the allure if you like, was telling friends and colleagues that you had done something different. You had Airbnb&#8217;d, and they hadn&#8217;t. The resulting story would set you apart. You were a trendsetter.</p>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p>These days, you&#8217;d be hard-pressed to find someone who hadn&#8217;t stayed at an Airbnb. Airbnb has, to some extent, lost its kudus. Staying at an Airbnb has become a vanilla experience. It doesn&#8217;t pique people&#8217;s interest like it used to.</p>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p>And that&#8217;s where Hipcamp comes in.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-what-is-hipcamp-and-how-does-it-work"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="What_is_Hipcamp_and_how_does_it_work"></span><strong>What is Hipcamp, and how does it work?</strong><span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h2>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p>Simply put, Hipcamp is an online marketplace connecting landowners with people looking to experience outdoor stays and camping. Its platform allows users to discover and book unique outdoor stays, including campsites, RV parks, cabins, tiny homes, treehouses, and glamping options. </p>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p><strong>Here&#8217;s a detailed breakdown of how Hipcamp works for prospective guests (campers):</strong></p>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<ol></p>
<li><strong>Searching and Booking:</strong>
<ul></p>
<li>Guests can search for campsites based on location, dates, type of accommodation, and other preferences.</li>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<li>Detailed listings help guests find the right match for their needs, including information about amenities, activities, and reviews from previous guests.</li>
<p></ul>
<p></li>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<li><strong>Booking Process:</strong>
<ul></p>
<li>Once a guest finds a suitable listing, they can book it through Hipcamp’s platform. The booking includes the total cost, encompassing the host’s price and a service fee for Hipcamp.</li>
<p></ul>
<p></li>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<li><strong>Stay and Experience:</strong>
<ul></p>
<li>After booking, guests receive details about the location and any specific instructions from the host.</li>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<li>Guests enjoy their stay, which can range from basic camping to more luxurious glamping experiences.</li>
<p></ul>
<p></li>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<li><strong>Reviews and Feedback:</strong>
<ul></p>
<li>After the stay, guests can leave reviews about their experience, which helps future guests make informed decisions and assists hosts in improving their offerings.</li>
<p></ul>
<p></li>
<p></ol>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Unique Features of Hipcamp:</h3>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<ul></p>
<li><strong>Diverse Options:</strong> Hipcamp offers a variety of outdoor accommodations, from simple tent sites to unique structures.</li>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<li><strong>Accessibility:</strong>&nbsp;Makes it easier for people to find and enjoy outdoor experiences.</li>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<li><strong>Community Focus:</strong>&nbsp;Emphasizes building a community of outdoor enthusiasts and responsible travelers.</li>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<li><strong>Environmental Consciousness:</strong>&nbsp;Many listings promote sustainable and eco-friendly practices.</li>
<p></ul>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p>Overall, Hipcamp is designed to make outdoor stays more accessible and enjoyable while allowing landowners to monetize their unused land. It caters to a range of outdoor experiences, appealing to a broad audience from hardcore campers to those seeking a more comfortable glamping experience.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-is-hipcamp-only-in-the-us"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Is_Hipcamp_only_in_the_US"></span>Is Hipcamp only in the US?<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h2>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p>Hipcamp is a US startup and, to begin with was only offering US campsites to choose from. It has since expanded into Canada, The UK, France, and Australia.</p>

Once the sale had gone through, Hipcamp closed Youcamp and migrated the listings to Hipcamp. Nothing unusual there &#8211; But Hipcamp introduced a 20% service fee and that upset existing users of Youcamp. <h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-hipcamp-pricing"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Hipcamp_Pricing"></span>Hipcamp Pricing<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h2>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p>Hosts offer a variety of experiences to Hipcampers, from primitive campsites to cabins to Airstreams and more. Below are average prices for different types of accommodations offered on Hipcamp, though these numbers can vary by location:</p>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<ul></p>
<li>Tent Sites: $10-30 per night</li>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<li>RV sites: $20-40 per night</li>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<li>Structures: (cabins, glamping tents, yurts, treehouses, etc.) $75+ per night</li>
<p></ul>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p>Some structures can run into hundreds of dollars a night, but the majority that I looked at were less than $100 a night in high season.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-what-are-hipcamp-s-policies-on-environmental-conservation-and-sustainability"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="What_are_Hipcamps_policies_on_environmental_conservation_and_sustainability"></span><strong>What are Hipcamp’s policies on environmental conservation and sustainability?</strong><span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h2>

<p></p>
<p>Hipcamp&#8217;s stance and initiatives on eco-friendliness and sustainable practices reflect a growing trend in the travel and tourism industry towards environmental responsibility. As a platform that facilitates outdoor stays, Hipcamp inherently promotes a closer connection with nature, which aligns with a broader commitment to sustainability.</p>

<p></p>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Promotion of Sustainable Tourism:</strong></h3>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<ul></p>
<li><strong>Low-Impact Accommodations:</strong>&nbsp;Hipcamp encourages the use of existing natural spaces for camping, minimizing the need for extensive construction or development that could harm the environment.</li>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<li><strong>Eco-Friendly Listings:</strong>&nbsp;Many Hipcamp hosts offer eco-friendly accommodations, such as solar-powered cabins or tents, and practices like composting toilets and organic farming.</li>
