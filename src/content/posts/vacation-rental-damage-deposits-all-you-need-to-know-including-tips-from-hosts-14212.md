<p>Accidents happen. They are part of life. Just because you are on vacation does not mean you are immune to accidents. In this article, we will look into vacation rental damage deposits, what they are, what’s covered (and what isn’t), and whether you should take out insurance just in case an accident happens.</p>



<p>We also asked thousands of vacation rental hosts and managers how they handle various types of damage, common problems that they deal with, and what the best course of action is for guests that cause accidental damage.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>What are damage deposits?</strong></h2>



<p>A damage deposit (sometimes called a security deposit) is often a pre-agreed fee (deposit) that is paid to the host of the rental prior to renting. This fee will be returned in full once you check out &#8211; as long as no damage has been incurred.&nbsp;</p>



<p>As a guest, you are staying in someone else’s home (very expensive homes in some cases), and if you cause damage, the host will have to repair or replace whatever has been damaged. These costs will be taken out of your security deposit if you have caused any damage.</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>How much will I need to pay on deposit?</strong></h2>



<p>Generally, hosts either require a flat fee or a percentage of the booking total.<br><br>The actual figure will depend largely on the size and type of the property. The damage deposit on a small rustic cabin will be much less than the deposit on a high-end large luxury villa.<br><br>On the lower end, you can expect to deposit between $200 and $500. On the upper end of the scale, you could have to put down between $500 and $2,000.</p>



<p>Some owners work on a percentage of the rental fee. This is commonly between 10% and 20% of the total cost of the accommodation. So, you may have to deposit between $1,000 and $2,000 for a $10,000 rental.&nbsp;</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>What’s covered and what’s not?</strong></h2>



<p>This is a grey area and will, to some degree, vary on a host-to-host basis and will depend on the degree of damage that has occurred.<br><br>Small accidents like a broken cup or a stained towel will often be put down to wear and tear. Most hosts will factor in a small number of breakages as part of running a hospitality business. Most will absorb the cost of these minor accidents.&nbsp;</p>



<p>Larger mishaps, like a broken window, stained sofa, or scratched table, will result in some (or all) of your deposit being withheld. If major damage occurs, you could find yourself losing all of your deposit, and you may be taken to court so that the host can claim for total damages (and loss of earnings in some cases &#8211; if they are forced to stay empty while repairs take place).</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>When will you get your damage deposit money back?</strong></h2>



<p>If all is well and you have caused no damage, as is the case with the vast majority of stays, you will receive your deposit back shortly after checkout. Once the host or cleaning staff have checked the property and confirmed that there is no damage, you should receive your money back within hours or a couple of days.</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>What happens if you have caused some damage to the property or contents?</strong></h2>



<p>Some, or all, of your damage deposit, will be retained by the host or manager in order to cover the cost of replacement or repair of what has been damaged.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Booking through the big box travel agencies</h2>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img decoding="async" width="1024" height="353" src="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/02/Booking-through-the-big-box-travel-agencies-1024x353.png" alt="Booking through the big box travel agencies" class="wp-image-14225" srcset="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/02/Booking-through-the-big-box-travel-agencies-1024x353.png 1024w, https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/02/Booking-through-the-big-box-travel-agencies-300x103.png 300w, https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/02/Booking-through-the-big-box-travel-agencies-768x265.png 768w, https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/02/Booking-through-the-big-box-travel-agencies-1536x530.png 1536w, https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/02/Booking-through-the-big-box-travel-agencies.png 1780w" sizes="(max-width: 1024px) 100vw, 1024px" /></figure>



<p>Each of the main three online travel agencies, Airbnb, Booking, and VRBO, handle vacation rental damage deposits in somewhat different ways. We outline each company&#8217;s policies below.</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>What happens if you book through Airbnb?</strong></h2>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img decoding="async" width="128" height="128" src="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/10/2613311_airbnb_apartment-rentals_homestays_hospitality_hotel-rooms_icon.png" alt="Airbnb" class="wp-image-3817"/></figure>



<p>Airbnb&#8217;s security deposit policies are designed to protect both guests and hosts. The deposit is intended to cover damages that may occur during a guest&#8217;s stay up to the amount specified by the host. If there isn&#8217;t any damage to the rental property, the deposit will be fully refunded to the guest. If there is damage, the deposit can be used to cover the cost of repairs.&nbsp;</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Do I pay a damage deposit when booking an Airbnb?</strong></h2>



<p>Hosts aren’t allowed to charge guests a security deposit through Airbnb’s <a href="https://www.airbnb.com/resolutions">Resolution Center</a> or outside the Airbnb platform. Instead, Airbnb informs guests at the time of booking that their payment method may be charged if they cause damage during a stay.</p>



<p>There is one exception: Hosts who manage their listings with <a href="https://www.airbnb.com/help/article/2683/">API-connected software</a> can set a security deposit using Airbnb’s offline fees feature. If a Host has done this, the deposit requirements will be clearly communicated during the booking process.</p>



<p>In the case of Airbnb bookings, the damage deposit is held by Airbnb, not the host.</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Damage reimbursements without a security deposit</strong></h2>



<p>If you, as a guest, someone you invite, or your pet causes damage during a stay, the Host can request reimbursement from you through Airbnb’s Resolution Center.&nbsp;</p>



<p>If Airbnb gets involved and determines that you are responsible, your payment method may be charged.</p>



<p>If you&#8217;re a guest, <a href="https://www.airbnb.com/help/article/1415/">learn more</a> about what to do if you cause damage here. If you&#8217;re a Host, <a href="https://www.airbnb.com/help/article/279/">learn more</a> about how to request reimbursement for damage, missing items, and unexpected cleaning here.</p>



<p>Read the rental agreement prior to booking to find out more.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>What happens if you book through Booking (.com)?</strong></h2>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-full is-resized"><img decoding="async" src="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/10/booking-logo.jpeg" alt="Booking prices" class="wp-image-3814" width="128" height="128" srcset="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/10/booking-logo.jpeg 256w, https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/10/booking-logo-150x150.jpeg 150w" sizes="(max-width: 128px) 100vw, 128px" /></figure>



<p>Booking (.com) has two different damage protection options. The first is their damage deposit option. The second is their damage program option.</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Damage Deposit</strong></h2>



<p>The damage deposit option allows the host to collect deposit money upfront in case a guest causes damage to the property or items during their stay.</p>



<p>The host will manage the entire damage payment process themselves.</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Here’s how a damage deposit works:</strong></h2>



<ul>
<li>Before their stay, the guest gives the host a deposit directly. The host defines how and when they collect this amount, and they manage the money themselves.&nbsp;</li>



<li>The host must collect and return the amount per the settings they defined at the time of booking.&nbsp;</li>
</ul>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Damage Program</strong></h2>



<p>The damage program allows Booking.com to facilitate the damage payment for the host at no additional cost.&nbsp;</p>



<p>With this option, BDC take care of everything, from contacting the guest if damage occurs to facilitating the payment process on the host’s behalf.</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Here’s how the damage program works:</strong></h2>



<ul>
<li>The guest doesn’t pay a deposit upfront – they only pay if they cause damage during their stay and the host reports it to them.</li>
</ul>



<ul>
<li>The host can only ask the guest to pay for the value of the damage caused, up to a maximum amount that the host selects when setting up the damage program.</li>
</ul>



<ul>
<li>In the event that damage does occur, the host submits a damage payment request that the guest will be asked to pay if they cause damage to the property.</li>
</ul>



<ul>
<li>The host has <strong>14 days</strong> after the guest checks out to submit a request via Booking.com.</li>
</ul>



<ul>
<li>BDC contact the guest, informing them that damage has occurred during their stay and that the host is requesting payment from them.</li>
</ul>



<ul>
<li>If the host’s damage payment request is accepted, BDC will pay it out to the host via bank transfer. This process can take <strong>up to 30 days</strong>.</li>
</ul>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>What happens if you book through VRBO?</strong></h2>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-full is-resized"><img decoding="async" src="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/10/VRBO-logo.jpeg" alt="VRBO pricing" class="wp-image-3815" width="128" height="128" srcset="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/10/VRBO-logo.jpeg 256w, https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/10/VRBO-logo-150x150.jpeg 150w" sizes="(max-width: 128px) 100vw, 128px" /></figure>



<p>At the time of booking, hosts may request one of the following as a damage deposit:</p>



<ul>
<li>A card on file</li>



<li>An upfront refundable damage deposit</li>



<li>That you purchase property damage protection to cover potential damage and/or incidentals at the property</li>
</ul>



<p><strong>Note:</strong> The amount required is set by the host. You’re shown this amount under the house rules on the property details page or during checkout before you book.&nbsp;</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Properties requiring a card on file</strong></h2>



<p>If the property requires a card on file, you’re only charged if the host files a claim. Your credit card information isn’t shared with them, and there’s no pre-authorization hold. The host has 14 days after checkout to assess the property and submit a claim.</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Properties requiring a refundable damage deposit</strong></h2>



<p>If the property requires a refundable damage deposit, this deposit is paid upfront at the time of booking and is refunded after 14 days if no claim is filed. Your financial institution may take up to seven business days to release the money into your account.</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Properties offering Property Damage Protection</strong></h2>



<p>Property Damage Protection is an optional insurance product that’s intended to protect you up to the coverage amount should damage occur to the rental property during your stay. Some hosts may allow you to purchase this option in lieu of paying a refundable damage deposit upfront. Please be sure to review the coverage details before selecting this option.<br><br>For high-end rentals, this is often the best method as you have far higher coverage compared to the other options.</p>

<p>This is probably the most common issue where the host retains some of the damage deposit.&nbsp;</p>



<p>The most common cause here is generally sunscreen. Some sunscreens, like P20 for example, contain avobenzone which reacts with iron in hard water and will leave stains that are next to impossible to remove.&nbsp;</p>



<p><strong>Here’s what hosts say about this…</strong></p>



<figure class="wp-block-pullquote is-style-plain has-theme-palette-9-background-color has-background has-small-font-size" style="font-style:italic;font-weight:400"><blockquote><p><br><em>I get the most damage to bedding and towels and specifically from suncream stains. Some brands leave yellow stains that show up on white or cream fabric, but only once they’ve been washed.</em></p></blockquote></figure>



<figure class="wp-block-pullquote is-style-plain has-small-font-size"><blockquote><p><br><em>I have only needed to use the damage deposit a couple of times. Once for a smashed lamp and once for sheets that were balled up in closet covered in some indiscernible substance.</em> <br><br><em>Had they not done the hiding in the closet I would not have charged.</em></p></blockquote></figure>



<p></p>



<p>Mascara and other creams can also cause issues with towels.</p>



<figure class="wp-block-pullquote has-small-font-size"><blockquote><p><br><em>The only claim I have made was via Airbnb when guests trashed virtually all the towels (only 2 months old) with their son&#8217;s skin cream.</em></p></blockquote></figure>



<p></p>



<p>Most hosts provide white towels and white bedding so that guests can see that everything provided is super clean. The downside is that these white linens also highlight stains, and a stained white sheet or towel is often a write-off and needs to be replaced.&nbsp;</p>



<p>If a family is returning from the beach, and they are hot and sweaty, and they each grab a towel to wipe their faces, they can ruin four towels in no time at all. All of a sudden, they are looking at $100 or more of their damage deposit being withheld as the host will need to replace the ruined items.</p>
