<p>Airbnb Alternatives &#8211; Airbnb v Booking.com &#8211; so you just found a place on Airbnb and are about to book it. But there&#8217;s that little niggle that maybe you could get a better deal or cancellation policy elsewhere.</p>

<p></p>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-highlights">Highlights</h3>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p>I looked at over 10,000 price comparisons between Airbnb and Booking.com. These were the same properties, same dates, and same number of guests, for 7 days or more.</p>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p>Airbnb was, on average, 13% cheaper in 50% of cases, but Booking.com was also, on average, 13% cheaper in the other 50% of cases.</p>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<ul></p>
<li>Very rarely were the prices the same on both platforms</li>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<li>There are no hard and fast rules regarding which platform is cheaper for any particular property.</li>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<li>It very much pays to check pricing between platforms before you book.</li>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<li>The average saving that I found was 13%</li>
<p></ul>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p class="has-background" style="background-color:#72ede5"><strong><strong>Note. The average booking spend is around $1,000 (4 nights at $250), so if a property is listed on both Airbnb and Booking.com, average savings are around $130 if you take the time to shop around.</strong></strong></p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-airbnb">Airbnb</h3>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p>During the last 15 years, I&#8217;ve witnessed the meteoric rise of Airbnb firsthand. </p>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p>This innovative platform, conceived in 2008 by founders Brian Chesky, Joe Gebbia, and Nathan Blecharczyk, initially addressed San Francisco&#8217;s acute hotel room shortage during a major conference. Their ingenious solution? Turning their apartment into an &#8220;Air Bed and Breakfast&#8221; with air mattresses for guests.</p>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p>What started as a makeshift lodging idea rapidly transformed into a global phenomenon. Airbnb has revolutionized the travel industry, evolving from a simple website to a sprawling online marketplace with an astonishing array of listings &#8211; from quaint apartments to grandiose castles.</p>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p>Airbnb&#8217;s journey hasn&#8217;t just stopped at providing unique accommodations. It has continually evolved, introducing Airbnb Experiences, allowing locals to share unique tours and activities, and delving into high-end travel with Airbnb Luxe. This evolution is a testament to Airbnb&#8217;s adaptability and its unwavering commitment to enhancing travel experiences globally.</p>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-airbnb-s-business-model">Airbnb&#8217;s business model</h3>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p>Airbnb&#8217;s business model operates on a platform-based approach, acting as an intermediary that connects hosts with guests. The company capitalizes on the sharing economy by allowing property owners to list their spaces – a room, an apartment, or a unique dwelling – for short-term rentals. </p>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p>In turn, travelers seeking accommodations with a more personal touch can browse these listings. </p>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p>Airbnb earns revenue by charging service fees to hosts and guests for each booking made through the platform. These fees generally range from 15% to 20%.</p>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p>This model has enabled Airbnb to scale rapidly without the overheads associated with traditional hospitality businesses. As for its target market, Airbnb appeals to a broad spectrum of users, ranging from budget-conscious solo travelers to luxury-seeking vacationers. </p>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p>The platform is particularly popular among millennials and younger travelers who value unique, local experiences over conventional hotel stays. However, its diverse range of accommodations also attracts families, business travelers, and adventure seekers, making it a versatile choice for various travel needs and preferences.</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-booking-com">Booking.com</h3>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p>Booking.com&#8217;s journey, starting in 1996 in Amsterdam, is a remarkable tale of digital innovation and strategic growth. Geert-Jan Bruinsma founded it as a visionary Dutch startup, harnessing the internet&#8217;s potential to connect hotels with guests seamlessly.</p>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p>Under the acquisition by Priceline Group (now Booking Holdings Inc.) in 2005, <a href="http://booking.com/">Booking.com</a> leaped onto the international scene. It has since diversified its offerings well beyond hotel bookings, embracing a variety of accommodations, including apartments, vacation homes, and even unconventional options like treehouses and igloos.</p>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p>Today, Booking.com is a colossal player in the travel marketplace, serving users in over 40 languages with a staggering 28 million listings. </p>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p>This includes 6.2 million homes, apartments, and other unique properties. The platform has grown into an all-encompassing travel hub, providing accommodation reservations, car rentals, flight bookings, and airport taxi services. This evolution positions Booking.com as a comprehensive travel solution for millions globally, a testament to its enduring vision and adaptability in the ever-evolving travel industry.</p>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-business-model-and-primary-audience">Business model and primary audience.</h3>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p>Booking.com operates on a classic online travel agency (OTA) business model, primarily generating revenue through commissions. </p>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p>When a customer books a hotel room or any other accommodation type through their platform, the property pays Booking.com a percentage-based commission on the booking value. This model has proven effective due to its vast inventory, which includes various accommodation types, from hotels and hostels to apartments and unique stays. </p>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p>Booking.com appeals to both leisure and business travelers due to its extensive global reach and the variety of accommodations it offers. It caters to budget-conscious travelers looking for affordable options and luxury seekers searching for high-end accommodations. </p>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p>The platform&#8217;s ability to provide additional travel services like car rentals and flight bookings further broadens its appeal, making it a one-stop shop for many travelers&#8217; needs.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-types-of-accommodation-offered"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Types_of_Accommodation_Offered"></span>Types of Accommodation Offered<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h2>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-airbnb-0">Airbnb</h3>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p>Airbnb offers various accommodation types to cater to various travelers&#8217; needs and preferences. The platform&#8217;s listings include:</p>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p><strong>Private Rooms: </strong>Guests rent a room in a host&#8217;s home, providing a comfortable, home-like experience while often sharing spaces like the kitchen or living room with the host or other guests.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-pricing-structures"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Pricing_structures"></span>Pricing structures<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h2>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-airbnb-1">Airbnb</h3>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p>Airbnb&#8217;s pricing structure can be divided into several components:</p>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<ol></p>
<li><strong>Base Price</strong>: This is the amount the host sets for staying at their property. It can vary depending on the time of the year, day of the week, and length of stay.</li>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<li><strong>Cleaning Fee</strong>: This is an additional fee charged by the host to cover the cost of cleaning their space after a guest&#8217;s stay. It&#8217;s a one-time fee per booking and not per night. The host sets this fee and can vary greatly depending on the property and the host&#8217;s preferences.</li>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<li><strong>Service Fee</strong>: Airbnb charges a service fee to both guests and hosts. For guests, this fee is a percentage of the total booking cost (including the base price and cleaning fee, but excluding Airbnb fees and taxes) and is typically under 14.2%. This fee helps Airbnb run its platform and offer services like 24/7 customer support.</li>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<li><strong>Note</strong> that Airbnb&#8217;s service fee fo Airbnb Luxe properties is 20%</li>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<li><strong>Extra Guest Fees</strong>: Some hosts charge additional fees for extra guests beyond a certain number. This accommodates additional costs associated with more people staying, like extra bedding, wear and tear, and utilities.</li>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<li><strong>Security Deposit</strong>: Some listings require a security deposit set by the host. This is not charged upfront but is a hold or authorization on the guest&#8217;s payment method to cover potential damage to the property.</li>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<li><strong>Local Taxes</strong>: Depending on the property&#8217;s location, additional local taxes may apply. Airbnb collects and remits these taxes in many jurisdictions.</li>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<li><strong>Other Fees</strong>: Occasionally, there may be other fees, such as pet fees or using certain amenities.</li>
<p></ol>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p>It&#8217;s important to note that Airbnb&#8217;s pricing and fee structures are subject to change and can vary by location and specific booking details. Guests are advised to review the total cost breakdown provided by Airbnb before confirming a booking to understand all applicable fees.</p>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-booking-com-1">Booking.com</h3>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p>Booking.com&#8217;s pricing structure includes commission rates, cleaning fees, and payment methods.</p>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<ol></p>
<li><strong>Commission Rates</strong>: Booking.com charges a commission rate to hosts for each booking made through their platform. This rate varies based on location, property type, and cancellation policy. The average commission rate is around 15% but ranges from 10% to 25%. These commissions are passed on to travelers as hosts increase their base rates when adding their listings to BDC.</li>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<li><strong>Cleaning Fees and Security Deposit</strong>: Hosts on Booking.com can add cleaning fees and security deposits to their listings. .</li>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<li><strong>Payment Methods</strong>: Booking.com offers various payment methods for hosts to receive guest payments. These include online methods like credit/debit cards (Visa, Mastercard, American Express, etc.), PayPal, AliPay, and offline methods like bank transfers and cash payments.</li>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<li><strong>No Hidden Fees</strong>: It&#8217;s important to note that Booking.com does not have hidden costs. The platform is transparent about the fees charged, including the commission rate.</li>
<p></ol>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">What happens if the host cancels an Airbnb booking?</h3>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p>There may be occasions when a host has to cancel your booking. These could include things like double bookings, the property has been damaged, earthquakes, hurricanes, a pandemic, etc.</p>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p>Here’s what Airbnb will do if a host cancels a booking…</p>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<blockquote class="wp-block-quote is-layout-flow wp-block-quote-is-layout-flow"><p></p>
<p>While it’s rare, sometimes a Host may need to cancel a reservation. We understand this can impact your plans in a big way. Rest assured that every booking made on Airbnb comes with&nbsp;<a href="https://www.airbnb.com/aircover">AirCover</a>, protection for guests against significant issues, including Host cancellations within 30 days of check-in.&nbsp;<br />In the unlikely event a Host needs to cancel your booking within 30 days of check-in, you’ll automatically get a refund and we can help you find a similar or better home to rebook.</p>
<p></p></blockquote>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p><a href="https://www.airbnb.com/help/article/170#:~:text=In%20the%20unlikely%20event%20a,or%20better%20home%20to%20rebook." target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">See here for more details</a></p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading has-text-align-center" id="h-airbnb-alternatives-airbnb-v-booking-com">Airbnb Alternatives &#8211; Airbnb v Booking.com</h3>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-customer-support-and-safety"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Customer_Support_and_Safety"></span>Customer Support and Safety<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h2>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-airbnb-2">Airbnb</h3>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p>Airbnb offers various methods for hosts and guests to contact customer support. The primary way is through their website, specifically the <strong><a href="https://www.airbnb.com/help" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">Help Center</a></strong>, where users can describe their issue to be directed to the appropriate department. They also offer live chat for real-time assistance. For more direct communication, Airbnb provides various phone numbers for different regions, allowing for quicker resolution of issues. </p>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p>Email is another option for those who prefer written communication without service fees, although it might take longer to receive a response. Social media channels like Twitter, Instagram, and Facebook are also actively used by Airbnb for customer interactions. </p>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-booking-com-2">Booking.com</h3>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p>Booking.com offers customer service support 24/7 through various channels. Customers can contact agents via message or phone for any urgent matters. The platform also encourages direct communication with each property for details about stays. </p>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p>Additionally, Booking.com provides a <strong><a href="https://secure.booking.com/help" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">Help Center</a></strong> where users can find answers to common questions, such as cancellation policies, payment procedures, and making changes to bookings. This comprehensive support system ensures that you can get assistance with your bookings and other queries at any time.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-conclusion"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Conclusion"></span>Conclusion<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h2>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p>In conclusion, when comparing Airbnb and Booking.com for travelers seeking the best accommodation options, it&#8217;s clear that both platforms have their unique strengths. </p>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p>My analysis of over 10,000 price comparisons (where properties are on both platforms) revealed that substantial savings can be made in most cases, with an average saving of 13%. </p>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p>This indicates there&#8217;s no definitive cheaper option between the two; it largely depends on the specific property and dates. </p>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p>The key takeaway for travelers is the importance of comparing prices on both platforms to potentially save an average of $130 on a typical booking of around $1,000. </p>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p>Both Airbnb and Booking.com offer diverse accommodation options and user-friendly experiences, but their pricing can vary significantly, underscoring the value of shopping around.</p>
