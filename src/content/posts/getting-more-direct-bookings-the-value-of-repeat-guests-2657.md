<div class="wp-block-getwid-advanced-heading has-custom-font-size" style="font-size:26px"><span class="wp-block-getwid-advanced-heading__content"><strong>Getting More Direct Bookings &#8211; The Value Of Repeat Guests</strong></span></div>



<p>In the vacation rental world, there is a lot written about direct bookings.</p>



<p>Hosts employ all sorts of tactics to try and boost their direct booking numbers. They create their own websites, spend time on social media, write traffic-driving articles about their destination, spend time on search engine optimization and Google My Business, and lots more besides.</p>



<p>But many don’t pay enough attention to the value of repeat guests.</p>



<p>In this article, I will outline some of the reasons to increase your efforts to encourage repeat booking and some tactics that you can employ that will help boost the number of travelers returning to your property, year after year.</p>

<p>Before we start</p>



<div class="wp-block-getwid-advanced-heading has-custom-font-size" style="font-size:22px"><span class="wp-block-getwid-advanced-heading__content"><strong>First things first&#8230;<br></strong></span></div>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Getting More Direct Bookings</strong></h2>



<div class="wp-block-getwid-advanced-heading has-custom-font-size" style="font-size:22px"><span class="wp-block-getwid-advanced-heading__content"><strong>Let’s kick off with some basics…</strong></span></div>



<blockquote class="wp-block-quote has-medium-font-size is-layout-flow wp-block-quote-is-layout-flow">
<p><strong>Repeat bookings are the best bookings of all.</strong></p>
</blockquote>



<ul class="has-theme-palette-9-background-color has-background">
<li>Repeat bookings are direct bookings.&nbsp;</li>



<li>They book with your house rules and your cancellation policy.</li>



<li>There is little to no marketing cost or effort associated with repeat bookings.</li>



<li>The guest/s already knows you and your property, and the destination.</li>



<li>You won’t be faced with the normal deluge of pre-booking and pre-arrival questions.</li>



<li>You are more likely to get good reviews.</li>
</ul>



<blockquote class="wp-block-quote has-medium-font-size is-layout-flow wp-block-quote-is-layout-flow">
<p><strong>You don’t start the year with an empty calendar</strong></p>
</blockquote>



<p>This is a big one.</p>



<ul>
<li>Without any repeat bookings, you start each year with an empty calendar along with the stress that that brings.</li>



<li>When you nurture repeat bookings you have fewer weeks to fill, right from the get-go.</li>
</ul>



<blockquote class="wp-block-quote has-medium-font-size is-layout-flow wp-block-quote-is-layout-flow">
<p><strong>You know these guests.</strong></p>
</blockquote>



<ul>
<li>New guests bring a certain amount of doubt with them.&nbsp;</li>



<li>Will they complain about the property?&nbsp;</li>



<li>Will they cause damage?</li>



<li>Will they leave the place clean and tidy?</li>
</ul>



<ul>
<li>These doubts are alleviated with repeat guests.</li>



<li>There’s a higher level of trust in the host &#8211; guest relationship.</li>
</ul>

<p>I have a friend, Steve, that has 3 lakeside properties, Pike Point, Osprey Point, and Beach Point, in the Adirondacks, New York State.</p>



<p>This year (2022) Steve has hosted 76 groups of guests across his 3 cabins. It’s a relatively short season but he’s pretty much fully booked, year on year.</p>



<p>41 of those groups were repeat guests. </p>



<blockquote class="wp-block-quote has-theme-palette-7-background-color has-background has-medium-font-size is-layout-flow wp-block-quote-is-layout-flow">
<p><strong>Over half of Steve&#8217;s bookings (41/76) were repeat guests that knew exactly what they were getting.</strong></p>
</blockquote>



<p><br>But there’s another factor at play here and that’s FOMO or fear of missing out.<br>When looking at high season, the repeat guest percentage was a whopping 90%+<br>They rebook at the end of their vacation because they are worried that someone else will take that week or weeks.<br><br>17 of those groups have been coming for 5 years or more.<br>Osprey Point has only been operating for 3 years but 80% of all guests that stay there have been every year.</p>



<p><strong>Steve (as of September 2022) already has 28 weeks booked for 2023 and 26 of those bookings are from his loyal fanbase of serial repeat bookers.</strong></p>



<p>It’s pretty much, vacation rentals on autopilot.</p>

<div class="wp-block-getwid-advanced-heading has-custom-font-size" style="font-size:22px"><span class="wp-block-getwid-advanced-heading__content"><strong>Up your game and reap the rewards.</strong></span></div>



<p><strong>There are a number of things that you can do that will make your guests feel more at home.<br></strong>And therein lies the secret.<br><strong>Make your guests feel at home. <br></strong><br>Hotels, and hotel rooms, in particular, are soulless cookie-cutter spaces that are in no way homely.<br>I&#8217;ve stayed in a lot of Airbnbs and in lots of traditional vacation rentals. Some have been set up like hotels and some have been presented like homes.<br>Guests will always be more inclined to rebook the latter.<br></p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><br><strong>Ideas that can boost repeat bookings</strong></h2>



<p class="has-medium-font-size"><strong>First impressions</strong></p>



<p><br>If you don&#8217;t do an in-person meet and greet, call or text to make sure that they arrived and settled in ok.<br>Antonio Bortolotti, the founder of the Vacation Rental World Summit, does a Zoom video call with his guests when they arrive. Even though he&#8217;s hundreds of miles away, he spends 5 or 10 minutes chatting and asking if they arrived ok and that all is well with the property. <br><br>This helps the guests feel cared for, it&#8217;s free to do, and only takes a few minutes.<br></p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><br><br><strong>Welcome packs</strong></h3>



<p><strong><br></strong><br>I’m a big fan of providing a welcome pack for guests on arrival.<br><br>I like to provide fresh bread, tea and coffee, milk, and some locally grown produce, all in a nice basket. <br>I alter the contents depending on the guest&#8217;s time of arrival. If they are arriving late, I&#8217;ll swap the tea and coffee option for some wine and beer.<br>My thinking here is if friends were coming over to my own home, I would offer them a cup of tea or coffee (if they come during the day) or a glass of wine or a cold beer (if they come later in the day, or if it&#8217;s super hot).<br><strong>I want to treat my guests like I treat my friends.<br></strong><br>These little touches also buy me some credit if something goes wrong. <br><br>If they have young children consider providing coloring books and some pencils. Older children appreciate a PlayStation or Xbox and some games.<br><strong>Happy kids equal happy adults.<br></strong><br><br>Fresh flowers in the property are another option that takes your offering to another level. They make the place look nicer and they show that you care.<br><br>Write individual, handwritten, welcome notes to each group of guests. Again, this adds a personal touch, is super easy to do and costs next to nothing.</p>



<p>As an alternative, you can buy welcome signage Canva templates (like the example below) specifically for short-term rentals on <a href="https://www.etsy.com/search?q=vacation%20rentals" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">Etsy</a> for around $5 and edit them to suit your own needs.</p>
