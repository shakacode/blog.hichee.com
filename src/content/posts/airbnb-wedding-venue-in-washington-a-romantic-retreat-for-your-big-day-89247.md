<p></p>
<p>Kingston House, a mid-century architectural gem surrounded by 27 acres of verdant hills and a working lavender farm, offers the perfect setting for an idyllic Northwest wedding. Just a stone&#8217;s throw away from the Kingston Ferry, this rustic retreat effortlessly marries comfort and style. It features a 4000 sqft living space, a sunken conversational area with a rock fireplace, a fully-equipped kitchen, and a Steinway grand piano perfect for wedding pictures. </p>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p>The grounds boast walking trails, an outdoor hot tub, two fire pits, a canoe-friendly pond, and garden gazebos.</p>
<p></p>

<p></p>
<p></p>
<blockquote class="wp-block-quote is-layout-flow wp-block-quote-is-layout-flow"><p></p>
<p>Kingston House is an Anker I. Molver-designed, mid-century “masterpiece” on 27 private acres of rolling hills, towering cedars, and a working lavender farm.</p>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p>Located on the Kitsap Peninsula between Bainbridge Island and Olympic National Park, we are a quintessentially Pacific Northwest setting for your getaway or celebration.</p>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p>Kingston House feels remote but is just 2 miles from the Kingston ferry (to Seattle &amp; Edmonds) and 11 miles from the Bainbridge ferry (to Seattle).</p>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p>A beautiful natural setting for a self-care retreat, private getaway, or a large space where family can gather safely.</p>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p>Fans of Pacific Northwest mid-century design love the residence, which was designed by an architect with roots in Norway and Japan from Douglas-fir timbers milled on-site.</p>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p>Guests describe the house as an “architectural delight” with “beautiful touches throughout” and the grounds as “gorgeous and spacious” (the word “magical” has been used more than once).</p>
<p></p>
<p></p>
<p>Kingston House has been updated with modern comforts while respecting its architectural integrity.</p>
