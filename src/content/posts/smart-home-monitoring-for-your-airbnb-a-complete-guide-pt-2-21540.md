<p>More and more short-term rental hosts and managers are using external cameras to monitor the security of their properties. This is especially true for those that host remotely. There are added benefits, such as detecting if guests are sneaking in more people than they have paid for or if they have arrived with a four-legged friend when you clearly state that you don&#8217;t accept pets.<br><br>The footage from these cameras can help prove claims with the resolution centers of Airbnb, Booking (.com), or VRBO.</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading has-medium-font-size" id="h-things-to-consider">Things to consider</h2>



<p>Look for cameras with motion detectors, as you don&#8217;t want cameras recording 24/7. You will need good cloud storage and simple access to recordings via your phone, and the cameras you choose should have excellent night vision.<br><br>Whether you go for wired or battery cameras is a personal choice. Wired cameras are fit and forget, but the initial installation takes longer and will require an electrician, so they come at a higher installation cost. Battery versions are easier to install, and any half-decent DIYer can manage the task, but you will need to swap out batteries occasionally.<br><br><strong>Again, as with video doorbells, you will need to disclose in your listing that you have cameras on site. <a href="#caneradisclosure">See details here</a></strong></p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading has-text-color" id="h-important-note" style="color:#f40404">Important note</h2>



<p>Listing sites require that you indicate the presence of all security cameras or other recording devices in or around a property, even if they&#8217;re not turned on or hooked up.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-airbnb-full-details-and-rules-here"><br><br><strong>Airbnb</strong> (<a href="https://www.airbnb.com/help/article/2914#:~:text=Airbnb%20prohibits%20security%20cameras%20or,turned%20on%20or%20hooked%20up" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">Full details and rules here</a>)</h3>



<ol>
<li>Go to&nbsp;<a href="https://www.airbnb.com/hosting/listings" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">Listings</a>&nbsp;and click or tap on the listing you want to edit</li>



<li>In&nbsp;<strong>Listing details</strong>, scroll down to&nbsp;<strong>Things guests should know</strong>&nbsp;and click or tap&nbsp;<strong>Edit</strong></li>



<li>Under&nbsp;<strong>Safety Devices</strong>, check the box for&nbsp;<strong>Security cameras/audio recording devices</strong></li>



<li>Click or tap&nbsp;<strong>Edit</strong>&nbsp;and describe each device, its location, and if it will be off or on (you’ll also be asked to confirm some details about your listing)</li>
</ol>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-booking-com-full-details-and-rules-here"><strong>Booking (.com)</strong> (<a href="https://partner.booking.com/en-us/help/legal-security/security/requirements-and-regulations-surveillance-devices" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">Full details and rules here</a>)</h3>



<p>To indicate the presence of security devices on the Extranet:&nbsp;</p>



<ol>
<li>Click the Property tab.</li>



<li>Choose&nbsp;<a href="https://admin.booking.com/?aid=1190473&amp;page=%2Fhotel%2Fhoteladmin%2Fextranet_ng%2Fmanage%2Ffacilities.html&amp;utm_source=partnerhub_help&amp;utm_medium=facilities&amp;utm_campaign=13844" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">Facilities &amp; Services</a>.</li>



<li>Scroll down to the Safety &amp; Security section and indicate the presence of any CCTV devices.</li>
</ol>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-vrbo-full-details-and-rules-here"><strong>VRBO</strong> (<a href="https://help.vrbo.com/articles/What-is-HomeAway-s-policy-on-surveillance-devices-at-a-property" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">Full details and rules here</a>)</h3>



<p>Surveillance devices, including security cameras and smart doorbells (which may record audio), are permitted if they follow these rules:</p>



<ul>
<li>Surveillance devices should only be used for security purposes.</li>



<li>The location and coverage of devices must be disclosed on the property description page (see&nbsp;<a href="https://help.vrbo.com/articles/How-do-I-add-or-edit-a-changeover-date" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">Edit your property listing</a>&nbsp;for step-by-step instructions). It&#8217;s not enough to include a photo of the device.</li>



<li>Outdoor cameras covering pools and hot tubs need to be disclosed on the property details page and in a reasonably discoverable location, such as a “guest instructions” binder, a notice on the wall of a high-traffic common space, or a placard next to the entry point itself. The disclosure must specify that the pool/hot tub is within the coverage area of the device.</li>



<li>Areas where guests have a reasonable expectation of privacy, such as the bathroom or bedroom, should not be under surveillance.</li>



<li>Reasonable measures should be taken to limit access to surveillance data.</li>

<p>With this in mind, you may want to look for control features over learning features.</p>
