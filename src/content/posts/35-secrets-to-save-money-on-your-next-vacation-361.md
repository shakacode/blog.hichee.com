<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Saving Money On Your Next Vacation</strong></h2>
</p>
<p>We all want to save money when we travel, but sometimes it can be hard to know where to start. Here are 35 tips to help you save money on your next vacation:</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">1. <strong>Plan ahead</strong></h2>
</p>
<p>This may seem like an obvious one, but planning your trip in advance can help you save a lot of money. Not only will you be able to find better deals on flights and vacation rentals, but you&#8217;ll also have a better idea of what activities you want to experience while you’re away and how much they&#8217;ll cost.</p>
</p>
<p>A well-planned vacation will reduce impulse spending and planning what to see and do in advance is a fun thing to do. <a href="http://Tripadvisor.com">Tripadvisor.com</a> is a great place to discover things to see and do and places to visit.</p>
</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">2. <strong>Avoid the most expensive destinations</strong></h2>
</p>
<p>If you&#8217;re looking to save money on your vacation, it&#8217;s best to avoid the most popular (and expensive) destinations. Instead, try exploring some of the less-visited places around the world where your Dollars, Euro, Pounds, or other currencies will go further.</p>
</p>
<p>Bermuda, Switzerland, Norway, Iceland, and Barbados are the 5 most expensive destinations to visit so don’t expect to have a cheap vacation at any of these places.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">3. <strong>Create a budget before your trip (and stick to it)</strong></h2>
</p>
<p>One of the best ways to save money on vacation is to create a budget before you even start packing your bags. Decide how much money you can realistically afford to spend and then make a list of all the expenses you&#8217;ll need to account for, including travel, lodging, food, activities, and souvenirs. Once you have your budget, do your best to stick to it!</p>
</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">4. <strong>Research the price of travel and seasonal trends</strong></h2>
</p>
<p><strong>Avoid the holiday seasons</strong></p>
</p>
<p>When it comes to saving money on vacation, knowledge is power. Before booking your trip, take some time to research the average cost of travel to your destination and look for any seasonal trends that could affect pricing.</p>
</p>
<p>Dates that coincide with school holidays, Easter, Thanksgiving, and Christmas are always classed as high season, and booking these dates will be more expensive than booking in mid or low season. Booking one week on either side of these high-season weeks can result in big savings.</p>
</p>
<p><strong>Seasonal weather impacts vacation pricing</strong></p>
</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">6. <strong>Be flexible with your travel dates.</strong></h2>
</p>
<p>If you&#8217;re willing to be flexible with when you travel, you can often find cheaper flights and accommodation. For example, traveling during the week can often be cheaper than traveling on weekends.</p>
</p>
<p>According to Experian…</p>
</p>
<blockquote class="wp-block-quote is-layout-flow wp-block-quote-is-layout-flow">
<p>“<em>Generally, Tuesday, Wednesday and Saturday are the cheapest days to fly, while Fridays and Sundays are pricier, according to travel deal site FareCompare. Leisure travelers tend to fly on the weekends, either starting their vacations on Friday or taking a long weekend Friday to Sunday. Business travelers often return on Fridays and head out on business trips on Sunday evenings. Mid-week flights are cheaper because there&#8217;s generally less demand from both business and leisure travelers</em>”.</p>
</blockquote>

</p>
<p>If you&#8217;re looking to save money on accommodation, one way to do so is by booking for a longer period of time. Many vacation rental hosts offer discounts for longer stays, so you can end up saving a lot of money in the long run. If you book direct with a host or owner you may even get a ‘Book six days, get an extra day free’ deal.</p>
</p>
<p>It pays to ask.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">9. <strong>Use a credit card to get a good foreign exchange rate.</strong></h2>
</p>
<p>If you have a good credit card, you can often get a better exchange rate than you would at a bank. Just be sure to pay off your balance in full to avoid any interest charges.</p>
</p>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Look for travel rewards credit cards.</strong></h3>
</p>
<p>There are a number of credit cards that offer rewards specifically for travel, including points, miles, and cash back. If you&#8217;re a frequent traveler, these cards can help you save a lot of money on your trips. Just be sure to pay off your balance in full each month to avoid interest charges.</p>
</p>
<p>Use points and miles to book free or discounted travel.</p>
</p>
<p>If you have any points or miles from past travel, be sure to use them! You can often use them to book free or discounted travel, which can save you a lot of money.</p>
</p>
<p>Save up your points and miles.</p>
</p>
<p>If you don&#8217;t have any points or miles yet, start saving them up! Many credit cards offer sign-up bonuses that can get you a free flight or hotel stay.</p>
</p>
<hr class="wp-block-separator aligncenter has-alpha-channel-opacity is-style-wide"/>
</p>
<figure class="wp-block-image aligncenter size-full"><img decoding="async" width="64" height="64" src="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/07/44823_atm_icon-1.png" alt="Don’t pay foreign transaction or ATM withdrawal fees" class="wp-image-379"/></figure>
</p>
<h2 class="wp-block-heading">10. <strong>Don’t pay foreign transaction or ATM withdrawal fees</strong></h2>
</p>
<p>Fees percentages may not sound like much, but they add up quickly when you’re withdrawing cash from an ATM abroad. To avoid these fees, look for a bank account that doesn’t charge them or use a credit card that doesn’t charge foreign transaction fees.</p>
</p>
<hr class="wp-block-separator aligncenter has-alpha-channel-opacity is-style-wide"/>
</p>
<figure class="wp-block-image aligncenter size-full"><img decoding="async" width="64" height="64" src="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/07/285662_map_icon-1.png" alt="Travel in places where your home currency takes you farther" class="wp-image-380"/></figure>
</p>
<h2 class="wp-block-heading">11. <strong>Travel in places where your home currency takes you farther.</strong></h2>
</p>
<p>You can travel to countries with strong currencies, like the United States or Japan, and get more bang for your buck. Another way to save money is by visiting cheaper destinations (see tip no.2). Countries like Thailand and Mexico offer cheaper accommodations and food options without skimping on quality or experiences.</p>
</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">12. <strong>Track your spending with an app</strong></h2>
</p>
<p><strong>NAB</strong> (You Need A Budget) is one of our favorite budgeting apps, but there are tons of others out there that can help you track your spending. Having a clear idea of where your money is going is the first step to saving money.</p>
</p>
<p><strong>Trail Wallet</strong> is another simple and elegant app for tracking your travel spending.</p>
</p>
<p>Trail Wallet can help you stay on budget while you&#8217;re on the go. The app lets you create budgets for individual trips and add expenses as you go. You can also see where you&#8217;re spending the most money and adjust your budget accordingly.</p>
</p>
<p><strong>XE Currency</strong></p>
</p>
<p>This free app is essential for anyone who travels frequently</p>
</p>
<p>XE Currency is a simple and easy-to-use currency converter that gives you live exchange rates for over 160 currencies. The app also lets you set up rate alerts, so you&#8217;ll always be aware of changes</p>
</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">13. <strong>Avoid losing all your money with an emergency stash</strong></h2>
</p>
<p>Losing your wallet on vacation is a nightmare, but it happens. Before you leave, stash some cash in a safe place in your luggage so you&#8217;re not completely stranded if it does happen. Don’t wander around town with a lot of cash and all of your credit cards on you. Just take a limited amount of cash and just one credit card.</p>
</p>
<p>That way, if the worst does happen you’ll not have lost everything.</p>
</p>

</p>
<h2 class="wp-block-heading">14. <strong>Never exchange money at the airport</strong></h2>
</p>
<p>The rates offered at airports are almost always terrible, so avoid exchanging money there if at all possible. If you must, XC (Local Exchange Company) is one of the better options, but your best bet is to withdraw cash from an ATM at your destination.</p>

</p>
<h2 class="wp-block-heading">15. <strong>Shop around for trip insurance online.</strong></h2>
</p>
<p>Gather quotes from a few different companies before you buy travel insurance. Prices can vary widely, so it&#8217;s worth it to shop around.</p>
</p>
<p>Be sure to read the fine print on any travel insurance policy to make sure it&#8217;s right for you.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">21. Look at skiplagging options</h2>
</p>
<p>This tip is a little bit risky but may save money for some travelers.</p>
</p>
<p>Skiplagging, also known as hidden-city ticketing, is a travel booking technique that takes advantage of how airlines price flights. By booking a flight with a layover at your desired destination, and then not taking the last leg of the trip, you can save money on airfare.</p>
</p>
<p>While this may sound like an obvious way to get cheaper flights, it&#8217;s actually against most airlines&#8217; rules and can result in passengers being banned from future flights.</p>
</p>
<p>It&#8217;s important to know the risks before you book a hidden-city ticket.</p>
</p>
<p>Here are a few things to keep in mind:</p>
</p>
<p>Most airlines consider skiplagging to be a form of fraud, and they may cancel your ticket if they find out you&#8217;re doing it.</p>
</p>
<p>You could be banned from flying with that airline in the future.</p>
</p>
<p>Your checked luggage will be sent to the final destination so you’ll end up without it. Hand luggage and cabin baggage shouldn’t be a problem.</p>

</p>
<h2 class="wp-block-heading">31. <strong>Stay off the beaten path</strong></h2>
</p>
<p>Do some research and try to&nbsp;avoid tourist traps, which are often overcrowded and overpriced. Instead, explore some of the&nbsp;lesser-known attractions&nbsp;or do some activities that are unique to the area you&#8217;re visiting. This can not only save you money but also give you a more authentic experience.</p>
</p>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity is-style-wide"/>
</p>

</p>
<h2 class="wp-block-heading">32. <strong>Eat like a local</strong></h2>
</p>
<p>Dining out can be one of the biggest expenses while on vacation, especially if you&#8217;re eating at tourist traps. To save money, try to&nbsp;eat like a local. This means seeking out&nbsp;local restaurants&nbsp;and markets where you can get&nbsp;cheaper and more authentic meals.</p>
</p>
<p>You can also save money by cooking some meals in your vacation rental or Airbnb. After all, that&#8217;s one of the main advantages of booking a vacation rental.</p>
</p>
<p>Take advantage of food delivery apps too. Having McDonald&#8217;s, KFC, Domino&#8217;s Pizza, Planet Sushi, Pizza Hut, Speed Burger, O&#8217;Tacos, Subway, and more delivered to your rental will be cheaper than eating out in a lot of cases.</p>
</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">33. <strong>Have sundowners on the deck, not on the beach</strong></h2>
</p>
<p>It’s tempting to visit well know bars like Harry’s bar in Venice, Napoleon House, New Orleans, and Tiki Ti, Los Angeles but these famous bars are super expensive.</p>
</p>
<p>The same goes for a lot of beach bars as you are paying for the view and location as much as you are for the drinks. Pick up your favorite drinks at the local store and have these on the deck or around the pool at your vacation rental. You’ll save a small fortune over the course of your vacation.</p>
</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">34. <strong>Buy local brands and locally-grown produce as snacks.</strong></h2>
</p>
<p>A fresh coconut can be a cheaper alternative to a can of Coke in many destinations. The same goes for local street food and fresh fruit over branded snacks.</p>
</p>
<p>If you are preparing meals in your vacation rental then check out the local farmers market. These always have the freshest produce and are always buzzing with local colourful characters.</p>
