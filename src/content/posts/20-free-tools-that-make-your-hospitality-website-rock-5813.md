<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Free Images</h2>



<figure class="wp-block-image"><img decoding="async" src="https://rentmoreweeks.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/09/polaroid-2315182_640.png" alt=""/></figure>



<p>There are often times when you need a great photo for your website or social media post but you don’t have a suitable image to hand. That’s where royalty-free image providers come into their own.<br>There are a lot of sites offering free images but I use the following three most of the time.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Unsplash</h3>



<p><a href="https://unsplash.com/">https://unsplash.com/</a></p>



<p>Usplash is one of my favourite online tools. It has a huge selection of images to choose from.<br>Once you add your keyword it will display a selection of images that match your search.<br>You can narrow the search by orientation and colour.</p>



<p>I use this site on a daily basis.</p>



<p>You can download each image in a number of widths. Just sign up for a free account, verify your email and you’ll be good to go.<br>The video below walks you through the basics of using the site</p>



<p><a href="https://youtu.be/L0sgHdTmzfs"></a></p>



<figure class="wp-block-image"><a href="https://youtu.be/L0sgHdTmzfs"><img decoding="async" src="https://rentmoreweeks.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/09/Screen-Shot-2021-09-23-at-12.04.09-e1633635037961.png" alt=""/></a></figure>



<p><a href="https://youtu.be/L0sgHdTmzfs"></a></p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Pixabay</h3>



<p><a href="https://pixabay.com/">https://pixabay.com/</a></p>



<p>Pixabay was the first of the large free royalty/copyright-free sites.<br>It works in a very similar way to Unsplash. There have been many times where I haven’t been able to find what I was looking for on Unsplash but Pixabay came up trumps.</p>



<p>It has over 2.4 million media options to choose from<br>It offers free illustrations as well as photos so can be useful if that’s what you are looking for.<br>It also has 1,000’s of free videos that may be of use to you</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Pxhere</h3>



<p><a href="https://pxhere.com/">https://pxhere.com/</a></p>



<p>Pxhere is very similar to Unsplash and Pixabay but I use it as an extra option if I can’t find exactly what I’m looking for on the other two.</p>

<p>There may be times when you’ll need a free video to insert into your site and Pexels seems to offer more free videos than any other site that I’ve come across.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Mockups</h2>



<figure class="wp-block-image"><img decoding="async" src="https://rentmoreweeks.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/09/Bookano-Page-templates-1024x605.jpeg" alt=""/></figure>



<p>There are times when you may want to add a photo onto the screen of a computer, a tablet, or a smartphone.<br>I often do this as it can make an image pop and it projects a sense of modern day realism.</p>



<p>There are a lot of mockup generator sites out there but quite a lot add a watermark to the free version and you can only remove the watermark if you subscribe (pay).</p>



<p>I’ve listed a couple of free versions below</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Mockup.photos</h3>



<p><a href="https://mockup.photos">https://mockup.photos</a></p>



<p>This site offers 1,500+ mockups that are entirely free.<br>Just search by device, laptop, iPad, smartphone, etc. Then find a mockup that is suitable for your purposes. Just drag an image from your computer into the ‘place image here’ section and it will mockup that image into the device. Then download it as a jpg, png, or pdf.<br>It’s super easy.</p>



<figure class="wp-block-image"><img decoding="async" src="https://rentmoreweeks.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/08/Screen-Shot-2021-08-30-at-15.12.54-1024x640.png" alt=""/></figure>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Mockuper</h3>



<p><a href="https://mockuper.net/">https://mockuper.net/</a></p>



<p>Mockuper works in a similar way to mockup.photos and is also free.<br>I often use this software if I can’t find what I’m looking for elsewhere.</p>



<figure class="wp-block-image"><img decoding="async" src="https://rentmoreweeks.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/09/Screen-Shot-2021-09-16-at-17.37.29-1024x640.png" alt=""/></figure>

<figure class="wp-block-image"><img decoding="async" src="https://rentmoreweeks.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/09/paint-24955_640.png" alt=""/></figure>



<p>We all need to manipulate images from time to time, whether it’s resizing, cropping, flipping, or other changes.<br><br>Below is a list of online and offline platforms that you’ll find super useful for all of your image editing needs.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Ipiccy</h3>



<p><a href="https://ipiccy.com/">https://ipiccy.com/</a></p>



<p>My go-to photo editor for working on individual images.</p>



<p>This free-to-use online editor offers pretty much all of the things you’ll need, including resize and crop (the two functions that I use the most).<br>Have a look at the video tutorial below. Ipiccy is very easy to use and offers lots of image editing options.</p>



<p><a href="https://youtu.be/hbCu_7Bj6rQ"></a></p>



<figure class="wp-block-image"><a href="https://youtu.be/hbCu_7Bj6rQ"><img decoding="async" src="https://rentmoreweeks.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/09/Screen-Shot-2021-09-23-at-13.12.41.png" alt=""/></a></figure>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Resizepixel</h3>



<p><a href="https://www.resizepixel.com/">https://www.resizepixel.com/</a></p>



<p>An online resizer that resizes one image at a time.<br>It’s quick, easy to use, and I find it very useful for resizing very large images that most other resizers can’t upload.</p>



<figure class="wp-block-image"><a href="https://www.resizepixel.com/"><img decoding="async" src="https://rentmoreweeks.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/09/Screen-Shot-2021-09-23-at-11.02.12-1024x326.png" alt=""/></a></figure>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Bulk Resize Photos</h3>



<p><a href="https://bulkresizephotos.com/en">https://bulkresizephotos.com/en</a></p>



<p>This bulk-online resizer does exactly what it says on the tin.</p>



<p>It’s ideal for resizing a bunch of gallery images before importing them into WordPress.<br>Just drag your images into the box, select the ‘longest side’ option, enter the size that you want, and convert.<br>It’s incredibly fast, which is always a bonus</p>

<p><a href="https://www.gimp.org/downloads/">https://www.gimp.org/downloads/</a></p>



<p>You could describe Gimp as the poor man’s Photoshop but it’s by no means a poor relation.<br><br>Gimp is open source, so it’s free. You can download a version for Mac or Windows and it’s capable of doing pretty much anything that Photoshop can do.<br><br>There’s a learning curve to Gimp but there are 100’s of tutorials on Youtube that will walk you through any and all aspects of this powerful software.</p>



<p>I tend to mainly use it for removing backgrounds. Its fuzzy select tool is a godsend.</p>

I actually use the paid version but the free version is straight-up awesome on every level.</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">WordPress</h3>



<p><a href="https://wordpress.org/">https://wordpress.org/</a></p>



<p>No list regarding free tools for a hospitality website would be complete without WordPress.<br>It’s hands down the best tool for building sites and it’s free, which is pretty amazing considering what you get.</p>



<p>To be honest, WordPress had a mixed reputation because of the fairly steep learning curve of getting used to using it. But since it’s introduced its drag and drop Gutenberg editor it’s much, much easier to use.</p>



<p>If you are new to WordPress don’t worry, there are 1,000’s of video tutorials on Youtube on all aspects from beginner guides to more advanced functions.</p>



<p>Be sure you use&nbsp;<a href="https://wordpress.org/">https://wordpress.org/</a>&nbsp;not wordpress.com</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Imagify</h3>



<p><a href="https://imagify.io/">https://imagify.io/</a></p>



<p>Images on your website account for the vast majority of site speed issues. Images make up most of each page’s size so if we can reduce the size of our images we can make each page load faster.</p>



<p>That’s where Imagify comes into its own. I actually use a paid version of this plugin but the free version will suffice for most hospitality owners.<br>Install and activate the plugin, apply the settings and Imagify will compress your images without affecting the visual quality.</p>



<p>This video will walk you through the installation and setup</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Send In Blue</h3>



<p><a href="https://sendinblue.com">https://sendinblue.com</a></p>



<p>Your hospitality website really should have a way of offering a free downloadable PDF about your location and also offer site visitors a regular newsletter.<br>This is how you harvest the email addresses of potential guests. This is lead generation 101.</p>



<p>I used to use Mailchimp to do this but I’ve recently changed over to Sendinblue as it offers more functions in its free package.<br>I particularly like the automation that you can build into your email marketing.<br>They also offer a free website bot that you can add to your site if you wish.</p>



<p>The only drawback, if you can call it that, is that there is a 300 emails a day limit to the free package. Saying that it’s easy to divide your email list into groups of 250 in order to get around that problem</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Speed Tests</h2>



<figure class="wp-block-image"><img decoding="async" src="https://rentmoreweeks.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/09/speedometer-1063350_640.png" alt=""/></figure>



<p>Now that you have used the above tools to make your hospitality website rock you really need to see how fast each page is.<br>This is crucial as site speed is now a major metric in Google’s algorithm.<br>Slow sites = Bad search ranking. Fast sites = High Ranking.</p>



<p>I use two sites to check page speed with GTmetrix being the one I use on a daily basis</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">GTmetrix</h3>



<p><a href="https://gtmetrix.com/">https://gtmetrix.com/</a></p>



<p>Here’s a short video that I made on how to use this site</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><br>PageSpeed Insights</h3>



<p><a href="https://developers.google.com/speed/pagespeed/insights">https://developers.google.com/speed/pagespeed/insights</a></p>



<p>This is Google’s own page speed testing site and I use this site because I want to know what Google thinks of the sites that I make. It also has the advantage of testing both mobile and desktop.</p>



<p>Just add one URL at a time and click the ‘Analyze’ button.<br>Around a minute later you’ll see a report giving you a score out of 100 and suggestions of how to make speed improvements.</p>
