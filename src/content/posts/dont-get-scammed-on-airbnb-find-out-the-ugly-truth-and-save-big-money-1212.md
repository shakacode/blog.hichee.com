<p>So, what&#8217;s the reason for this type of massive price differential and why do some hosts hate Airbnb?</p>
<p>Before I qualify that bold <strong>Vacation rental hosts really hate Airbnb</strong> opening, I should add that I have been helping vacation rental hosts market their properties since 2003. That&#8217;s 5 years before Airbnb even existed.</p>
<p>Back then, hosts were called owners, because they owned the property. Hence, VRBO or Vacation Rental By Owner.<br />In most cases, owners rented out their second homes when they weren&#8217;t using them themselves. In a lot of cases, these homes were worth an awful lot of money.</p>
<p>Then along came Airbnb with their blow-up mattresses, sofa beds in spare rooms, and couch surfing options.<br />To be honest, vacation rental owners just ignored Airbnb for the first 5 years or so. They looked at them as chalk and cheese. They were in a different marketplace. </p>
<p>But, as the years passed, Airbnb grew from market disrupter to market force majeure.</p>
<p>They became a verb. <br />People Airbnb&#8217;d. <br />They became a marketing force to be reconned with. Something that couldn&#8217;t be ignored.<br />Airbnb ripped the heart out of VRBO, HomeAway, Owners Direct, and many other companies.</p>
<p>The old school property owners had no choice but to join the spare room renting hosts because that&#8217;s where the bookings were to be had. Airbnb was THE brand. They had THE traffic. </p>
<p>And there, the trouble began&#8230;</p>
<p>When it comes to hosts, Airbnb has a one size fits all outlook.<br />They see no difference between a host renting a sofa bed in a single room in a shared house and the owner of a multi-million Dollar mansion on the beach in Malibu.</p>
<p>And, when it comes to guests, Airbnb has a similar outlook.<br />They see no difference between a stag party of 10, 18 to 20-year-olds and a couple in their 50&#8217;s taking a city break.</p>
<p>Now, don&#8217;t get me wrong, I&#8217;m all for equality but we all need to look at things from both sides of the fence.</p>
<p>Imagine that I own a high-end, million-dollar property on the beach in Malibu,  (I don&#8217;t, in case you&#8217;re wondering).<br />Let&#8217;s say that it comes complete with white carpets, white sofas, white bedding, designer fittings, and expensive artwork. </p>
<figure class="wp-block-image alignwide size-large"><img decoding="async" width="1024" height="568" src="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/Screenshot-2022-08-11-at-16.46.03-1024x568.png" alt="" class="wp-image-1398" srcset="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/Screenshot-2022-08-11-at-16.46.03-1024x568.png 1024w, https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/Screenshot-2022-08-11-at-16.46.03-300x166.png 300w, https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/Screenshot-2022-08-11-at-16.46.03-768x426.png 768w, https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/Screenshot-2022-08-11-at-16.46.03-1536x852.png 1536w, https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/Screenshot-2022-08-11-at-16.46.03.png 2008w" sizes="(max-width: 1024px) 100vw, 1024px" /></figure>
</p>
<p>I really don&#8217;t want to be playing host to stag parties, spring break groups or under 25&#8217;s in general.</p>
<p>I&#8217;m going to be laying awake at night worried about my lovely home and my precious possessions. I&#8217;m going to be concerned that if someone holds a party at my place, things could get out of hand, my neighbors may get upset, and things could get damaged, stained or broken.</p>
<p>I may have to cancel the next group of guests if things need to be replaced or repaired.<br />It&#8217;s all a bit of a potential nightmare.</p>
<p>So, what do I do?</p>
<p>Well, I stick with VRBO because I can refuse to rent to under 25-year-olds on their platform and their guest demographic is, in general,  targeted at family-oriented and older audiences.</p>
<p>In order to hedge my bets,  I&#8217;ll advertise on Airbnb because they can help with my occupancy rate due to their popularity.<br /><strong>But I&#8217;ll do so at a heavy premium.</strong></p>
<p>Now, this may all sound a bit far-fetched but this is exactly what&#8217;s happening.<br />It&#8217;s not uncommon to see properties listed on Airbnb, priced at double, to five times the price of the same property on VRBO.</p>
