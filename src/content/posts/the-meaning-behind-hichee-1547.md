<p>You have heard of&nbsp;<a href="https://www.hichee.com/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">HiChee</a>, but where does “<em>Chee</em>” come from?</p>
<p>Chee comes from the word<em>&nbsp;Cheehoo</em>, a Hawaiian slang originating from the Samoa culture. It is a term that is often used around family and friends in a positive light. “<em>Cheehoo!</em>” is exclaimed when you are celebrating or if something is exciting. The term is most similar to terms like&nbsp;<em>woohoo, yee-haw</em>, or&nbsp;<em>wahoo</em>.</p>
<p>Check out this video from&nbsp;<em>Hawaii News Now&nbsp;</em>that features native Hawaiian, William Vaana, exclaiming the Hawaiian term “<em>Cheehoo!</em>” He emulates Disney’s “Moana” rendition of Maui, the demigod. Since release of the movie, William Vaana has been capturing the attention of many on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii.</p>
