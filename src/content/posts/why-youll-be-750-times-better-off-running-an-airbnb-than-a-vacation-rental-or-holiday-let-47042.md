In the UK, you may tell people that you&#8217;re operating a holiday let or a holiday cottage. </p>

<p>Certain brands become so synonymous with a thing or an activity that they define that thing or activity. When I said I was Hoovering the lounge in the opening paragraph, you all knew what I was doing, even though I don&#8217;t own a Hoover &#8211; I have a Dyson vacuum cleaner. </p>



<p>William Henry Hoover didn&#8217;t invent the vacuum cleaner. He redesigned and reinvented it, and his company&#8217;s marketing led to higher brand awareness than the competition&#8217;s.<br><br>The same goes for Velcro, Kleenex, Dumpsters, Jacuzzis, Tupperware, and Band-Aid. All of these brands became so synonymous with what they made that their brand name is used to describe the item &#8211; Even if it&#8217;s made by someone else.</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading has-text-align-center"><strong>That&#8217;s where we&#8217;re at with Airbnb.</strong></h2>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading has-text-align-center has-text-color" style="color:#f60202">Airbnb owns our space. <br>Not because they bought it but because the public bought into it. </h2>



<p>Airbnb has become synonymous with vacation rentals, holiday rentals; call it what you will. So much so that these other terms have become redundant. They&#8217;re dead in the water, lying face down in your short-term rental swimming pool. </p>

<p><strong>Screenshot the above line. Reread it again and again. The public, your potential guests, aren&#8217;t searching for vacation rentals.</strong> <strong>That&#8217;s what this is all about.</strong></p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading has-text-align-center">Conclusion</h2>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Take the emotion out of the equation</h3>



<p>I&#8217;m fully aware that many hosts and property managers dislike Airbnb. The company has disrupted and transformed the rental space since its inception in 2008. I can understand that many would be reluctant to even mention Airbnb on their websites, but that reluctance could ultimately cost web traffic and bookings.</p>



<p>If I make and sell orange flavor ice lollies and my customers keep asking for lemon flavor, I would be silly not to make and sell lemon flavor lollies. Even if I don&#8217;t personally like lemon flavor.</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Adapt to succeed</h3>



<p>In order to be (or stay) successful in business, we need to adapt. We must align with our customer&#8217;s needs, wants, and expectations.<br><br>Potential guests are looking for an Airbnb to stay in. They aren&#8217;t looking specifically for a property listed on Airbnb, though. They are just using the word Airbnb as short-hand for a vacation rental, holiday rental, gite, holiday let, or short-term rental. </p>



<p>They&#8217;re using Airbnb as a generic term to describe what they are looking for, and if you want your property to be found as a result of those searches, you better rebrand your business, to some degree, as an Airbnb. </p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading has-text-align-center">If you want to be found, you must make yourself visible to those looking for you. </h3>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">What can you do?</h2>



<p><strong>For those hosts and managers using Airbnb.</strong><br>If you list your property on Airbnb, then you are running an Airbnb. End of.  No one (including Airbnb themselves) could complain if you have wording along the lines of &#8216;<strong>Book our Airbnb directly with us</strong>&#8216; or &#8216;<strong>The best Airbnb in the area</strong>&#8216; or &#8216;<strong>A stunning Airbnb in xxxx</strong>&#8216;. There&#8217;s no need to link to your listing, in fact, I would discourage that altogether.</p>



<p><strong>For those hosts and managers not using Airbnb.</strong><br>This is a little trickier. Generically you&#8217;re running an Airbnb but technically you aren&#8217;t. </p>



<p>As Airbnb is free to list on, personally I would list my property and then block all dates. I would now have an Airbnb and I&#8217;d be comfortable using the wording in the paragraph above.</p>

<p>Have a thorough read through your site, looking for mentions of vacation rental, holiday rental, etc. Think about swapping out those terms for Airbnb. <br><br>Pay particular attention to headings. This underlines that the user has arrived on a page that meets their needs.</p>
