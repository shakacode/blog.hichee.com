<p>Few things can be as frustrating for Airbnb guests than to see their reservations cancelled by the host, especially on short notice.</p>
<p>Airbnb is hiking the maximum fee 10-fold to $1,000, from the current $100, on August 22 for certain host-generated cancellations.</p>
<p>“Avoidable host cancellations are not common, but when they do happen, they can have a negative impact on a guest’s experience, as well as the wider hosting community,” an Airbnb spokesperson said. “Following a thorough review of our policy, we have updated our existing fees to better reflect the costs associated with preventable cancellations, including rebooking guests, often at the last minute.”</p>
<p>Airbnb explains the new fee structure to hosts&nbsp;<a href="https://www.airbnb.com/help/article/990/host-cancellation-policy">here</a>.</p>
<p>The actual fee that hosts would get charged would range from $50 to $1,000, depending on the amount of the reservation.</p>
<p>Hosts would still be able to cancel bookings without penalties or other repercussions, such as getting their listing or account suspended, for things such as the need to make emergency repairs, if they can prove the guest plans to have a house party or break other property rules, or if the home or apartment can’t accommodate families or guests, for instance.</p>
