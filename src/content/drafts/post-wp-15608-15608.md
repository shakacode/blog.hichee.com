<p>This is one of Valencia&#8217;s most impressive natural parks and is located near another water reserve known as the Laguna de la Mata. This place spreads across 1,400 hectares and is separated from the Laguna de la Mata by an anticline (a formation of stratified rock raised up) called the Chaparral. Still, both are connected to the ocean through a canal known as Acequión. This canal was created to obtain salt from the ocean in the 13th century. As a legacy of its past, huge mountains of salt are piled high along the shore.</p>
