<p><em>The following guest post is by my friends Adam from the website <a href="https://www.gettingstamped.com/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Getting Stamped</a>. He and his wife are long time residents of Milwaukee and, since I’ve never been, I’ve invited him on to share some tips about visiting the city.</em></p>
<p>Milwaukee is underrated, in my opinion. I love it! </p>
<p>The largest city in Wisconsin and only 1.5 hours north of Chicago, it is the perfect size: not too small not too big. It offers tasty food and drinks, great sports teams, and fun festivals. Plus, it’s affordable. And don’t forget the friendly locals!</p>
<p>I have lived here my whole life, and my wife has lived here for 20 years. We’ve traveled around the world many times, and when it came time to plant roots and start a family, we could have gone anywhere. But we choose to come home to Milwaukee, which has so much to offer.</p>
<p>If you want to know how to experience it like a local, here are my insider tips:</p>

<h3><span id="1-stay-central">1. Stay central</span></h3>
<p>I highly recommend staying downtown or the Third Ward. This is where most of the popular attractions are located. The hotels might be slightly more expensive than elsewhere, but you’ll save on transportation costs since everything is walkable.</p>
<p>These are two hotels I love:</p>
<h3><span id="2-take-advantage-of-free-rides">2. Take advantage of free rides</span></h3>
<p>Milwaukee does not have a subway system, but it does have an amazing free streetcar service called the Hop, with numerous stops downtown and around the Third Ward area. Simply hop on and off — no tickets or reservations are needed! The Hop runs every 15 minutes during peak times and every 20 minutes off-peak and weekends. <a href="https://thehopmke.com/interactive-map/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener noreferrer">Click here for the map and schedule</a>.</p>
<p><strong>Insider tip</strong>: If you are heading to a sporting event, many bars and restaurants offer free shuttles to American Family Field for Brewer baseball games and concerts, Summerfest Grounds for festivals and concerts, Fiserv Forum for Bucks basketball games, and other events around town.<br /> </p>
<h3><span id="3-eat-like-a-local">3. Eat like a local</span></h3>
<p><img decoding="async" src="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/03/PublicMarket.jpeg" width="675" height="395" alt="The popular Public Market at night in bustling Milwaukee, USA" /></p>
<p><noscript><img decoding="async" src="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/03/PublicMarket.jpeg" width="675" height="395" alt="The popular Public Market at night in bustling Milwaukee, USA" /></noscript><br />You might think Milwaukee’s all beer and cheese (and there is no shortage of those), but the food scene has a lot more to offer. Milwaukee has its own (sometimes quirky) food traditions and staples, like ButterBurgers and frozen custard. </p>
<p>It also has some incredible markets, where you can enjoy some of the city’s best food from many vendors, all in one place and in a fun and affordable food-hall atmosphere. These are two of my favorites:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="https://3rdstmarkethall.com/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener noreferrer">3rd Street Market Hall</a> – This is a must-visit destination, as it provides a place for tourists and locals alike to enjoy Milwaukee’s diverse cuisine. Here, you will find the aforementioned frozen custard, plus pho, pizza, burgers, artisan sandwiches, tacos, hot dogs, home-cooked meals, ramen, sushi, arepas, and so much more — all at affordable prices.</li>
<li><a href="https://milwaukeepublicmarket.org/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener noreferrer">Milwaukee Public Market</a> – Located in the historic Third Ward, this awesome market is the best place to patronize a wide variety of vendors with unique products, food offerings, and drink pairings. It’s a unique spot to experience the flavors of Milwaukee’s culinary scene, all under one roof, and soak in the local culture while shopping or grabbing a bite.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span id="4-indulge-in-local-delicacies">4. Indulge in local delicacies</span></h3>
<p><img decoding="async" src="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/03/Vanguard.jpeg" width="675" height="395" alt="Feasting at Vanguard, a restaurant in Milwaukee known for its amazing house-made sausages" /></p>
<p><noscript><img decoding="async" src="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/03/Vanguard.jpeg" width="675" height="395" alt="Feasting at Vanguard, a restaurant in Milwaukee known for its amazing house-made sausages" /></noscript><br />There are a few foods that you need to try that you (maybe for good reason) will not find many other places. They are unique, tasty, and overall pretty affordable!</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Deep-fried cheese curds</strong> – Yes, it’s a thing, and they are amazing — order them and thank us later. <a href="https://lakefrontbrewery.com/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener noreferrer">Lakefront Brewery</a> is our top pick.</li>
<li><strong>Frozen custard</strong> – It may look like ice cream, but it’s much richer and creamier, with a few extra ingredients. <a href="https://kopps.com/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener noreferrer">Kopps</a> is the best place for it (and great burgers) — and also one of those places that, if you don’t go, did you even visit Milwaukee?</li>
<li><strong>Bratwursts</strong> – Another Milwaukee staple. Nobody does them at a higher level than <a href="https://www.vanguardbar.com/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener noreferrer">Vanguard Restaurant</a> in the Bayview neighborhood. It is best known for its amazing house-made sausages, including vegan options, with many at an affordable price of under $10.</li>
</ul>
<p>In this list, we have only focused on the Milwaukee favorites, but foodies who want to delve deeper into the culinary scene may also want to check out our full list of the <a href="https://www.gettingstamped.com/best-restaurants-in-milwaukee/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">best restaurants in Milwaukee</a>.<br /> </p>
<h3><span id="5-explore-brewing-history">5. Explore brewing history</span></h3>
<p>While microbreweries aren’t exactly a new trend, Milwaukee has been brewing since before it was cool. At one time, it brewed more beer than any city in the world. That history is evident all around town in breweries, restaurants, and pubs.</p>
<p><a href="https://lakefrontbrewery.com/brewery-tours/our-famous-tour" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener noreferrer">Lakefront Brewery</a> offers probably the best brewery tour in the city. It’s a lot more fun than just looking at shiny tanks and sniffing a pungent jar filled with hops. The tour guides are hilarious (some are literally comedians from a local club), and your tour will include four beer samples. This is by far the best value and our personal favorite tour — plus, the beer hall is a lot of fun. The tours are super popular and sell out on the weekends, so it is advised to purchase tickets in advance.</p>
<p>If you want to keep sampling local beers, make sure to check out a few other of my favorite Milwaukee breweries:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="https://www.mobcraftbeer.com/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener noreferrer">MobCraft Beer</a> – This brewery offers tours and also has a great taproom with tasty pizzas, as well as really good IPAs and sour beers. </li>
<li><a href="https://www.eagleparkbrewing.com/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener noreferrer">Eagle Park Brewing Company</a> – They do not offer tours, but they do have a huge selection of unique sour beers.</li>
<li><a href="https://www.goodcitybrewing.com/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener noreferrer">Good City Brewing</a> – There are two locations: the Eastside one offers tours, and the downtown one does not. But the latter is right across the street from Fiserv Forum and great place for a beer before a game.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span id="6-do-some-affordable-activities">6. Do some affordable activities</span></h3>
<p><img decoding="async" src="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/03/Riverwalk.jpeg" width="675" height="395" alt="The relaxing Riverwalk along the river in Milwaukee, USA" /></p>
<p><noscript><img decoding="async" src="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/03/Riverwalk.jpeg" width="675" height="395" alt="The relaxing Riverwalk along the river in Milwaukee, USA" /></noscript><br />Milwaukee has way more to do than we could squeeze onto this page, but we’ve narrowed it down, focusing on must-do and budget-friendly options (some are even free!):</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="https://county.milwaukee.gov/EN/Parks/Explore/Lakefront/Bradford-Beach" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener noreferrer">Bradford Beach</a> – This is a sandy park on the shorelines of Lake Michigan. Bring a towel and go for a swim, join in a game of beach volleyball, or grab custard at MooSa’s or lunch at The Dock Waterfront Bar &#038; Grill.</li>
<li><a href="https://city.milwaukee.gov/DCD/Projects/RiverWalk" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener noreferrer">Stroll the Milwaukee Riverwalk</a> – Experience the heart of the city, and immerse yourself in its culture. Many of the Milwaukee’s best shops and restaurants can be found along the three-mile route along both sides of the Milwaukee River.</li>
<li><strong>Disc-golf at Milwaukee County Parks</strong> – Milwaukee has <a href="https://county.milwaukee.gov/EN/Parks/Experience/Sports/Disc-Golf" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener noreferrer">11 disc-golf courses</a> — six full ones ($5) and five short-practice ones (free).</li>
<li><strong>Bike the Oak Leaf Trail</strong> – Milwaukee County offers over 135 miles of trails for cycling, many of which feature scenic views of Lake Michigan. You can find the trail map <a href="https://county.milwaukee.gov/EN/Parks/Explore/Trails/Oak-Leaf-Trail" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener noreferrer">here</a>.</li>
<li><a href="https://www.visitmilwaukee.org/about-milwaukee/the-bronze-fonz/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener noreferrer">Bronze Fonz</a> – Stop to take a picture with a bronze sculpture of Arthur Fonzarelli, aka the Fonz, from Happy Days (a sitcom about a Milwaukee family in the 1950s).</li>
<li><strong>Indoor lawn games at 3rd Street Market Hall</strong> – Enjoy a variety of unique restaurants and free indoor games, including giant Jenga, cornhole, shuffleboard, and golf simulators. It’s a fun place, especially when the weather isn’t great!</li>
</ul>
<p>After you’ve scoured this guide, if you need to fill any blank spots in your itinerary, we have an in-depth list covering all of the <a href="https://www.gettingstamped.com/things-to-do-in-milwaukee/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">things to do in Milwaukee</a>.<br /> </p>
<h3><span id="7-enjoy-free-admission-days">7. Enjoy free-admission days</span></h3>
<p>Some of Milwaukee’s top attractions welcome visitors for free on certain days. Watching the calendar can score you free tickets that otherwise would set your group back hundreds.</p>
<h3><span id="8-chase-cheap-happy-hours">8. Chase cheap happy hours</span></h3>
<p>For better or worse, the people of Milwaukee enjoy their fun times intertwined with a beer or three. To kick off an exciting night in Milwaukee, head to where the locals go: find a good happy hour, or go to a budget-friendly dive bar. </p>
<ul>
<li><a href="https://www.mcgillycuddysmilwaukee.com/specials.php" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener noreferrer">McGillycuddy’s</a> – $1 taps, $2 craft beers, $4 rails and mixers, and $5 appetizers (Tuesday–Friday, 3–7pm).</li>
<li><a href="https://mosirishpub.com/milwaukee/happyhour/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener noreferrer">Mo’s Irish Pub</a> – $2.72 drafts, $5 wines (Tues–Thurs 4–6pm, Fridays 3–6pm); also free brewery shuttles.</li>
<li><strong>Milwaukee Brat House</strong> – Happy hour specials change daily (Mon–Fri 3-6pm).</li>
</ul>
<h3><span id="9-explore-the-vibrant-public-art-scene">9. Explore the vibrant public art scene</span></h3>
<p>Many places around town have large-scale public art displays that you can visit for free. Most of these pieces are in some of the most fun and trendy areas. These are a couple of our favorites:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="https://www.blackcatmke.com/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener noreferrer">Black Cat Alley</a> – One of the largest areas dedicated to public murals is located near North Avenue, in the Eastside neighborhood. In addition to the art, you’ll also find great restaurants, breweries, and artisanal shops.</li>
<li><a href="https://www.sculpturemilwaukee.com/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener noreferrer">Sculpture Milwaukee</a> – Annually, this city-funded program displays sculptures by artists from across the world on Wisconsin Avenue. Some are permanent and some are changed out every year, so there are always new things to see.</li>
</ul>
<p><center>***</center></p>
<p>From food to free days and bratwurst to beer, Milwaukee has a ton of things to do and see on a budget. Hopefully this list will give you a good idea of where to start!</p>
