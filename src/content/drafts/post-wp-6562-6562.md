<span style="font-size: small;"><span style="font-family: inherit;"><b>3. Sangria from Spain</b></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-size: small;"><span style="font-family: inherit;">Sangria is Spain’s favorite and is made using red wine and a<br />
punch. You can add a variant of fruits to make a punch like berries, apples,<br />
lemons, pineapple, oranges, etc. Adding this punch to the wine along with<br />
brandy or orange juice gives a sweet flavor to this beverage. The right wine<br />
for a Sangria mix is the dry red wines that are simple, has fruity flavor and<br />
aroma. Older red wines won’t taste appropriate for Sangria.</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
</div>
<p><span style="font-size: small;"><span style="font-family: inherit;"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal;"><span style="background: white; mso-highlight: white;">4. The Greek Ouzo <br />
</span></b><span style="background: white; mso-highlight: white;">Ouzo drink is a<br />
favorite among Greeks. It is a strong drink taken with cold water in a thin<br />
tall glass. Due to its high alcohol and sugar content, it is advisable to take<br />
this strong drink slowly and with some Ouzo Mezedes. Mezes are small plates of<br />
appetizers in greek like salads, sardines, octopus or calamari. It&#8217;s customary<br />
to have ouzo with a choice of Mezedes.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;"> <br />
</span>Varvayiannis or Ouzo 12 are the two popular and high-quality brands of<br />
ouzo. On your next trip to Greece, the highlight should be enjoying a glass of<br />
Ouzu sitting by the seaside.</span></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="background: white none repeat scroll 0% 0%; line-height: 150%; margin: 8pt 0in 19pt; text-align: left;">
<span style="font-size: small;"><span style="font-family: inherit;"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal;"><span style="background: white; mso-highlight: white;">5. <span style="color: black;">Arak from the beautiful beaches of Bali</span><br />
</span></b><span style="background: white; mso-highlight: white;">Arak is an Arabic<br />
word meaning spirit and here it refers to the locally produced alcohol in<br />
Indonesia and Malaysia. The locals use distilled coconut palm sap, sugarcane or<br />
a mixture of black sticky rice and coconut milk to make this. The alcohol<br />
percentage ranges between 30-50 percent and looks similar to rum. You should be<br />
careful while trying arak since sometimes it contains Methanol that can cause<br />
coma, blindness or death. Try it from a local shop, rather than trying<br />
homemade.</span></span></span></div>

<span style="font-size: small;"><span style="font-family: inherit;"><span style="background: white; mso-highlight: white;">This drink is a favorite<br />
among many because of the experimentation scope it provides. The core<br />
ingredient of Gin is Juniper berries, the distillation of which involves the<br />
