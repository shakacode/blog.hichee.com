<h2>Travel and destination predictions</h2>
<p>According to Expedia’s <a href="https://welcome.expediagroup.com/en/research-and-insights/traveler-value-index-2023">Traveller Value Index 2023</a>, even though the cost of living crisis is sweeping each nation, a staggering <strong>79% of travellers (asked) are still planning to take a ‘leisure trip’ in 2023,</strong> with just under 50% increasing their travel budget for the upcoming year. </p>
<p>This shows us the current economic climate is yet to impact the travel decisions of many – even SkyScanner’s <a href="https://www.skyscanner.net/news/travel-trends#:~:text=Wildlife%20spotting%20and%20hiking%20are,key%20component%20of%20travel%20plans.">Travel Trend Report</a> found <strong>only 8% of travellers were looking to spend less on travel in 2023.</strong> </p>
<p>On the other hand, Skyscanner also suggested <strong>‘price-driven travel’ will be the main priority for 2023.</strong> Humphrey Bowles (Know Your Guest) agrees, suggesting that <strong>the impact will be on the means of travel,</strong> with <a href="https://youtu.be/hEW_ZSNXqj0">fly-in destinations</a> being less popular in comparison to drive-in destinations, as guests will look to stay local and explore easy-to-access locations.</p>
<p>On the other hand, Madison Rifkin (Mount) explores this further, suggesting <strong>travel will be affected based on a generational disposition:</strong> </p>
<p><em>“I think older generations are probably going to turn more to driving because they’ve lived through a recession and they understand how to save money, whereas this new generation, gen Z – that I’m a part of – will continue to fly. We haven’t lived through a recession before, so will have to navigate that.”</em></p>
<p>With that in mind, what are the top destinations for 2023? According to Airbnb, the top <a href="https://news.airbnb.com/cheers-to-2022s-top-destinations-airbnb-shares-its-2023-travel-trends/">trending international travel spots</a> include:</p>

<li>Auckland, New Zealand</li>

</ul>
<h2>Airbnb, direct bookings or mid-term stays, which way is forward?</h2>
<p>Despite what you may hear, there isn’t a clear-cut right or wrong answer here. </p>
<p>With the industry sharing short-term rental predictions for 2023, it’s important to note that not all hosts are the same, and not everyone’s aims are the same. But, there are <strong>three things </strong>we are certain will impact the industry in 2023:<strong> demand/oversaturation, direct booking channels and mid-term stays.</strong></p>
<p><strong>Demand for short-term rentals has surpassed even the highest expectations throughout 2022</strong> (<a href="https://www.airdna.co/blog/2023-us-short-term-rental-outlook-report">AirDNA</a>) and 2023 growth is predicted to continue but at a slower pace. </p>
<p>We spoke to Erin Lenhardt, Co-Founder of STR to Wealth and Founder of <a href="https://thebrighthouse.us/">The Brighthouse</a> to find out why this may be: </p>
<p><em>“Due to oversaturation of listings, combined with economic factors, some hosts will be exiting the space and looking to turn their STRs into mid-term furnished rentals. At the same time, amateur hosts will continue to enter the market, hoping to put a few extra bucks in their pocket, especially due to recession and inflation.”</em></p>

<p>So, with short-term rental market data showing <a href="https://www.businessinsider.com/airbnb-and-vrbo-owners-better-brace-for-2023-2022-12?r=US&amp;IR=T">more competition than ever</a>, and supply hitting <a href="https://www.airdna.co/blog/2023-us-short-term-rental-outlook-report">record levels</a> in 2022, how do you stand out from the crowd in 2023? </p>
<p>It seems the trending answer is direct bookings.</p>
<p>According to Humphrey Bowles, <strong>2023 will be the breakout year for direct bookings</strong> with everybody looking for the best deal possible, and with the new release of <a href="https://www.amazon.co.uk/Book-Direct-Blueprint-Mark-Simpson/dp/1913284360">The Book Direct Blueprint</a>, hosts have a multitude of tips at their fingers to help them embark on this journey. </p>
<p>To implement a successful direct booking channel, hosts will need to remember 4 things:</p>
<ol>
<li><strong>Provide a quality experience to every guest </strong>– make guests remember you; word of mouth is a great marketing resource.</li>
</ol>
<ol start="2">
<li><strong>Market effectively to previous guests </strong>– automate your guest communication, collect their details, add them to mailing lists or even offer complimentary discounts; the possibilities are endless. Brindy Bringhurst (Airbnb Expert) predicts <em>“guests will start noticing Airbnb hosts putting in more effort to collect emails, and greater marketing efforts to encourage returning guests”.</em></li>
</ol>
<ol start="3">
<li><strong>Understand who your audience is</strong> – are they families, couples, digital nomads, or naturists; knowing who your audience is allows you to personalise their stay and create a greater guest experience.</li>
</ol>
<ol start="4">
<li><strong>Choose the right platform to target your audience</strong> – be where your audience is; Google search can be a hard competitor – unless you’ve nailed down your SEO strategy – so if your audience uses social media, that’s where you need to be.</li>
</ol>

<p>It’s evident the individual needs of software and solutions are unique to each business, but the commonality is that automation was the highest-voted (<a href="https://www.hostfully.com/2022-hospitality-industry-study/">18%</a>) obstacle faced in terms of technology. </p>
<p>As such, <strong>we’re predicting technology to be a popular trend for property manager investments throughout 2023</strong>, as they look to implement direct booking strategies and will need value-added solutions to do so. </p>
<p>So what technology and software will trend in 2023? Here’s what we think:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Risk Management &#038; Guest Screening</strong> – In 2023, more property managers will be turning to risk management solutions, including <a href="https://knowyourguest.superhog.com/guest-screening/">guest screening</a>, <a href="https://noiseaware.com/">noise monitoring</a> and <a href="https://www.minut.com/?campaignid=1733322407&amp;adgroupid=67972750735&amp;adid=355257096122&amp;gclid=Cj0KCQiA-oqdBhDfARIsAO0TrGGTGVTsJiyGtAWwhd8czqK_8t9xn6InsgWwpvlOigUDIxqPy24gcB4aAlVUEALw_wcB">occupancy monitoring devices</a>, to help mitigate the risks associated with short-term rental bookings, so they can host safely, maintain privacy and not compromise the guest experience. </li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li><strong>Property Management Software</strong> – The most popular software for short-term rental property managers and they won’t be going anywhere, but we could be in for some new improvements and features that will allow for greater automation, greater reservation control and website building features to support the direct booking trend. </li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li><strong>Damage Protection</strong> – The move towards more independence and control over listings will see more property managers and hosts look for their own damage protection solutions. Collecting a <a href="https://superhog.com/blog-posts-damage-waiver-vs-damage-deposit/">damage deposit or damage waiver</a> and ensuring the right protection is in place will allow property managers to reduce their reliance on the big <a href="https://superhog.com/airbnb-aircover-winter-release-2022/">OTAs</a>. </li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li><strong>Guest Communication Platforms</strong> – Automation is a key obstacle property managers are looking to solve and automated guest messaging will help streamline the whole process and boost the guest experience. These platforms can be utilised throughout the whole booking journey including post-stay communication to help retarget and market to previous guests.</li>
</ul>
