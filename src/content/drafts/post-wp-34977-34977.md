<p>When it comes to finding and booking accommodation for holidays or vacations, we are creatures of habit. We jump on the web or grab our mobile, and we open up an app of choice.<br><br>Most of us will head to Airbnb, or Booking (.com), or Expedia (VRBO). </p>



<p>We use these app for a number of reasons. They are very easy to use, they have a vast inventory of properties to choose from, and there&#8217;s a certain peace of mind that comes with booking via a big brand website.</p>



<p>But what if I told you that you may be paying more than twice the price if you don&#8217;t shop around.</p>



<p>In this article, We&#8217;ll show you the huge savings that can be found, just by doing a few minutes searching on the web.</p>



<p></p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Notes</h2>



<p>I started with a property in France (details below) that&#8217;s been verified on HiChee so that I know the book-direct details. I can then find the lowest price anywhere on the web. I selected 2 weeks in July with six adults and four children.</p>



<p>I then started searching for other websites that list this property. This particular property is listed on over 15 sites.</p>



<p>The direct booking price is $6.649&nbsp;</p>



<p>The same booking with Marriott is $11,943 (79% more)<br>The same booking with Booking (.com) is $15,224 (a staggering 128% more)<br>The other sites vary wildly in price from low to high.</p>



<p>The price on Airbnb is $8,803, and the price on VRBO is $7.917 ($2,154 and $1,268, respectfully more than booking direct)</p>



<p>I know that most travelers like to book through the big brand sites for added peace of mind, but they could take out substantial travel insurance and still have a chunk of spending money in the bank.</p>



<p>It gets worse&#8230;</p>



<p>If a potential guest was to find this property on&nbsp;Coxycozy, the best deal link takes them to Hometogo, where the best deal link then takes them to Gites.com, so three &#8216;middlemen&#8217; sites are each taking a cut of the booking.</p>



<p>Hometogo has three options to book the same property for the exact same dates and the same mix of guests.<br>1. The&nbsp;<a href="http://gites.com/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">gites.com</a>&nbsp;price is $7.654 (15% or $1,005 more than booking directly with the owner).<br>2. Booking through the platform with Hometogo at $10,211 (53% or $3,562 more than booking directly with the owner).<br>3. Booking with Booking.com at $15,276 (129% or $8,627 more than booking directly with the owner). Insert gulp emoji.</p>



<p>This highlights the tangled web that potential guests have to navigate when finding and booking vacation rentals.</p>



<p>I have listed the prices in a spreadsheet and taken screenshots in case the property becomes booked for those two weeks.</p>



<p>This may be an extreme example as I&#8217;m using a large property and looking at two weeks in high season, but it does highlight some of the price gouging that is common in the STR business world.</p>
