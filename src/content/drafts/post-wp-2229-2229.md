</p>
<p>What is the cost of doing this business?</p>
</p>
<p>Is it in terms of monetary value of items that have been damaged and need replacement or repair, or is it reputation management?</p>
</p>
<p>The phrase is used across Facebook groups and forums and is garnering its own acronym – COB.&nbsp; It’s a topic every host and manager must grapple with on a day-to-day basis as they make decisions, handle conflict from multiple directions, and risk losing goodwill.</p>
</p>
<p>Sharon Michie is a seasoned property manager with over 2 decades of hospitality experience running Cottages to Castles in Sanibel and Captiva.&nbsp; Her portfolio of properties includes many upscale and luxury homes where repairs and replacement can run into thousands of dollars.</p>
</p>
<p>In this episode, we explore the highly charged topic of damage management from big-ticket items to the more mundane, with lots of practical examples.</p>
</p>
<p>From building owner expectations to damage prevention strategies and insurance options, this is a must-listen episode.</p>
</p>
<p>You’ll hear:</p>
</p>
<ul>
<li>Sharon’s pragmatic take on the cost of doing business &amp; the uniqueness of every home (and owner)</li>
<li>What she brings to a discussion with owners prior to them registering their property</li>
<li>Why owners need to clarify their emotional attachment to items at the outset</li>
<li>The clause in her rental agreement that deters negative reviews</li>
<li>How to guest-proof furniture and make it less prone to damage</li>
<li>Her absolute rule on responding to a complaint</li>
<li>What to avoid putting in a vacation rental to prevent accidental damage</li>
<li>Why a $3500 table had to be replaced a week after it was put in place</li>
<li>Options for insurance and damage waivers</li>
</ul>
