<h2>What Is Vacation Rental Management?</h2>
<p>Vacation rental management is the supervision and administration of vacation rental properties, ranging from handling bookings, <a href="https://www.igms.com/vacation-rental-cleaning/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">cleaning</a>, and maintenance, to marketing, and taking good care of the guest experience.</p>
<p>Someone who undertakes vacation rental property management is responsible for a whole host of daily operations with the purpose of providing a brilliant guest experience and ensuring the smooth running of a short-term rental business.</p>
<p>Although it overlaps in some instances, <a href="https://www.igms.com/airbnb-vs-renting/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">vacation rental management is different from regular property management</a>. Short-term rental management requires a lot of time spent on guest communication and marketing of properties, far more than traditional property management.</p>
<p>People book vacation rentals more commonly for holiday purposes, so vacation rental managers also focus on providing a top-notch guest experience. This is in contrast to regular property management, as most people lease regular properties on a longer basis for living, not vacation, purposes.</p>
<p> <img decoding="async" src="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/6831c22b-0e8f-43ba-a9d9-5b03b23e8d9c.png" alt="Property management services for seaside properties" /> </p>
<h2>How Does Vacation Rental Management Work?</h2>
<p><a href="https://www.igms.com/holiday-let-management/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Vacation rental management</a>, in essence, is simple. If you own a property such as an apartment, house, cottage, or villa, you can rent it out to travelers and holidaymakers.</p>
<p>People who book your vacation rental property are likely to stay for several days up to a week, however, they can also <a href="https://www.igms.com/airbnb-long-term-rentals/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">book for extended periods</a>. The tasks that you handle relating to property maintenance, and ensuring your guests enjoy their stay, make up rental management.</p>
<p>Vacation management can be outsourced, meaning you hire an external manager or management company to oversee your rentals, or you can do DIY management meaning you undertake all management tasks yourself.</p>

<h3>1. What does a property manager do?</h3>
<p>Despite the title, it’s not actually necessary to own a property to become a vacation rental property manager. With the necessary qualifications and expertise, you can manage properties of other property owners without ever owning one yourself.</p>
<p>As a property manager, your day-to-day operations will include:</p>
<ul>
<li>Responding to <a href="https://www.igms.com/airbnb-messages/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">guest messages</a> and inquiries</li>
<li>Handling reservations and cancellations</li>
<li>Setting and adjusting <a href="https://www.igms.com/airbnb-smart-pricing/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">rental prices</a> for reservations</li>
<li>Obtaining payment for booking</li>
<li>Overseeing guest check-in and checkout</li>
<li>Restocking supplies after every stay</li>
<li>Arranging property cleanings and maintenance visits</li>
<li>Writing <a href="https://www.igms.com/getting-airbnb-guest-reviews-101/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">guest reviews</a></li>
<li>Marketing and promoting the property online.</li>
</ul>
<p>When scaling your property management business, you can take on managing several more vacation rentals for various clients.</p>

<h2>Hiring a Vacation Rental Management Company</h2>
<p>Whether you are a homeowner of one vacation home, or several, you may need a property management company to help you manage things. There are a few things to keep in mind for <a href="https://www.igms.com/airbnb-property-management-services/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">hiring a property management service</a> for your vacation home:</p>
<h3>1. How to find the best vacation rental property management company?</h3>
<h4>1. Identify your needs</h4>
<p>The most important first step is to identify your needs as a host. How much help do you need with managing your vacation rentals? Do you only need help with managing guests and the rental, or do you also need help with marketing and attracting bookings? Once you’ve identified and made a list of your needs you can begin looking for a company that meets your requirements.</p>
<h4>2. Do your research</h4>
<p>The next step is to begin searching for a company that offers the services you are looking for. It’s a good idea to also keep your budget in mind, as larger enterprises will likely charge more for the same property management services than smaller, independent companies.</p>
<p>After identifying potential companies, you can begin screening and vetting them. Check the reviews on Yelp and Google Business and ask the businesses in question to provide references from their current or former customers.</p>
<p><img decoding="async" src="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/vacation-rental-management.pic3_.jpeg" alt="Person researching a vacation rental management company" /></p>
<h4>3. Ask the right questions</h4>
<p>To make sure you select the ideal company for you, it’s important to ask specific questions about their business model. Some questions you can ask include:</p>
<ul>
<li>How many employees make up the company?</li>
<li>Do they offer full-services, or can you select the services you require?</li>
<li>Will you be assigned a fixed agent for your rental, or will it be managed by a team of agents?</li>
<li>Does the company respond to guest requests and messages outside of office hours?</li>
<li>How does the company handle emergencies outside of work hours?</li>
<li>How will the company track and monitor the performance of your rental property?</li>
<li>What strategies will the company use to improve your rental’s performance and <a href="https://www.igms.com/airbnb-occupancy-rate/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">occupancy rate</a>?</li>
<li>Does the company work from a fixed contract?</li>
</ul>
<h4>4. Find out about fees</h4>
<p>Another important topic is <a href="https://www.igms.com/vacation-rental-property-management-fees/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">vacation rental property </a>management fees. Companies generally work from one of three fee structures: the guaranteed income model, the fixed-rate model, and the commission model.</p>
<p>Each fee structure has unique pros and cons for property owners. Aside from knowing what businesses charge, you also need to know what services are being covered in the fee structure, and if certain services will cost extra.</p>

<h3>2.  What does a vacation rental management company charge?</h3>
<p>A rental company’s fees range from around 10% to 50% of the monthly rental income, and can depend on certain factors:</p>
<ul>
<li>The location of the rentals (urban, beach rentals, mountainside properties, etc.)</li>
<li>The number of rentals you would like the company to manage</li>
<li>The size and type of the rentals (luxury villas will require more overall management compared to apartments)</li>
<li>The size of the company</li>
<li>Whether the company is a local company or an international organization</li>
<li>The services offered by the property management company</li>
<li>The fee structure used by the company (fixed-rate, guaranteed income, commission-based, etc.).</li>
</ul>
<p>Keep in mind that the <a href="https://www.igms.com/booking-com-fees/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">fees</a> you end up paying will impact your net revenue as a property owner. Finally, after vetting each company’s property management fee and service structures, you can compile your results in a list or spreadsheet. This will help you to cross-compare each company and weigh up their pros and cons, before deciding on the company that best suits your needs.</p>
