<p>You may not be aware, but Airbnb, Vrbo, and Booking.com are now using AI to determine how photos are displayed on listings on their sites. </p>
</p>
<p>Until now, the host has chosen the most enticing photo of their property, and they have selected this image as their &#8216;hero&#8217; image. That became their lead image. The host also determined the order of the remaining photos, often choosing to order them as a walkthrough of the property, as if you were entering the rental at the front door and taking a tour.</p>
</p>
<p>Not anymore.</p>
</p>
<p>Up until now, that lead photo was your brand. Quite literally. People who have seen your lead photo, the one you&#8217;ve had for years, could recognize your listing. Now, potential guests may not see the same lead photo from one visit to the next. </p>
</p>
<p>They could even recommend a property to a family member or friend because the place has a great view, but when the friend opens up the link, they may be presented with a photo of a third bedroom, a kitchen, or even a toilet.</p>
</p>
<p>Even if they have your place on a trip list and they made a mental note of, that pink house with a big lawn, who&#8217;s to say that&#8217;s what will show on their next visit?</p>
</p>

<p>Laurie,My name is John, with the Executive Customer Relations team. I hope this message finds you well. I&#8217;m reaching out to provide important information regarding recent changes to the display of property photos on Vrbo.</p>
</p>
<p>As part of our commitment to enhancing the traveler experience, Vrbo has implemented a new feature utilizing machine learning to dynamically select both the hero image and the order of all photos on live property sites. This means that the sequence of photos displayed to travelers may vary based on how each photo performs, rather than adhering strictly to the partner&#8217;s upload preference (e.g., photo1, photo2, etc.).</p>
</p>
<p>Additionally, part of this machine-learning feature is that different travelers may see a different primary photo displayed for the same property. Please note that there is no way to opt out of this feature or manually override the primary image selection.</p>
</p>
<p>We understand that this represents a significant change, and we want to assure you that our decision-making process for primary image selection is based on extensive research and ongoing tests. While this may not always align with the preferred primary image indicated in the dashboard, our machine learning algorithm is designed to optimize booking conversion rates by selecting images that resonate most effectively with travelers.</p>
</p>
<p>Factors such as image composition, content, traveler preferences, and subtle cues are all taken into account in this process. Please be aware that we are continuously testing and refining our selection and ranking models to best represent individual properties.</p>
</p>
<p>It&#8217;s important to note that this change is being gradually introduced to our platform, with a portion of traffic currently being exposed to the new feature. If manually selected orders perform better than our algorithm, adjustments will be made accordingly.</p>
</p>
<p>We understand that this change may raise concerns, and we want to assure you that your feedback is invaluable to us. We are more than happy to forward any feedback you may have regarding the new AI photo selection feature to our internal channels for consideration.</p>
</p>
<p>Thank you for your understanding and patience as we continue to strive for improvements that benefit both our partners and travelers alike.If you have any further feedback about the AI photo selection, please don&#8217;t hesitate to reach out.</p>
