<p>But did you know, the perceived negativity surrounding these bookings comes at a cost to your business? </p>
<p>How? Here are 3 examples:</p>
<p>1. <em>You remove your vacation rental listings until after spring break.</em></p>
<p>In this scenario, you <strong>miss out on revenue</strong> for an entire month! Not only that, but this can <strong>damage your marketing efforts</strong> and set you back a month behind your competitors.</p>
<p><em>2. You cancel bookings last minute without evidence to support your reasoning</em>.</p>
<p>In this scenario, <strong>you lose both revenue and trust</strong>. Gut intuition isn’t enough to effectively screen bookings for troublesome guests. <strong>If you’re using Airbnb as your booking platform, you can also lose your superhost status.</strong> </p>
<p>3. <em>You host during spring break without any risk-preventative measures in place.</em></p>
<p>In this scenario, you can be subject to <strong>damage, fraud, parties and the hefty costs they can incur.</strong></p>
<p>It sounds like a lose-lose situation: the short-term rental industry’s conditioned to believe hosting spring breakers is a disaster waiting to happen. It’s a hard no for many.</p>
<p>Yet, this doesn’t have to be the case. </p>
<p>Spring break bookings are <strong><em>not </em></strong>all partiers looking for a booze-fuelled holiday. </p>
<p>Travellers from all niches and markets look to short-term rentals to book that last-minute spring break vacation. But last-minute bookings are not always made by bad guests. </p>
<p>In fact, they could be the best guest your listing has seen.</p>
<p><strong>It’s important to remember, that as a property manager, you are in the business of making memories.</strong> Spring break is the perfect getaway opportunity for many. You need to embrace that.</p>

<h3><strong><span>1. Optimisation through your listing description and pricing strategy</span></strong></h3>
<p>Spring break is a popular holiday for many Americans. It’s important you’re not missing out on the scaleable revenue it has to offer.</p>
<p>Optimising your listing description with the amenities on offer will allow you to target your ideal customer. During spring break, small adjustments and tweaks to parts of your listing, including the <strong>listing title, pricing and even how many your property can sleep, will help strategise your rental property to your ideal guest, maximise your profits and avoid problematic bookings.</strong></p>
<p><em>Remember, you can always edit listing descriptions and you should regularly look to optimise this throughout the year as part of your </em><a href="https://superhog.com/5-marketing-tips-for-direct-booking-websites/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener"><em>marketing</em></a><em> strategy.</em></p>
<h3><span>2. Revise your house rules and implement a signed guest agreement</span></h3>
<p>House rules set guest expectations for their stay, as well as the host’s expectations of the guest’s behaviour prior to booking. </p>
<p>Revising house rules allows you as a property manager to adapt to changing circumstances. Ensure you’re <strong>adding in specific quiet times</strong> and remind guests you do not permit parties: e.g <strong>‘no parties of any kind’. </strong></p>
<p>Reiteration is key, use the rule of three. </p>
<p>Places to enclose your house rules include:</p>
<ul>
<li>in your listing description, </li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>within your guest agreement </li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>re-send the guests the house rules alongside their check-in details</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>A signed guest agreement confirms the guest accepts your rules. </strong>It gives you a legal framework in the instance your guest breaks said rules, which then results in damage, or other misconduct, affecting your business/reputation.</p>

<h3><span>3. Clear communication with your guests throughout the booking journey</span></h3>
<p>It goes without saying that <strong>clear communication enhances the guest experience.</strong> To help you host safely during spring break 2023 and avoid bad guests, ensure your communication can not be faulted. </p>
<p><strong>Provide enough communication to insert your ground rules and expectations, but respect boundaries and your guest’s privacy too</strong>. Make them feel confident in you as a host and provide them with a hospitable stay. Guests feel trusted by hosts when communication is clear. This helps build respect between both parties. </p>
<p><strong>Remember to keep records of all your communication with guests. </strong>If something does go wrong during the booking, your communication is a great pool of evidence, that will enable you to deal with the situation with a professional demeanour. It also allows you to reach a quicker, more efficient and more accurate resolution for all parties involved.</p>
<h3><span>4. Collect damage deposits or damage waivers from all guests before their booking begins</span></h3>
<p>Reduce the probability of spring break parties by asking for a damage deposit from your guest. Before any booking, <strong>this should be standard practice before guests enter your property.</strong> </p>
