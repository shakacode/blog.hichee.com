<h2 id="need-insurance">Why do you need insurance for short-term rentals?</h2>
<p>Let’s ask you this:</p>
<p><em>“What business would lend a stranger hundreds of thousands (or millions) of dollars worth of assets, no questions asked?”  </em></p>
<p>They wouldn’t.</p>
<p>Yet, in the vacation rental industry, property managers and owners do this every single day. But, why?  </p>
<p>As a short-term rental property manager, host or owner, <strong>you’re renting out your property to a high turnover of unknown guests on a regular basis.</strong> This <strong>increases your chances of potential damage, liability and even the probability of being sued</strong>, which is why as a host, insurance is a no-brainer. </p>
<p>It’s important to note that although it is <strong>not yet a legal requirement</strong> to obtain proper insurance when operating a home rental in the majority of countries and US states, it is highly advisable that insurance is put in place. </p>
<p>The tightening of home-sharing regulations will no doubt bring specific short-term rental insurance requirements, so it’s a good time to ensure you have the right coverage.</p>

<h3>So, what exactly is short-term rental insurance?</h3>
<p><strong>Accidents happen. Damages occur. Business revenue can be lost. </strong></p>
<p>Similar to other industries, whereby protection and premiums are put in place to cover damage and loss of assets – such as equipment rentals, and car hires – the short-term rental industry is no different. </p>
<p>As your rental property is a valuable asset and resource that holds economic value to you and your business, you need assurances in place from the get-go. </p>
<p><strong>Short-term rental insurance is, therefore, a method of managing risks</strong>: <strong>protecting you from the unexpected.</strong> </p>
<p>It offers <strong>financial protection</strong> in the case of an incident when renting out to guests, specifically on a short-term basis.</p>
<h3>How to classify ‘short-term basis’?</h3>
<p>Most notably, short-term rentals are furnished, self-contained accommodations rented for a short period of time and seen as an alternative to hotels. </p>
<p>Typically, this is classified as <strong>less than six months</strong>; however, in the rapidly booming sharing economy, a property rented<strong> between one night to a few weeks</strong> is considered to be a short-term rental.</p>
<p>All the more reason you need home rental insurance.</p>

<h3 id="homeowner-cover-rental">Does homeowners insurance cover short-term rentals?</h3>
<p>Don’t assume your insurance policy covers you and you do not need to do anything. </p>
<p><strong>Traditionally, standard landlord and homeowners insurance does not cover short-term rentals and holiday lets, </strong>nor do they provide the proper levels of coverage you need to lease out your property on a short-term basis. </p>
<p>So, getting in touch with your provider and knowing where you stand is important.</p>
<h4><span><strong><em>Homeowner insurance</em></strong> </span></h4>
<p>This is typically designed for the homeowner occupying the property. </p>
<h4><strong><em><span>Landlord policies</span></em></strong></h4>
<p>Are designed for a tenant occupying the property.</p>
<h4><strong><em><span>Vacation rentals</span></em></strong></h4>
<p>A vacation rental is unique in how it operates. It will typically see<strong> three uses</strong>:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Guest stays:</strong> rented out to strangers on a short-term basis, including one-night stays to a few weeks</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li><strong>Owner vacations</strong>: the owner of the property will vacation in the property at certain times in the year or offer the property to friends and family</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li><strong>Unoccupied periods:</strong> the property will remain empty (unoccupied by guests or the owner) at certain times in the year</li>
</ul>
<p>Not declaring the true operation of your property to your insurer is not an option. <strong>This can invalidate your policy.</strong> </p>
<p>You will be unable to make a claim when an accident arises due to a guest’s actions, and your insurer will likely revoke your insurance policy – affecting your business and ability to find a new insurance policy provider.</p>
<h4><strong><em><span>Are there homeowner policies that cover short-term rentals?</span></em></strong></h4>
<p>Increasingly, we are seeing more and more property insurers allowing owners to engage in the short-term rental-sharing economy without invalidating their policies. This is excellent news! </p>
<p>However, there are still significant coverage gaps within these policies, so it’s important to fully understand the T’s &#038; C’s that come with this. The coverage may not be the right fit for you. </p>
<p><strong>Remember, insurance is not one size fits all, so if you have multiple properties in different regions, states or even countries, it’s even more important to check where you’re covered.</strong> </p>

<h3>What is short-term rental damage protection?</h3>
<p><strong>Damage protection is a guarantee</strong> or feature that can be applied to your rental property as an added layer of protection <strong>against accidental guest damages</strong> that may occur during their booking.</p>
<h3>Home insurance vs damage protection</h3>
<p><strong>Short-term rental damage protection</strong> can help bridge the coverage gap of standard home insurance, but it is important to note that it <strong>is not a substitute for homeowner or landlord’s insurance policies. </strong></p>
<p>By ensuring your rental property has both insurance and damage protection, you are <strong>building an effective multi-faceted risk management strategy.</strong> The additional layers of protection will allow you to host with confidence and peace of mind that you are covered from all angles, protecting you from the unexpected.</p>
