<p><span>Having a love for travel since I was very young, I was always interested in working in the travel sector one way or another, and had always wondered what – Life As A Travel Consultant – must be like.</span></p>
<p>One of my first real jobs after school was having the opportunity to be a long distance 🚍 Charter Bus Driver.</p>
<p>What a great job that was at the time, driving people on vacation all around North America, to many popular vacation destinations. </p>
<p><span>Even though my career ended up in a completely different field, I still managed to put myself through a prestigious travel college, becoming a licensed travel agent. I had hoped to work as a consultant either part time or once retired.</span></p>
<p>I did end up working as a part time home based consultant for a short time, with a major 🛳 cruise vacation company, but quickly discovered this was not an easy task working in this manner.</p>
<h3><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Working_As_A_Travel_Agent_and_Blogging_For_A_Living"></span>Working As A Travel Agent and Blogging For A Living<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>

<p><span>After working in this field part time for a short while, I then decided to focus my efforts as an amateur {travel blogger}, instead of trying to make a few bucks as a part time consultant.</span></p>
<p>This was definitely the right move for me!</p>
<p>But it also left me with a greater appreciation for travel agents and consultants out there. Working hard each and every day with their clients, putting together the best travel and vacation packages for their desires. </p>
<p><a href="https://gr8traveltips.com/best-tourist-attractions-in-bangkok-thailand/great-travel-deals/" rel="attachment wp-att-8128"><img data-lazyloaded="1" src="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/Great-Travel-Deals-1024x341-1.jpg" decoding="async" loading="lazy" alt="Great Travel Deals" width="600" height="200" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-8128" data-sizes="(max-width: 600px) 100vw, 600px" srcset="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/Great-Travel-Deals-1024x341-1.jpg 1024w, https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/Great-Travel-Deals-300x100-1.jpg 300w, https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/Great-Travel-Deals-900x300-1.jpg 900w, https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/Great-Travel-Deals.jpg 1080w" /></a></p>
<p><span>It’s very hard work that many people out there don’t appreciate, which gave me the idea of putting together this post interviewing actual travel consultants on what life as a travel consultant is really like. </span></p>
<p><span>Below are a few travel agents and bloggers I contacted, that were happy to contribute to this post and tell others what it’s actually like to work as a – travel consultant.</span></p>
<h3><span class="ez-toc-section" id="The_Travel_Lady"></span>The Travel Lady<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>
<p><strong>Life As A Travel Consultant #1</strong></p>
<p><a href="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/03/Jordan-and-LMK-at-Global.jpg" data-rel="lightbox-image-2" data-rl_title data-rl_caption><img data-lazyloaded="1" src="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/03/Jordan-and-LMK-at-Global-600x434-1.jpg" decoding="async" loading="lazy" class="aligncenter wp-image-22659 size-large" title alt="Jordan and LMK at Global" width="600" height="434" data-sizes="(max-width: 600px) 100vw, 600px" srcset="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/03/Jordan-and-LMK-at-Global.jpg 600w, https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/03/Jordan-and-LMK-at-Global-300x217-1.jpg 300w" /></a></p>
<p><b>1 – What Motivated You To Become A Travel Consultant?</b></p>
<p>The love of travel and the desire to be an entrepreneur. As a new immigrant to Canada I wanted a change and decided to start my own business as a franchisee with UNIGLOBE.</p>
<p><b>2 – What Type Of Training Did You Undergo To Become A Consultant?</b></p>
<p>I had done some online courses but nothing prepared me for the actual task of being a travel consultant. The mysterious world of the GDS was a new language of $BB and Pillow and Colon.</p>
<p>I was told as owner of the franchise that I should leave the travel booking side of things to my agents and not learn how to sell or book travel. I thought that sounded pretty stupid and set out to learn the business from the bottom up.</p>
<p><b>3 – Why Should People Book Their Travels With A Professional Travel Consultant, Instead Of Booking Online On Their Own?</b></p>
<p>There are a lot of travel options that people can book on their own these days. A flight from A to B or a week long package in Mexico. That’s pretty basic stuff. We decided that in order to stand out from the competition of online travel agencies we would narrow down what we do and offer niche services to destinations that we actually visit.</p>
<p>As result we no long sell all inclusive packages to Mexico and the Caribbean, nothing into North America like Disney, no air only. Instead we focus on customized vacations and travel to exotic destinations such as Africa, South America, South Pacific and Europe.</p>
<p>Places our team has actually visited.</p>
<p>We are also cruise experts with our team having sailed on many different ocean and river cruises. First hand experience is what the consumer is looking for these days. As we have a lot of repeat customers we get to know what they like – and what they don’t – so we are able to offer valuable advice and suggestions.</p>
<p><b>4 – Since Becoming A Travel Consultant, What Is The Biggest Change You’ve Noticed In Your Profession In Recent Years?</b></p>
<p>The advent of Independent Contractors. Some of the independent contractors are seasoned professionals who have decided to work independently through a host agency. Sadly however there seems to be a trend for large host agencies to recruit anyone who would like to be a “travel agent”.</p>
<p>In the industry we call them “hobbyists” and the feeling is that they have become a “travel agent” so that they can go on (fams) and get agents rates. Some feel that this takes away from the professionalism of our industry. It is an ongoing debate.</p>
<p><b>5 – What Do You Like Most About Your Job?</b></p>
<p>Every day is different – never boring and let’s face it – we are dealing with a sexy product. It’s travel! It’s exciting.</p>
<p><b>6 – What Do You Find The Most Frustrating Aspect Of Your Job?</b></p>
<p>Probably dealing with the airlines. Many customer issues seem to revolve around delayed flights, seat selection, schedule changes and more. The airlines are so big now that the little people don’t seem to count. I have noticed as well that it is always really easy to blame the travel agent.</p>
<p>This actually happened to me at the check in desk for one of my flights when something had gone wrong. The agent said “well you need to speak to your travel agent about this” – LOL – I told her “I am the travel agent so let’s talk”.</p>
<p>She had nothing for me!</p>
<p><b>7 – What Has Been Your Most Memorable Moment Working As A Travel Consultant?</b></p>
<p>There are so many. My first round the world cruise – always a goody! – and contrasting – A young woman and her brother who traveled to Tahiti because she had been diagnosed with a terminal illness and this was her dream. We all cried.</p>
<p><b>8 – Where Do You Most Love To Travel To?</b></p>
<p>Wow – another hard question. Every place has it’s special thing! Having grown up in Southern Africa I do love the bush as we call it there and have had many adventures there. Sleeping in a tent with a pack of hyenas having a huge fight very close to us, hearing the roar of a rogue lion in the night and watching those amazing African sunsets.</p>
<p><b>9 – What Are The Key Ingredients Needed In Order To Become A Successful Travel Consultant In This Competitive Field?</b></p>
<p>Good sales technique, ability to assess people, intuition and attention to detail – plus of course authentic passion for travel.</p>
<p><a href="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/03/Fishermans-island-lunch.jpg" data-rel="lightbox-image-3" data-rl_title data-rl_caption><img data-lazyloaded="1" src="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/03/Fishermans-island-lunch-600x461-1.jpg" decoding="async" loading="lazy" class="aligncenter wp-image-22660 size-large" title alt="Fishermans island lunch" width="600" height="461" data-sizes="(max-width: 600px) 100vw, 600px" srcset="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/03/Fishermans-island-lunch.jpg 600w, https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/03/Fishermans-island-lunch-300x231-1.jpg 300w" /></a></p>
<p><b>BIO:</b><br />Born in England, grew up in South Africa – now a proud Canadian. I have been lucky enough to visit some amazing places all over the world – Tanzania, Morocco, Egypt, most of Europe, South America, Tahiti, Cook Island, Fiji, Australia and New Zealand. Some of my favorite places were India, Cambodia and Vietnam and the back roads of Ireland.</p>
<p>I have slept under a tent in the Sahara Desert and spent Earth Hour under the stars in the Serengeti. When I first arrived in Calgary I was invited by Global TV to present travel ideas to Calgary’s viewers each week on the Morning News.</p>
<p>That was over 20 years ago and I still love appearing each week. I plan never to retire – I am having too much fun.  You can visit Lesley’s Blog at; <a href="http://www.thetravellady.ca" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow"><em>The Travel Lady</em></a>.</p>

<h3><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Sonia_Jones_Travel"></span>Sonia Jones Travel<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>
<p><strong>Life As A Travel Consultant #2</strong></p>
<p><a href="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/03/Sonia-Jones-Travel.jpg" data-rel="lightbox-image-5" data-rl_title data-rl_caption><img data-lazyloaded="1" src="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/03/Sonia-Jones-Travel-600x375-1.jpg" decoding="async" loading="lazy" class="aligncenter wp-image-22662 size-large" title alt="Sonia Jones Travel" width="600" height="375" data-sizes="(max-width: 600px) 100vw, 600px" srcset="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/03/Sonia-Jones-Travel.jpg 600w, https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/03/Sonia-Jones-Travel-300x188-1.jpg 300w" /></a></p>
<p><b>1 – What Motivated You To Become A Travel Consultant?</b></p>
<p>After I graduated from university, I went travelling for two years. I was on the last leg of my trip and I started looking at jobs, but really couldn’t find anything that appealed to me. I then came across an ad for Flight Centre and I thought, “That’s it!”</p>
<p>I applied and the rest is history. That was sixteen years ago!</p>
<p><b>2 – What Type Of Training Did You Undergo To Become A Consultant?</b></p>
<p>I was trained on the job. Flight Centre Travel Group in Australia has its own in-house training program. The version of training that I went through is vastly different to today’s course, but essentially I learned everything from the airline booking system to sales techniques while on the job.</p>
<p>It was a very steep learning curve and the first sixth months was tough, but one day it all clicked.</p>
<p><b>3 – Why Should People Book Their Travels With A Professional Travel Consultant Instead Of Booking Online On Their Own?</b></p>
<p>If I want something done properly, I go to a professional. I don’t try to colour or cut my own hair – I go to my hairdresser. I don’t fix my own blocked drain – I call a plumber. I believe the same is true for travel – if you want to try and do it yourself, you can, but wouldn’t you rather a professional handle your arrangements for you?</p>
<p>We spend every single day looking at hotels, researching cruise lines, booking airfares and uncovering hidden gems to make your next trip even more special. We have contacts across the globe who can help us to plan the perfect holiday for you at the best rates.</p>
<p>Through my affiliation with networks such as Virtuoso, my clients often receive complimentary amenities like breakfast, room upgrades, hotel credits, spa treatments and late check out.</p>
<p>You can’t get those if you book directly with these hotels.</p>
<p><b>4 – Since Becoming A Travel Consultant, What Is The Biggest Change You’ve Noticed In Your Profession In Recent Years?</b></p>
<p>The biggest change I have noticed in travel is around pricing. When I started out in 2002, prices were static and consumers couldn’t really make bookings or access better pricing themselves. As the internet grew and more hotels, cruises and airfares came online, pricing became more competitive, but also more transparent.</p>
<p>It is a myth that booking through a travel advisor will cost more than booking yourself. How could we survive as a profession if we knew our rates were always dearer? I always send my clients the hotel or cruise line website when I quote them anything, and there are a number of times clients tell me my rate is in fact lower than what they could find for themselves.</p>
<p>My clients trust me to find them great value and extra inclusions wherever possible.</p>
<p><b>5 – What Do You Like Most About Your Job?</b></p>
<p>I love the planning phase of a trip and then seeing it come to life. When a client sends me a photo of their view from their room, or the lion they saw on safari, or the smiles on their children’s faces with their favorite character at Disneyland, then it makes all the hours of planning and carefully crafting their journey worth it.<br />Hearing them recount what a perfect trip they had brings me so much joy! Travel is an emotional experience and I love being part of those “ah-ha” moments with my clients.</p>
<p><b>6 – What Do You Find The Most Frustrating Aspect Of Your Job?</b></p>
<p>So many times we are caught in the middle of something that is completely not our fault, and yet it is our responsibility to find a resolution for the client then and there. A flight is cancelled; the private transfer doesn’t arrive; severe weather strikes; the hotel has promised twin beds but cannot provide them for the guest – you get the idea.</p>
<p>It is incredibly frustrating, however as the advisor, I have to find a solution.</p>
<p>Looking after my client is my number one priority. I’m the security blanket and the back-up should anything go wrong. There’s a saying, “Without a Travel Advisor – You Are On Your Own.”</p>
<p><b>7 – What Has Been Your Most Memorable Moment Working As A Travel Consultant?</b></p>
<p>There have been so many wonderful moments during my career as a travel advisor, but one of the most memorable was making a trip of a lifetime come true for an elderly client. She had never traveled overseas, but she had dream of seeing the Great Wall of China. I put a simple, but very special trip together for her, her daughter and her granddaughter, flying them to Beijing and arranging their stay at the iconic Aman Summer Palace.</p>
<p>They made it to the Great Wall – not an easy feat for someone with various health and mobility challenges – and the three women stood at the top together. I felt like I was there with them at that moment!</p>
<p><b>8 – Where Do You Most Love To Travel To?</b></p>
<p>It’s like asking me if I have a favorite child! I love travel, full stop. I love exploring somewhere new, but I also love revisiting a city and discovering something different about it. If I had to pick one place though, it would have to be Japan. A little piece of me lives there, and I struggle to leave at the end of each trip.</p>
<p>I love the culture, the food, the people and the diversity that Japan offers. It is a unique and truly special part of the world.</p>
<p><b>9 – What Are The Key Ingredients Needed In Order To Become A Successful Travel Consultant In This Competitive Field?</b></p>
<p>I believe that the key to being a successful travel advisor is to find your point of difference. It may be that you specialize in a particular destination, or it may be that you are a guru for a certain type of travel. Find that point of difference, drill down on it and be the best that you can be.</p>
<p>Spend time (and money) on research, training and experiencing your chosen area. Knock your competitors out of the park and make your unique selling point known. People will come looking for you and will want to work with you because you have proven yourself a specialist in that area.</p>
<p>Remember that having a niche or area of specialization doesn’t mean you are pigeon-holing yourself – but it will be why people know of you and want to be part of the journey with you!</p>

<h3><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Milk_and_Honey_Travels"></span>Milk and Honey Travels<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>
<p><strong>Life As A Travel Consultant #3</strong></p>
<p><a href="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/03/Erica-Mendenhall-Photography.jpg" data-rel="lightbox-image-8" data-rl_title data-rl_caption><img data-lazyloaded="1" src="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/03/Erica-Mendenhall-Photography-600x472-1.jpg" decoding="async" loading="lazy" class="aligncenter wp-image-22668 size-large" title alt="Erica Mendenhall Photography" width="600" height="472" data-sizes="(max-width: 600px) 100vw, 600px" srcset="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/03/Erica-Mendenhall-Photography.jpg 600w, https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/03/Erica-Mendenhall-Photography-300x236-1.jpg 300w" /></a></p>
<p><b>1 – What Motivated You To Become A Travel Consultant?</b></p>
<p>I was interested in being an entrepreneur first and foremost. I wanted to be my own boss, do something I was passionate about, and create a certain lifestyle — preferably one that allowed me to work from anywhere. I was obviously passionate about travel on a personal level, and I also loved helping people plan their own trips.</p>
<p>It was just a matter of figuring out how to make that a career.</p>
<p><b>2 – What Type Of Training Did You Undergo To Become A Consultant?</b></p>
<p>Becoming a consultant actually doesn’t take much professional training, but you should seek out all different types of training if you want to be successful. I started by working as an independent contractor and joining a host agency that offered a lot of help in the form of webinars and tutorials for people new to the industry. I also did online training from a small business perspective.</p>
<p>The most helpful was a program called B-School.</p>
<p><b>3 – Why Should People Book Their Travels With A Professional Travel Consultant, Instead Of Booking Online On Their Own?</b></p>
<p>Working with a consultant adds an important personal touch to your travels, while also providing you with a knowledgeable professional that will help guide you in creating the type of experience you are looking for. Booking online is simply where you go to make a transaction, and there is so much information out there, it’s often hard to know if you are making the best transaction you could be.</p>
<p>A professional travel consultant listens to what you are looking for, uses their personal expertise and extensive resource of contacts around the globe, and provides a seamless travel experience where your only responsibility is to show up and enjoy it.</p>
<p>That seems like a pretty sweet deal to me!</p>
<p><b>4 – Since Becoming A Travel Consultant, What Is The Biggest Change You’ve Noticed In Your Profession In Recent Years?</b></p>
<p>People specializing in certain types of travel and becoming niche experts is really popular. I also think that there is a younger generation starting to use Travel Advisors because what is most important for them are experiences that are out of the box, and unique — things you can’t necessarily google.</p>
<p><b>5 – What Do You Like Most About Your Job?</b></p>
<p>I love helping people’s dreams come true. I see each trip I plan as my gift to the special people who are traveling. These trips are some of their most precious memories, and it’s an honor to play a part in that, and something I don’t take lightly. When I get to follow along on social media while they are traveling and see them share all of their special moments, it makes me love what I do even more.</p>
<p><b>6 – What Do You Find The Most Frustrating Aspect Of Your Job?</b></p>
<p>Hold times when you call the airlines. 🙂</p>
<p><b>7 – What Has Been Your Most Memorable Moment Working As A Travel Consultant?</b></p>
<p>I was able to work on a special trip to the Olympics in Rio de Janeiro a few years ago and that was really special for me because of my background as a professional track and field athlete.</p>
<p>It was a full circle moment to help plan a trip like that!</p>
<p><b>8 – Where Do You Most Love To Travel To?</b></p>
<p>Bali is my favorite place in the world and I love introducing it to people and traveling there myself. I just returned from my last trip about a month ago.</p>
<p><b>9 – What Are The Key Ingredients Needed In Order To Become A Successful Travel Consultant In This Competitive Field?</b></p>
<p>A love for travel is a must. I think another key ingredient is really understanding the clients you hope to work with because it’s about making their dreams come to life. I am a big believer in personal branding because it’s the foundation of my business success. <p><b>1 – What Motivated You To Become A Travel Consultant?</b></p>

<p>That summer and from that point forward, I did not take on any more wedding photography jobs and I spent my summer in Sosua, Dominican Republic learning Spanish and soul-searching. <p>In the end, I decided on a complete career change, going from being a full-time, successful professional photographer running my own business, to a 9 to 5  poorly paid, but stable office job. <p><b>2 – What Type Of Training Did You Undergo To Become A Consultant?</b></p>
<p>For me, all of my training was hands-on.  From having experience traveling to many countries, to learning the computer and software systems used for working in the industry.  I did weeks of online courses and in-person training to learn to use Amadeus (an industry flight-booking system) and various internal training sessions for other programs specific to the office I was working in.</p>
<p>On top of that, I did product training for everything from cruises, all-inclusive to adventure travel, depending on what was being offered online and in Halifax at the time.</p>

<p><b>3 – Why Should People Book Their Travels With A Professional Travel Consultant, Instead Of Booking Online On Their Own?</b></p>
<p>Booking with a travel agent is all about saving time and receiving extra value for your vacation. What most people don’t realize about travel consultants is that they are able to talk with you, get to know your likes and dislikes and help narrow down a trip for you within a 20 – 30 minute phone conversation, a few emails or even over coffee.</p>
<p>They can help you make the best decisions for your vacation and avoid amateur mistakes.</p>
<p>They often already know which destinations have the best weather at a specific time of year, when major festivals take place, if you want to attend them or avoid the busy seasons.  If not, they have resources at their fingertips without sorting through various advertisements and unreliable resources on the internet.</p>
<p>Not to mention their professional connections with local suppliers all over the world.</p>
<p>They know about Skip the Line tickets, how to get them and why they are so important. They know that traveling during rainy season can be amazing. They know the benefits and downfalls of cruising vs adventure cruising, coach-bus tours vs small group tours, camping in the Serengeti vs glamping!</p>
<p>Travel Consultants have a vast knowledge of different styles of travel and can educate you to the differences and what to expect. Plus they can offer up the best ideas for places that you should check out, not to mention personal recommendations from their own travels.</p>
<p>While Google can tell you the top five trending places for travel next year, your travel consultant knows which ones you’ve been to and which ones will suit your interests, budget and feed your worldly curiosity.</p>
<p>On top of all of that, travel consultants also have access to discounts and value added products and services that are not always advertised online, while still being able to get most of the same prices that you find online. In the end, booking with a travel consultant saves you time, gets you reliable, first-hand information, and peace of mind while you are traveling.</p>
<p>Often times, they can get you extra perks like on board credits, free breakfast, included meal vouchers, free transfers and more!</p>
<p>Perhaps the most important and valuable reason for booking with a travel consultant is that they have your back if anything goes wrong on your trip.</p>
<p>Flights cancelled? No problem – you go enjoy the beach while your travel consultant contacts the airline, gets you on a different flight and notifies your upcoming transfer and hotel that you’ll be arriving late.</p>
<p>Family emergency? No problem, your travel consultant will help you make the arrangements necessary to get home as quickly as possible and give you advice about claiming through travel insurance.</p>
<p>Why wouldn’t you want all of this!?</p>
