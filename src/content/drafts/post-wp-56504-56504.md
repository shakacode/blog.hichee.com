<p><span data-color="transparent">If you want to generate a retirement income from rental </span><a href="https://www.igms.com/airbnb-property-to-buy/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><span data-color="transparent"><u>property investments</u></span></a><span data-color="transparent">, Airbnb is a very feasible option. <p><span data-color="transparent">Whatever</span> rental houses or real estate investing<span data-color="transparent"> option you choose, one question remains. How many</span> rental <span data-color="transparent">properties do you need to own if you want to retire?</span></p>
<p><span data-color="transparent">Here’s everything you need to know about relying</span> cash on cash return from<span data-color="transparent"> your rental properties to keep your</span> monthly cash flow<span data-color="transparent"> in good shape.</span></p>
<h2><strong><span data-color="transparent">How Many Rental Properties Do You Need To Retire? </span></strong></h2>
<p> <img decoding="async" src="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/67342764-72eb-4db0-bfa8-9d017121f949.png" alt="Rental Property Retirement Formula: find out how many investment properties you need" width="700" height="462" /> </p>
<p><span data-color="transparent">According to </span><a href="https://www.usatoday.com/story/money/personalfinance/2023/08/02/how-much-money-americans-need-for-retirement/70507274007/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><span data-color="transparent"><u>recent studies</u></span></a><span data-color="transparent">, the average US citizen needs $1.8 million to retire comfortably at the age of 64. However, the real answer to this question depends on who is asking. Many factors are at play, such as health status, region, living preferences, and how many dependents you have. </span></p>
<p><span data-color="transparent">Other sources suggest that US retirees need </span><a href="https://www.aarp.org/retirement/planning-for-retirement/info-2020/how-much-money-do-you-need-to-retire.html" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><span data-color="transparent"><u>80 to 90% of their yearly income</u></span></a><span data-color="transparent"> before quitting work, or 12 times whatever they used to make annually. </span></p>
<p><span data-color="transparent">Unsurprisingly, the prospect of generating such a high level of cash flow can be daunting for those approaching retirement age, especially in the midst of rising inflation and the economic flux that we’ve faced over the past few years. But this is why owning rental properties make such worthwhile investments— they’re always in demand and can provide consistent passive income. </span></p>
<p><span data-color="transparent">So, the answer to the question of how many rental properties you need to retire really depends on how much each rental property is worth, how much rental income you can generate, and how much income you desire. </span></p>
<p><span data-color="transparent">The answer to this question also depends on how much your active income is, i.e., the money you earn directly from your current job as opposed to the passive income you can earn through rental properties. Your spending habits also come into the equation, as they’ll dictate your real estate retirement income requirements in the long term.  </span></p>
<p><span data-color="transparent">When it comes to figuring out how many rental properties you need to retire, there’s a simple rental property retirement formula you can use in rental real estate. </span></p>
<p><strong>Rental Property Retirement Formula:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li><strong><span data-color="transparent">The monthly amount you need for retirement ÷ monthly cash flow per rental property = the number of rental properties you need in order to comfortably retire </span></strong></li>
<li><strong><span data-color="transparent">Cash flow = monthly rental income – mortgage and operating expenses </span></strong></li>
</ul>
<p><span data-color="transparent">In this rental property retirement formula in real estate, your main source of income is assumed to be rent from tenants. Your list of operating expenses should include things like maintenance costs, vacancies, interest rates, mortgage payment, and property taxes. </span></p>
<p><span data-color="transparent">You could either invest in multiple smaller rental properties that compound and create multiple rental income streams, or you could invest in just one piece of prime real estate that brings in enough cash flow on its own to subsidize your annual salary and help you prepare for a comfortable, sustainable retirement. </span></p>
<h2><strong><span data-color="transparent">Why Rental Properties Are The Key To Financial Freedom</span></strong></h2>
<p><span data-color="transparent">A rental property is widely considered the most financially advantageous and risk-averse investment type in real estate. Compared to high-risk investments such as cryptocurrency or hedge funds, rental property is a relatively stable market, making it one of the best assets to invest in for long-term wealth generation and, in this case, retirement. </span></p>
<p><span data-color="transparent">A few rental properties not only provide the owner with the safety net of self-owned housing, but they also create the potential for generating monthly income. </span></p>
<p><span data-color="transparent">In other words, you can either choose to live in the rental property you invest in or rent it out to tenants for a profit. With</span> the number of rental properties, both property management<span data-color="transparent"> and rental investments, you save money or you earn it. It’s a win-win either way.</span></p>

<p><span data-color="transparent">Investing in a rental property may require substantial</span> initial investment of<span data-color="transparent"> capital, but it’s vastly more profitable and more predictable as a source of passive retirement income in the long run. <h2><strong><span data-color="transparent">The Perks Of Passive Income </span></strong></h2>
<p><span data-color="transparent">Even though the prospect of rental property income is arguably most beneficial to retirees or real estate investors who are otherwise physically or mentally unable to work full-time, this is the kind of salary setup that’s advantageous for everyone, regardless of age, career seasonality, or level of labor capability. </span></p>
<p><span data-color="transparent">This is because passive income empowers real estate investors to either focus more on a job they enjoy that perhaps doesn’t pay well or simply expedite their savings in a way that simply isn’t possible on a single salary. By renting out property on the side, you can significantly stabilize and elevate your financial situation. </span></p>
<p><span data-color="transparent">Aside from helping you accumulate more wealth, the monthly retirement income that comes with rental cash flow attracts many other significant benefits worth exploring, such as: </span></p>
<h3><strong><span data-color="transparent">Protects you from financial crisis </span></strong></h3>
<p><span data-color="transparent">Unfortunately, emergencies are a fact of life, and some of them have the potential to cause financial devastation. A big injury, economic stagnance, or a worldwide pandemic all have the power to unleash a financial crisis. However, having a form of passive cash flow can prevent them from doing their worst and ensure you can still cover all of your monthly expenses. </span></p>
<p><span data-color="transparent">While you can’t avoid financial flux altogether, you can certainly prepare for it by boosting your cash flow and diversifying your revenue streams. </span></p>
<h3><strong><span data-color="transparent">Allows for more free time </span></strong></h3>
<p><span data-color="transparent">Time is money, as they say—and when it comes to passive cash flow, you can have both with rental properties. Without the pressure to earn a set active income every month, you can take things easier and indulge in a little more free time to spend however you choose. </span></p>
<p><span data-color="transparent">Whether that means traveling more, spending time with family, going out into nature, or simply working on personal hobbies, more free time is an excellent perk that comes with passive income. </span></p>
<h3><strong><span data-color="transparent">Can decrease stress and burnout</span></strong></h3>
<p><span data-color="transparent">Financial freedom and stability can significantly contribute to lower stress levels and stronger resistance to both physical and emotional burnout. Money matters are a common source of anxiety, but with that extra stream of low-to-no-effort retirement income, such worries can be alleviated or, at the very least, reduced. </span></p>
<p><span data-color="transparent">Retirement is supposed to be something that people look forward to. But with the high financial expectations and needs posed by today’s expensive world, it is often associated with dread. </span></p>
<p><span data-color="transparent">Retirement anxiety affects more than half of the US population, with </span><a href="https://www.cnbc.com/2023/06/13/retirees-biggest-fear-is-outliving-their-assets-research-finds.html" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><span data-color="transparent"><u>over 58% of adults</u></span></a><span data-color="transparent"> admitting that they fear they may outlive their assets. The fear of outliving your assets is a major concern, but with the right rental property investment strategy, those approaching retirement age can relax and look forward to their golden years. </span></p>
<h3><strong><span data-color="transparent">Invest and reinvest in personal business ventures </span></strong></h3>

<h3><strong><span data-color="transparent">Pursue personal hobbies </span></strong></h3>
<p><span data-color="transparent">Having the freedom to pursue personal hobbies is a luxury for most people. And it’s often those with access to more financial resources who can take their passions to the next level. </span></p>
<p><span data-color="transparent">Hobbies like creative writing, painting, hiking, and even gardening all require ample resources to really pursue, which is why passive income can be such a useful tool for developing new skills. By investing in rental property or several rental properties, you can prepare for retirement while committing to personal dreams and goals simultaneously. </span></p>
