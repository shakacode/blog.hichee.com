<p><span>JetBlue and Spirit Airlines announced the termination of their $3.8 billion merger agreement after losing a federal antitrust lawsuit challenging the deal. </span><a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2024/03/04/business/jetblue-spirit-airlines-merger.html"><span>According to the New York Times,</span></a><span> the decision to terminate the agreement is a “big win” for the Biden administration which has sought to limit corporate consolidation. </span></p>
<p><span>The merger was blocked by a federal judge in January for fear it would reduce competition in the airline industry and raise ticket prices for travelers who are in need of more affordable flight pricing. </span><span>The proposal for JetBlue to acquire the low-cost airline came in October of 2022 at which time it was agreed that JetBlue must pay Spirit $70 million plus another $400 million to Spirit’s shareholders if the merger failed. </span></p>

<p><span>NYT reports, “Judge William G. Young of U.S. District Court for the District of Massachusetts, noted that Spirit played a vital role in the market as a low-cost carrier and that travelers would have fewer options if JetBlue absorbed it.” </span><span>If the merger was approved, JetBlue would have become the fifth largest U.S. Airline with around 10 percent of the market share.</span><a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2023/12/05/business/jetblue-spirit-justice-department-trial.html?timespastHighlight=chokshi,AND,spirit?timespastHighlight=chokshi,AND,spirit?timespastHighlight=chokshi,AND,spirit,AND,antitrust"><span> In court, JetBlue’s lawyers argued</span></a><span> the merger would “allow it to better compete with the four large national airlines,” which include American, Delta, Southwest and United. </span></p>
<p><span>In December, </span><a href="https://news.alaskaair.com/newsroom/alaska-airlines-and-hawaiian-airlines-to-combine-expanding-benefits-and-choice-for-travelers-throughout-hawaii-and-the-west-coast/"><span>Alaska Airlines announced a merger plan with Hawaiian Airlines</span></a><span> to “unlock more destinations for consumers and expand choice of critical air services options and access throughout the Pacific region, Continental United States and globally.” The acquisition is worth a whopping $1.9 billion, which would give Alaska around 8% of the market if approved. </span></p>
