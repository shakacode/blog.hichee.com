<div class="MsoNormal">
Airbnb, originally founded as a company pairing transients<br />
and college kids with hard-up property owners and subletters desperate for<br />
cash, is quickly making moves in the multi-billion dollar vacation<br />
rental industry. If they are going to succeed long term, though, they’ll need<br />
to drop their current global marketing approach, which is: DIRT CHEAP.<o:p></o:p></div>
</p>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">Rent from people in<br />
“your property location” from $20/night</i></div>

<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
Vacation rental owners with respectable quality standards<br />
should be asking themselves the questions, “What is the quality of renter that I<br />
can expect, and what am I supporting if I enroll my property in a global campaign enticing<br />
people to rent from $20/night?” These are important questions that vacation<br />
rental owners should be taking very seriously as regulation of short-term rentals<br />
starts to take hold. Unfortunately, Airbnb is beginning to leave a stain<br />
on the home rental industry &#8211; one that may take some time to come clean.</div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<o:p></o:p></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
</div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
“Airbnber’s” tend to be a <i>drastically </i>different crowd (not to be confused with &#8220;trendy&#8221; or &#8220;savvy&#8221;) than traditional vacation rental clientele compromised of families, professionals, and responsible adults. Airbnber’s<br />
tend to use properties hard and frequently “fly under the radar” with their age<br />
and/or group type and size. Not all Airbnb rentals are necessarily bad, but<br />
there is no question that overall there is a significant difference in renter quality,<br />
responsibility, and accountability. Owners or their property managers need to<br />
monitor Airbnb reservations heavily, and face an uphill battle if there are<br />
issues. Airbnb is notorious for favoring renters, and holding them accountable<br />
for unacceptable behavior or damages is <i>extremely<br />
</i>difficult, and in some cases impossible. Permanent residents in areas where<br />
Airbnb’s are prevalent are becoming more and more frustrated, and frankly,<br />
disgusted.<o:p></o:p></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
</div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
</div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
Airbnb is making conscious efforts to improve their reputation<br />
and offerings with the Airbnb Plus product line, a selection of homes verified<br />
for quality, as well as their review system, which allows owners to rate<br />
guests. They have a long way to go, though, before convincing the general<br />
public and regulatory agencies that they are truly a company focused on quality<br />
standards.<o:p></o:p></div>
