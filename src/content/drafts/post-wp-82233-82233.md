<p class="has-lg-font-size">With long nights, cold weather and the post-Christmas blues, January is a contender for the grimmest month in the UK. But it also comes with a fresh batch of annual leave, making it a great time to book an instant getaway. Blue Monday is much less dismal when you have something sunny to look forward to. </p>
<p class="has-lg-font-size">So, in 2024, jet off to one of these destinations that are hot in January to start the year with some much-needed sun. </p>
<figure class="wp-block-table is-style-stripes"></figure>
<h2 class="wp-block-heading has-xl-font-size" id="h-which-destinations-are-hot-in-january">Which destinations are hot in January?</h2>
<p><strong>The UK may be stuck in the depths of winter in January, but the Southern Hemisphere is basking in the peak of summer. </strong>Crossing the equator is a great way to explore a new culture while seeking some glorious summer sun.  </p>
<p>Then there are the Northern Hemisphere destinations closer to the equator, in the Middle East or southern USA, which give some nice January warmth that’s sorely lacking in the UK. And, while the south of Europe isn’t hot, it’s still pretty warm. You can enjoy warm, mild weather in the south of Spain, Portugal and Italy without spending hours in the air.</p>
<p>Need some inspiration? Here are some top destinations that are hot in January. </p>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-css-opacity is-style-wide" />
<h2 class="wp-block-heading has-xl-font-size" id="h-1-dubai-uae">1. Dubai, UAE</h2>
<p><strong>If you love city breaks but still want some sweet winter sun, Dubai is hot in January. </strong>This glitzy Middle Eastern city is famous for its magnificent malls, and during January the bargains are even better thanks to the <a href="https://www.visitdubai.com/en/whats-on/dsf">Dubai Shopping Festival</a>. As well as amazing deals in participating shops, you can also expect pop-ups, live entertainment and bustling outdoor markets. In 2024 the event will run until 14 January. </p>
<p>January is winter in Dubai, but daytime temperatures are a very pleasant 24°C (75°F). This means it’s toasty enough to enjoy outdoors attractions without the stifling heat of summer. Enjoy a sunrise tour of the desert, take a cruise along Dubai Creek or simply sunbathe on the city’s pristine beaches. Not to be missed is an al-fresco meal at the city’s <a href="https://www.timeoutdubai.com/timeout-market-dubai">Time Out food market</a>, where you can pick and choose dishes cooked by some of the city’s best-known chefs.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading has-xl-font-size" id="h-2-guanacaste-costa-rica">2. Guanacaste, Costa Rica</h2>
<p><strong>If you’re looking for outdoor adventure, Guanacaste is hot in January – and the dry season’s weather makes exploring a joy. </strong>This region of Costa Rica combines stunning Pacific beaches with lush tropical forests. Enjoy water sports (or chilling out) on white sand beaches like Playa Conchal and Tamarindo, or head inland to discover rare dry tropical forests.</p>
<p>Adrenaline seekers should enjoy a day trip to the <a href="https://selvatura.com/">Selvatura Adventure Park</a>. Here, you can zipline through the forest canopy or simply take a stroll along the 1.9 mile-long, treetop suspension bridge. Or, head underground at Barra Honda National Park with its intricate network of limestone caverns. Just make sure you go with a professional guide. Barra Honda is also great for hikers, with well-maintained paths and diverse scenery. Where else in the world can you see cacti and capuchin monkeys at the same time?</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading has-xl-font-size" id="h-3-the-maldives">3. The Maldives</h2>
<p><strong>With its tropical climate and low rainfall, the Maldives is a dream come true if you’re looking for heat in January.</strong> There’s plenty of sunshine, so you can make the most of the archipelago’s pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters and luxurious overwater villas. The Maldives is all about relaxation – something we all need after the busy festive period.</p>
<p>January is part of the dry season, so underwater visibility is brilliant. The island’s coral reefs are home to diverse marine life, so strap on a snorkel and get exploring. One of the best places to try is Baa Atoll, a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve. Join an excursion and come face-to-face with seat turtles, manta rays and colourful fish.</p>
<p>The Maldives is also home to the world’s first underwater restaurant. Ithaa Undersea Restaurant sits five metres beneath sea level, and is part of the luxurious <a href="https://www.skyscanner.net/hotels/maldives/maldives-hotels/conrad-maldives-rangali-island/ht-133814441">Conrad Maldives Rangali Island</a> hotel. So, if snorkelling isn’t your thing, you can enjoy watching the tropical fish swim by while tucking into dinner.</p>
