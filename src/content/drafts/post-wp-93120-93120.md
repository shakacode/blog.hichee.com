<p>If you want to snag the best deal on your vacation accommodation, you&#8217;re in the right place. </p>
</p>
<p>With the myriad of booking options on offer, it can be both time-consuming and frustrating. Trawling through platform after platform and looking for the best deal can be a significant headache.</p>
</p>
<p>In this article, I&#8217;ll show you your best options and look at data that shows which platform is best for you.</p>
</p>
<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-battle-of-the-titans">Battle of the Titans</h2>
</p>
<p>The short-term rental (STR) space is dominated by 3 online travel agents (OTAs). These are Airbnb, Booking.com, and Vrbo. These three companies offer a whopping 14.5 million listings between them.</p>
</p>
<p>But, and it&#8217;s a big but, over one and a half million of those listings appear on two or more platforms. These &#8216;cross-listed&#8217; properties are hardly ever advertised at the same price on different OTAs, so you could get lucky and bag a deal &#8211; Or you could be overpaying and not know it.</p>
</p>
<p>Here&#8217;s a visual of the booking landscape as it stands today</p>
</p>

<p>There are no hard and fast rules when it comes to finding the best deal. I compared over 100,000 cross-listing quotes, and the findings are eye-opening.</p>
</p>
<p>These quotes are for 7 nights or more for the same number of guests in each case.</p>
</p>
<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-airbnb-v-vrbo">Airbnb v Vrbo</h2>
</p>
<p>Of 90,000 quotes where the property was listed on both Airbnb and Vrbo, Airbnb was cheaper in only 12,390 cases. But in those cases, Airbnb was 13% cheaper (on average) than Vrbo. Conversely, Vrbo offered the best price 77,541 times. In these cases, if you were to book through Vrbo, you would save 12% on the average Airbnb price.</p>
</p>
<p>So Vrbo offered the best deal 87 percent of the time. That&#8217;s a pretty big number.</p>
</p>
<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-airbnb-v-booking-com">Airbnb v Booking.com</h2>
</p>
<p>I price-checked 13,000 of these cross-listed properties.</p>
</p>
<p>Almost exactly half were cheaper on Airbnb, and half were cheaper on Booking.com.<br />Where Airbnb was cheaper, savings were 17% on average. Where Booking was cheaper, the savings were, on average, 14%</p>
</p>
<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-booking-com-v-vrbo">Booking.com v Vrbo</h2>
</p>
<p>I price-checked over 14,000 of these cross-listings.</p>
</p>
<p>Booking offered the best deal in 6,328 cases, whereas Vrbo offered the best deal in 7,886 cases. So Vrbo came out on to 55% of the time. </p>
</p>
<p>But Vrbo was, on average, nearly 18% cheaper than BDC across comparisons, whereas BDC was &#8216;only&#8217; 11% cheaper than Vrbo.</p>
</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-average-savings">Average savings</h2>
</p>
<p>According to NerdWallet, the average nightly price (for a 7-night stay on Airbnb) is $213. So, it&#8217;s fair to say that the average 7-night booking will cost around $1,500.  </p>
</p>
<p>As you can see, the price differentials range between 11% and 18%, which can add up to a considerable amount of money you may overpay or save. That could equate to $270</p>
