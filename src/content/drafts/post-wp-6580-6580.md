<span style="font-size: 12pt; text-align: justify;">Traveling, one of the most popular<br />
hobbies in the world is a multi-billion dollar industry. Allowing you to<br />
experience new things and see the world, it is one of the most exhilarating<br />
experiences. Traveling for office tours and vacations can sound pretty tempting<br />
but people who travel frequently, know the discomfort and pain that comes with<br />
it. traveling for long hours in an airplane, car, train, bus or motorbike, can<br />
all leave you hurting and aching by the end of the trip. Sitting or riding for<br />
long periods causes pain and stiffness in different areas of your body,<br />
especially if you have any sort of pre-existing health conditions, this can<br />
easily stop you from enjoying your trip. But this can all be avoided by<br />
preparing beforehand and following a few simple tips during the trips.</span></p>
<div style="border-bottom: solid #4F81BD 1.0pt; border: none; mso-element: para-border-div; padding: 0in 0in 4.0pt 0in;">
<div class="MsoTitle" style="margin-bottom: 10pt; text-align: left;">
<b style="text-align: justify;"><span lang="EN-GB" style="font-size: 12.0pt; line-height: 115%;"><br /></span></b><br />
<b style="text-align: justify;"><span lang="EN-GB" style="font-size: 12.0pt; line-height: 115%;">Here are 6<br />
simple tips to ensure that your travels are as stress-free and pain-free as<br />
possible!</span></b></div>
<div class="MsoTitle" style="margin-bottom: 10pt; text-align: left;">
<b style="text-align: justify;"><span lang="EN-GB" style="background: white; font-size: 12.0pt; line-height: 115%;"><br /></span></b><br />
<b style="text-align: justify;"><span lang="EN-GB" style="background: white; font-size: 12.0pt; line-height: 115%;">1. Support Your Back</span></b><br />
<span lang="EN-GB" style="background: white; font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%; text-align: justify;"><br /></span><br />
<span lang="EN-GB" style="background: white; font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%; text-align: justify;">Sitting still in cramped spaces for long<br />
hours is a recipe for back pain, especially for those with pre-existing back<br />
conditions. Most automobile seats don&#8217;t offer sufficient lower back support and<br />
your slumping posture does nothing to help. To avoid back pain, carry a lumbar<br />
support pillow to support your lower back and help your posture. In case you<br />
don&#8217;t have a pillow, you can use a rolled up towel or jacket for some support<br />
too. Additionally, consciously ensure that your back is aligned against the<br />
back of your seat in a sitting position and avoid hunching or slouching to<br />
prevent extra stress on your spine. Another vital point to keep in mind to<br />
protect your back is to be careful while lifting your luggage or putting </span><span lang="EN-GB" style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%; text-align: justify;">it<span style="background: white;"> on overhead </span><span class="InternetLink"><span style="background: white;"><a href="https://bboffroad.com.au/luggage-racks">luggageracks</a></span></span><span style="background: white;">. Use<br />
proper body mechanics to lift the luggage and break it into different stages<br />
instead of lifting it all the way in one go. Use your leg muscles instead of<br />
your back muscles and avoid twisting the back while lifting</span></span><br />
<b style="text-align: justify;"><span lang="EN-GB" style="background: white; font-size: 12.0pt; line-height: 115%;"><br /></span></b><br />
<b style="text-align: justify;"><span lang="EN-GB" style="background: white; font-size: 12.0pt; line-height: 115%;">2. Take Frequent Breaks</span></b></div>
<div class="MsoTitle" style="margin-bottom: 10pt; text-align: left;">
<span lang="EN-GB" style="background: white; font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%; text-align: justify;"><br /></span><br />
<span lang="EN-GB" style="background: white; font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%; text-align: justify;">Sitting or staying in one particular<br />
position for too long is bound to make you sore in a lot of places. The only<br />
way to avoid this is to take breaks every 1-2 hours and walk around a bit<br />
before going back to your seat. If you&#8217;re driving or biking, stop every once in<br />
a while to walk around and fix the blood circulation in your limbs and back. If<br />
you&#8217;re </span><span lang="EN-GB" style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%; text-align: justify;">traveling<span style="background: white;"> </span>o<span style="background: white;">n a bus, train or plane, get out of your seat and walk around<br />
the aisle for a while. Getting an aisle seat instead of the window seat can be<br />
completely worth it in long flights because it allows you to get out of your<br />
seat without having to bother the passenger next to you every time. Stretching<br />
while you&#8217;re out of the seat can make a world of difference in releasing the<br />
tightness in your muscles from sitting in cramped places for long.</span></span><span style="font-size: 12pt; text-align: justify;"> </span><br />
<b style="text-align: justify;"><span lang="EN-GB" style="background: white; font-size: 12.0pt; line-height: 115%;"><br /></span></b><br />
<b style="text-align: justify;"><span lang="EN-GB" style="background: white; font-size: 12.0pt; line-height: 115%;">3. Protect Your Neck</span></b></div>
<div class="MsoTitle" style="margin-bottom: 10pt; text-align: left;">
<span lang="EN-GB" style="background: white; font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%; text-align: justify;"><br /></span><br />
<span lang="EN-GB" style="background: white; font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%; text-align: justify;">Your neck is the next most vulnerable area<br />
after your lower back while </span><span lang="EN-GB" style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%; text-align: justify;">traveling<span style="background: white;"> and<br />
sitting for prolonged hours. Trying to sleep while sitting upright ends with<br />
you leaning against a window or lowering your head for sleep, which causes a<br />
lot of strain and stiffness in your neck and those flat, foamy airline pillows<br />
don&#8217;t offer any help. Carry an inflatable neck pillow for added support to your<br />
neck while resting or sleeping in a sitting position during travel and<br />
stretching your neck from time to time can make a huge difference. Try<br />
stretches like Upper Trapezius Stretch and Levator Scapulae Stretch to relieve<br />
a lot of strain from your neck while sitting in your seat.</span></span><br />
<b style="text-align: justify;"><span lang="EN-GB" style="background: white; font-size: 12.0pt; line-height: 115%;"><br /></span></b><br />
<b style="text-align: justify;"><span lang="EN-GB" style="background: white; font-size: 12.0pt; line-height: 115%;">4. Wear Comfortable Clothing and Shoes</span></b></div>
<div class="MsoTitle" style="margin-bottom: 10pt; text-align: left;">
<span lang="EN-GB" style="background: white; font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%; text-align: justify;"><br /></span><br />
<span lang="EN-GB" style="background: white; font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%; text-align: justify;">When you&#8217;re going to be stuck in cramped<br />
seats, tight restricting clothes or high heels should never be your outfit of<br />
choice when </span><span lang="EN-GB" style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%; text-align: justify;">traveling<span style="background: white;"> for long hours. Even beyond the sitting,<br />
you have to stand in long lines while hauling around your luggage. Hence,<br />
whenever possible, choose comfortable clothes made of breathable material, like<br />
<span class="InternetLink"><a href="https://houseofpain.com/"><span style="color: black;">gym appare</span></a><a href="https://houseofpain.com/"><span style="color: black;">l</span></a><a href="https://houseofpain.com/"><span style="color: black;">,</span></a></span> and shoes like sneakers that<br />
slip on and off easily and have no heel while </span>traveling<span style="background: white;">.</span></span><br />
<b style="font-size: 12pt; text-align: justify;"><br /></b><br />
<b style="font-size: 12pt; text-align: justify;">5. Stay Hydrated</b></div>
<div class="MsoTitle" style="margin-bottom: 10pt; text-align: left;">
<span style="background-color: white; font-size: 12pt; text-align: justify;"><br /></span><br />
<span style="background-color: white; font-size: 12pt; text-align: justify;">It is very important to stay hydrated during<br />
long trips. Not only does that help diffuse the fatigue and jet lag and prevent<br />
sore/dry throat, dry eyes, and dehydration, but the more you drink fluids, the<br />
more frequently you&#8217;ll need to use the washroom which will give you an excuse<br />
for getting out of your seat and get moving.</span><br />
<span style="text-align: justify;"><span lang="EN-GB" style="background: white; font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;"><b><br /></b></span></span><br />
<span style="text-align: justify;"><span lang="EN-GB" style="background: white; font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;"><b>6. Carry </b></span></span><b style="text-align: justify;"><span lang="EN-GB" style="color: windowtext; font-size: 12.0pt; line-height: 115%;">Painkillers</span></b></div>
<div class="MsoTitle" style="margin-bottom: 10pt; text-align: left;">
<span lang="EN-GB" style="background: white; font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%; text-align: justify;"><br /></span><br />
<span lang="EN-GB" style="background: white; font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%; text-align: justify;">Nobody forgets to carry snacks<br />
like dark chocolate, potato chips etc. on their trips, but people commonly<br />
forget to carry over the counter</span><span lang="EN-GB" style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%; text-align: justify;"> <span style="background: white;">lower back pain<br />
