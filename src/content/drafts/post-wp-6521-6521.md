<span lang="EN" style="font-family: " Calibri>5 Questions That Can Help You Buy the Right<br />
Vacation Rental<o:p></o:p></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
</div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span lang="EN" style="font-family: " Calibri>Finding a<br />
vacation rental that fits your wants and needs perfectly can take some work.<br />
Most people think of more obvious questions, like whether to buy a beach<br />
cottage or a mountain home, but forget to consider other crucial questions to<br />
inform their process. That’s why we’ve put together a list of obscure but<br />
important questions every buyer should ask themselves when looking for the<br />
perfect vacation rental.<o:p></o:p></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
</div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<b style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal;"><span lang="EN" style="font-family: " Calibri>How Will You Finance Your Vacation Rental?<o:p></o:p></span></b></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
</div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span lang="EN" style="font-family: " Calibri>This should<br />
be the very first question you ask when looking for a vacation property.<br />
Finding the right rental in a </span><span lang="EN"><a href="https://www.vacasa.com/top-markets/best-places-to-buy-a-vacation-home" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><span style="color: #1155cc; font-family: " Calibri>perfect location</span></a></span><span lang="EN" style="font-family: " Calibri> and at the right price is a good place to<br />
start, but you also need to research available funding options for your second<br />
home. </span><span lang="EN"><a href="https://www.pennymacusa.com/conventional-home-loans" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><span style="color: #1155cc; font-family: " Calibri>Conventional mortgages</span></a></span><span lang="EN" style="font-family: " Calibri> are often preferred by homebuyers because you<br />
can choose between adjustable- and fixed-rate loans, and you may also be able to<br />
avoid paying for additional mortgage insurance. Just keep in mind that you will<br />
need a 20 percent down payment to forgo mortgage insurance. <o:p></o:p></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
</div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<b style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal;"><span lang="EN" style="font-family: " Calibri>Will You Use Your Vacation Home or Just Rent<br />
It Out? <o:p></o:p></span></b></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
</div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span lang="EN" style="font-family: " Calibri>Just because<br />
you’re planning to rent out your vacation rental doesn’t mean you can’t stay in<br />
it yourself. The beauty of investing in a rental is that you, the owner, get to<br />
enjoy it from time to time. However, if you intend to just rent it out, you<br />
need to really think about what you </span><span lang="EN"><a href="https://www.investopedia.com/articles/personal-finance/041515/buying-second-home-rent-dos-and-donts.asp" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><span style="color: #1155cc; font-family: " Calibri>should and shouldn’t do</span></a></span><span lang="EN" style="font-family: " Calibri> as its landlord. While renting can help cover<br />
your mortgage and provide lucrative tax breaks, you will also be dealing with<br />
the stress of being a landlord. If you want to reduce that stress, you should<br />
also think about </span><span lang="EN"><a href="https://invitedhome.com/vacation-rental-owner-articles/10-questions-to-ask-before-choosing-a-vacation-rental-manager" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><span style="color: #1155cc; font-family: " Calibri>hiring a property manager</span></a></span><span lang="EN" style="font-family: " Calibri> to help out with your vacation rental. <o:p></o:p></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
</div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<b style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal;"><span lang="EN" style="font-family: " Calibri>Should You Buy a Brand New Home or a<br />
Fixer-Upper? <o:p></o:p></span></b></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
</div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span lang="EN" style="font-family: " Calibri>If you want<br />
to get creative with financing, you could </span><span lang="EN"><a href="https://www.marketwatch.com/story/consider-buying-a-fixer-upper-home-using-a-renovation-loan-2019-01-03" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><span style="color: #1155cc; font-family: " Calibri>use renovation loans</span></a></span><span lang="EN" style="font-family: " Calibri> to purchase a fixer-upper as an investment or<br />
vacation property. An FHA 203(k) loan can be used for any of these two types of<br />
home purchases and can allow eligible vacation home buyers to purchase their<br />
second homes with less of a down payment. This is a huge incentive for buying a<br />
fixer-upper, in addition to having more available properties to choose from and<br />
potentially lower prices, but </span><span lang="EN"><a href="https://www.northwesternmutual.com/life-and-money/is-a-fixer-upper-house-worth-the-cost/" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><span style="color: #1155cc; font-family: " Calibri>buying a fixer-upper</span></a></span><span lang="EN" style="font-family: " Calibri>, even as your second home, can be stressful.<br />
So for many buyers, a finished vacation property may be the better choice,<br />
especially if you do not want to budget for repairs. <o:p></o:p></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
</div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<b style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal;"><span lang="EN" style="font-family: " Calibri>How Will You Manage Maintenance for Your<br />
Property? <o:p></o:p></span></b></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
</div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span lang="EN" style="font-family: " Calibri>If you<br />
already own a home, you already know that </span><span lang="EN"><a href="https://porch.com/resource/cost-of-home-maintenance-in-america" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><span style="color: #1155cc; font-family: " Calibri>home maintenance costs</span></a></span><span lang="EN" style="font-family: " Calibri> can really add up. Average home maintenance<br />
expenses can run more than $16,000 a year, so you really need to include maintenance<br />
costs in your overall home budget. In addition to figuring out how you will pay<br />
for vacation home maintenance, you also need to determine how you will resolve<br />
issues from afar. If you plan on renting your property, a property manager </span><span lang="EN"><a href="https://blog.myvr.com/vacation-rental-management-diy-or-hire-a-property-manager/" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><span style="color: #1155cc; font-family: " Calibri>could be beneficial</span></a></span><span lang="EN" style="font-family: " Calibri> in managing unexpected repairs and keeping<br />
your home in good condition for potential renters. <o:p></o:p></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
</div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<b style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal;"><span lang="EN" style="font-family: " Calibri>Will You Need to Invest in a Home Security<br />
System? <o:p></o:p></span></b></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
</div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span lang="EN" style="font-family: " Calibri>If you plan<br />
on living in your vacation home occasionally, installing a </span><span lang="EN"><a href="https://www.safehome.org/security-systems/best/" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><span style="color: #1155cc; font-family: " Calibri>security system</span></a></span><span lang="EN" style="font-family: " Calibri> is a wise move to protect your investment.<br />
Even from afar, you will have some peace of mind knowing that your vacation<br />
home, as well as the contents, is better protected against potential break-ins<br />
and burglaries. In addition to deterring crime, a comprehensive security system<br />
can also provide added protection and alerts for other emergencies, including fires<br />
and gas issues. Most security systems also allow homeowners to access features,<br />
and even video, remotely, which can be a serious benefit for vacation property<br />
owners who live far away. <o:p></o:p></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
</div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span lang="EN" style="font-family: " Calibri>With the<br />
right research, the right financing, and the right moves, buying a vacation<br />
rental doesn’t have to be stressful. Thinking about the small details involved<br />
with owning a rental can help you make a more informed decision and make the<br />
process of buying the perfect property much more enjoyable. <o:p></o:p></span></div>
