<h2 class="wp-block-heading">The importance of direct bookings in the hospitality industry</h2>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img decoding="async" width="48" height="48" src="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/06/294399_service_business_bell_customer-service_important_icon.png" alt="The importance of direct bookings in the hospitality industry" class="wp-image-37052"/></figure>



<p>I won&#8217;t dwell on this too much, as so much has been written on this subject, but we are in a fiercely competitive marketplace. The short-term rental space is awash with booking options for potential guests.</p>



<p>There are the big brand sites, local sites, property management sites, a myriad of social media pages and groups, and more, all shouting book me, book me.</p>



<p>As a host or small property manager, it&#8217;s essential to be able to take direct bookings, now more than ever.</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Advantages of having a dedicated direct booking website</h2>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img decoding="async" width="48" height="48" src="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/06/5241496_emoji_face_positive_smile_smiley_icon.png" alt="Advantages of having a dedicated direct booking website" class="wp-image-37050"/></figure>



<ul>
<li><strong>Trust</strong> &#8211; Put yourself in the position of the traveler. Why would I my next vacation via a Facebook post, as opposed to via a big brand like Airbnb or VRBO? A professional-looking, easy-to-navigate, and simple-to-book website ups your online presence and instills trust in potential guests.</li>
</ul>



<ul>
<li>  <strong>Control</strong> &#8211; Having your own website gives you more control of your business and how it&#8217;s represented online. You dictate who stays and who doesn&#8217;t (no bachelor parties, no spring breakers), and you operate your own cancellation policies (no more cancel any-time policies from third-party companies).</li>
</ul>



<ul>
<li>  <strong>Price</strong> &#8211; Without the commission (service fees) that the big listing sites add, you can offer the lowest price on the web. That&#8217;s the most significant incentive for potential guests to book with you.</li>
</ul>



<ul>
<li>  <strong>Exposure</strong> &#8211; Site visitors only see your property. They aren&#8217;t overwhelmed with 10&#8217;s or 100&#8217;s of other options.</li>
</ul>



<ul>
<li><strong>Reduced risk</strong> &#8211; If you rely solely on the likes of Airbnb and Booking.com and, for whatever reason, they remove your listing, your business is dead in the water. Having your own website is a great fallback position.</li>
</ul>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Disadvantages of having a dedicated direct booking website</h2>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img decoding="async" width="48" height="48" src="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/06/2291008_angry_bad_face_irritated_negative_icon.png" alt="Disadvantages of having a dedicated direct booking website" class="wp-image-37051"/></figure>



<p>You won&#8217;t find too many people telling you about the drawbacks of creating and operating your own book-direct website, but I&#8217;m here for you.</p>



<ul>
<li>Cost &#8211; Websites and website designers don&#8217;t come cheap. As a general rule, if you find a cheap solution, it&#8217;s probably not worth having. I&#8217;ll cover costs later on in some detail-</li>
</ul>



<ul>
<li>Time &#8211; No matter which solution you plump for, you&#8217;ll need to spend time setting up the site (even if someone else is creating the site, you&#8217;ll need to collate photos, list amenities, write copy, etc.), and you&#8217;ll need to manage bookings.</li>
</ul>



<ul>
<li>Work &#8211; Taking direct booking is more work. Potential guests have questions, emails must be answered, deposits must be invoiced, and installments must be chased. It all adds up.</li>
</ul>



<ul>
<li>Longevity &#8211; This is the one area that hosts don&#8217;t factor in when budgeting for a direct booking website. Web design is forever evolving, both technically and visually. The bottom line is that you will need a new website (or your existing website will need a makeover) every 3 &#8211; 5 years.</li>
</ul>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Considerations before creating a direct booking website </h2>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img decoding="async" width="48" height="48" src="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/06/667353_brain_mind_process_think_icon.png" alt="Considerations before creating a direct booking website " class="wp-image-37053"/></figure>



<ul>
<li><strong>Budget and resources</strong> &#8211; As I mentioned before, there are no exceptionally cheap options, and those that are cheap aren&#8217;t really worth having. If you have a website made from scratch, you probably need to budget somewhere between $1,000 and $3,000 for a single property site (plus monthly hosting fees of around $30). If you are using a vacation rental site builder, you´re probably looking at between $15 to $60 a month. If you create the site yourself, you&#8217;ll only need hosting and maybe some paid plugins. We&#8217;ll look at these options in more detail later in the article.</li>
</ul>



<ul>
<li><strong>Target audience </strong>&#8211; It&#8217;s important to align your website with your target audience (where possible). This isn&#8217;t always possible, though. When I was running my last Airbnb, I had all sorts of guests, from 18 to 80, from all over the world, so my target audience was pretty much everyone. </li>
</ul>



<p></p>



<ul>
<li>If your property is near Disney and you cater to families, or if your home sits on a golf course and caters to groups of golfers, these are things that you need to consider, and your site should speak to these demographics. </li>
</ul>



<ul>
<li><strong>Market research</strong> &#8211; If you are just building a website showcasing your property, I wouldn&#8217;t get too bogged down with market research to begin with. Keyword research will be very useful if you are planning on writing a blog.</li>
</ul>



<ul>
<li><strong>Your brand, property, and domain names &#8211; </strong>I recommend choosing a unique name for your property and domain. There are so many condos called Ocean View and hundreds of Rose Cottage cottages. If someone is searching for one of these, they are going to have a hard time finding it. </li>
</ul>



<ul>
<li>It&#8217;s not easy coming up with, choosing, and deciding on a name for your business, but there are brand name generators online. Check out <a href="https://logo.com/business-name-generator" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">https://logo.com/business-name-generator</a> &#8211; Most are free to use and also tell you if the domain is available.</li>
</ul>



<ul>
<li>Some options here offer sub-domain or provider name domains. myplace.wordpress.com or xxx.xxx.com &#8211; Avoid these options as they are very unprofessional.</li>
</ul>



<ul>
<li><strong>Email</strong> &#8211; Also avoid using Gmail, Hotmail, etc. email addresses. This also projects an unprofessional air about your business and brand.</li>
</ul>



<ul>
<li>Logo &#8211; Once you have a brand name, you will also need a logo. Head over to <a href="https://www.fiverr.com/search/gigs?query=logo%20design&amp;source=main_banner&amp;acmpl=1&amp;search_in=everywhere&amp;search-autocomplete-original-term=lo&amp;search-autocomplete-available=true&amp;search-autocomplete-type=suggest&amp;search-autocomplete-position=1&amp;ref_ctx_id=81d9a9e8a72d56bbd5da48d385cfba95" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">Fivrr.com</a> and choose a logo designer. Prices range from $5 and upward. My advice here is to spend $50 or more. You get what you pay for. Look for a design package that will produce a number of logos to choose from, and make sure that some revisions are included.</li>
</ul>



<ul>
<li><strong>User experience and design </strong></li>
</ul>



<ul>
<li><strong>Integration with existing systems (property management system, channel manager, etc.) </strong>&#8211; If you are already using a PMS or a channel manager, you will need to make sure that it can be integrated with the platform that you choose to create your site on.</li>
</ul>



<ul>
<li><strong>Booking engine</strong> &#8211; If you are creating a direct booking website, you will need to integrate a booking engine. These manage your calendar, rates, quotes, bookings, and in many cases, emails. Take some time to research which booking engine you would like to use. Most work on a monthly subscription basis, but some work on commission. Check out <a href="https://www.ownerreservations.com/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">Owner Rez</a> and <a href="https://en.freetobook.com/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">Free To Book</a>.</li>
</ul>



<ul>
<li><strong>SEO and online visibility</strong> &#8211; SEO stands for Search Engine Optimization. This is the one area that hosts don&#8217;t understand. The majority of hosts build a 5 &#8211; 10 page website that promotes their property and the hope (expect) their website to appear in search (organic Google search) when people search for terms like &#8216;vacation rental *their destination*&#8217;, or &#8216;holiday rental *their destination*&#8217;, or something similar. That&#8217;s not going to happen.</li>
</ul>



<p>I&#8217;m not saying this to rain on your parade. I&#8217;m just sharing my experience and the reality of the situation. There are millions of short-term rental websites and possibly hundreds serving your location. Most of these will be older (more established), and many will be larger (more informative) than your new site. Google prefers older and informative over and above new.</p>



<p>That said, there is still a lot to be gained by having your own site. Friends, family, and colleagues can book directly with you. Your social posts can point to your site. You can start to build your brand. But, when your site is new, you will need to drive traffic to it as opposed to sitting back and expecting traffic to just arrive.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Create your website yourself</h2>



<p>There are a few ways that you can do this. You can use WordPress, Wix, Weebly, or SquareSpace.</p>



<p><strong>Pros</strong><br>You get to know everything about your website and how it works.<br>You have full control.<br>You decide on design and branding.<br>This is pretty much the cheapest option.</p>



<p><strong>Cons</strong><br>There&#8217;s a learning curve involved.<br>It will take time.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">WordPress</h2>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img decoding="async" width="48" height="48" src="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/06/8726526_wordpress_icon.png" alt="Wordpress
" class="wp-image-37225"/></figure>



<p>455 million websites have been built using WordPress, so it&#8217;s doing many things right. I could write for days on WordPress, I have built 100s of WordPress sites, but I will keep things brief.</p>



<p>WordPress is free, but you will need a hosting package; I recommend Cloudways.com as their services are well-priced and very fast. WordPress comes in two versions, and you will need the wordpress.org version.</p>



<p>There&#8217;s a learning curve to using WordPress (WP), but its drag-and-drop interface is much easier to use than it used to be.</p>



<p>When you first install WP, it comes as an empty shell, and you add things to it in order to create your site. You can choose from 1,000&#8217;s of themes (both free and paid-for options).</p>



<p>You will also need to add plugins. Plugins add functionality. </p>



<p>Imagine that you are buying a new car. You get the base model, but you can upgrade by choosing a special paint scheme and by adding electric windows, a sunroof, and a towbar. This is how WordPress works. The initial installation is the base car, the paint scheme is the theme, and the other upgrades are called plugins.</p>



<p>You can add plugins that manage calendars, image slideshows, email forms, and thousands of other functions. Most plugins are free, and some you&#8217;ll need to pay for. If you are using a channel manager or property management system, the software system that you are using likely has a plugin for WordPress.  </p>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading">WordPress Themes</h4>



<p>As I said before, there are 1,000&#8217;s of themes available for WordPress. In fact, there are so many it&#8217;s very confusing, making the decision of which one to use very difficult. I would recommend using a simple theme like <a href="https://www.kadencewp.com/kadence-theme/starter-templates/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">Kadence</a> or <a href="https://wpastra.com/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">Astra</a>. Both offer a choice of free themes and all are super fast (fast is very important).</p>



<p>Have a look at the <a href="https://www.kadencewp.com/kadence-theme/starters/yosemite/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">Kadence Yosemite theme</a> or <a href="https://wpastra.com/templates/home-stay-04/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">Astra&#8217;s Home Stay theme</a>; both are great starter themes for your short-term rental business.</p>

The paid version is only $39 a year and it even has Ical integration, so you can connect it to your OTA listings.</p>

<p>Shopify is primarily designed as an e-commerce platform, with a focus on helping businesses create online stores and sell products. While Shopify offers various features and customization options, it may not be the most suitable choice for creating a vacation rental direct booking website. Here are a few reasons why:</p>



<ol>
<li>Limited Booking Functionality:  While you can integrate third-party apps or plugins to add booking functionality, it may not offer the comprehensive set of features required for managing vacation rental bookings, availability calendars, and other specific needs of the vacation rental industry.</li>



<li>Lack of Built-in Rental Management Tools: Shopify doesn&#8217;t include built-in features tailored specifically for vacation rental management, such as property listing management, reservation tracking, or automated guest communication. </li>



<li>Template Limitations: Shopify&#8217;s template library primarily focuses on e-commerce designs and doesn&#8217;t offer specific templates for vacation rentals. Customizing and adapting e-commerce templates to accommodate rental-specific features like property listings, booking calendars, and property descriptions might require additional development work.</li>



<li>Pricing Structure: This is the kicker. Shopify&#8217;s pricing plans are based on transaction charges, and what with so many aspects of the rental sector taking a percentage of your revenue, this is another expense that you could well do without. </li>
</ol>
