<p>So, you own a short-term rental and you want more direct bookings. Well, this article is for you.</p>
<p>But first I have some questions&#8230;</p>
<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-who-are-you-and-why-should-i-trust-you">Who are you, and why should I trust you?</h2>
</p>
<p>The biggest stumbling block to converting direct bookings is TRUST &#8211; Or a lack of it to be more accurate.</p>
<p>First-time potential guests don&#8217;t know me or you, or our properties. Let&#8217;s face it: We are internet strangers to them and we&#8217;re asking them for money. </p>
<p>That&#8217;s bound to raise some doubts like; </p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Can we be trusted? </strong></li>
</p>
<li><strong>Is this a real rental? </strong></li>
</p>
<li><strong>Are we scammers?</strong></li>
</ul>
</p>
<p>As a host, you know you can be trusted. You know that your rental is real. You know that you&#8217;re not a scammer. </p>
<p>BUT POTENTIAL GUESTS DON&#8217;T KNOW THESE THINGS. And THEY HAVE DOUBTS.</p>
<p>We are competing with very big brands that have spent time and money nurturing these doubts; So that Jane and Joe Stranger book on-platform. </p>
<p>For some travelers, these doubts are very real. Last year holiday fraudsters conned consumers out of more than £15m &#8211; And that was only in the UK.</p>
<p>The reporting centre for fraud and cybercrime – which has launched a national awareness campaign hoping people “don’t get burnt before they are on the beach” – said the average loss per victim last year was £2,372.</p>
<p><strong>So how do we alleviate these doubts and build trust?</strong></p>
<p><a href="https://www.theguardian.com/money/2023/may/23/fraudsters-uk-holidaymakers-action-fraud-fake-flights">https://www.theguardian.com/money/2023/may/23/fraudsters-uk-holidaymakers-action-fraud-fake-flights</a></p>
<p>How many of you here would accept a direct booking request that ensures guests will show up so that you can take payment any way you want (cash, Venmo, etc.) once the guests arrive.</p>
<p>Guests are ensured to show up because they authorize a charge to their credit card. This is like Open Table, providing diners show up for reservations.</p>
<p>Guests will appreciate not needing to pay in advance. Thus, it’s easier to get the guest to commit.</p>
<p>You would not need a website, nor any of the hassles and costs of maintaining a website. The guest would pay a small convenience fee to provide the booking guarantee.</p>
<ul>
<li>No — I only want payment ahead of time and don’t care if I lose a few bookings.</li>
</p>
<li>Maybe — I’d consider the fine print and how comfortable I feel with the guest.</li>
</p>
<li>Yes! — I’d love to make it more comfortable for guests to confirm a book direct stay, and I’d love to take better payment options upon arrival.</li>
</ul>
