<p><strong>Protect you and your property from fraudulent bookings and reservations by staying alert, creating regimented policies and implementing 5 key risk management strategies, including screening your guests, creating accurate listings and knowing how to secure your direct booking website to help safeguard your business.</strong></p>
<p>Fraudulent bookings and chargebacks can be disastrous for you as a property manager or host and likely leave you out of pocket or with costly damage to your listing. Sometimes your reputation is negatively impacted too. <strong>This means you need to take higher precautions when accepting bookings.</strong></p>
<p>As a property management company or host, you want to focus on what truly matters: guest experience and your success and performance as a vacation rental business. Unfortunately, fraudsters like to exploit the industry. </p>
<p>In this article, we explain how you can avoid vacation rental fraud on popular OTAs and your direct booking websites as well as provide the tools and strategies you need to safeguard your business. </p>
<p>We’ll cover:</p>
<h2 id="fraudulent-bookings"><strong>Different types of fraudulent bookings</strong></h2>
<p>As a vacation rental host, you need to be aware of two different types of fraud when accepting a reservation:</p>
<ul>
<li><em>Identity fraud</em></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li><em>Guest chargebacks </em></li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Top Tip</strong> –<strong> </strong>It’s also good to be aware of website fraud. This is where fraudsters will imitate your website or business, deceiving guests with fraudulent bookings and listings and ultimately damaging your reputation as a business.</p>
<h4><em><span>Identity fraud</span></em></h4>
<p><strong>A booking can be fraudulently created by a guest when they are using a fake ID or when identity fraud has occurred.</strong> This is when a guest makes a reservation under someone else’s name and identification. </p>
<p>When this happens, <strong>your property is more vulnerable to illicit activity occurring</strong> during that booking, and it can also make it harder for you to trace your guests for compensation if damage does occur.</p>
<h4><em><span>Guest chargebacks</span></em></h4>
<p><strong>Sometimes, what appears to be a genuine booking at first, can result in chargeback fraud. </strong></p>
<p>After a guest has completed their stay, a guest can fraudulently request a chargeback from their credit card provider, leaving you out of pocket for a genuine transaction.</p>
<h2 id="chargeback-fraud"><strong>What is chargeback fraud?</strong></h2>
<p>There are three types of chargeback fraud:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Accidental </strong>– The guest doesn’t recognise the transaction on their bank statement, so they request a chargeback.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li><strong>Opportunistic </strong>— Guests spot an opportunity to get money back if they are not fully satisfied with their stay. They request a chargeback instead of notifying their host about problems with the property.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li><strong>Malicious </strong>— As the name suggests, a malicious chargeback is deliberate fraud, where guest intends to claim their money back after checking out.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Chargebacks can sometimes be genuine if listing descriptions have been largely inaccurate or misleading or guests make you aware of problems, yet you do nothing to resolve them. </strong></p>
<p>However, chargebacks usually come as a shock, and unfortunately, businesses only win around <a href="https://chargebacks911.com/chargeback-stats/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">32% of chargeback disputes</a>.</p>

<p>While accepting every reservation is tempting, it pays to be aware of the red flags that often come with fraudulent bookings.</p>
<p><strong>Here are 4 red flags to look out for when guests book that will keep you hosting good guests:</strong></p>
<h3>1. Guest communication </h3>
<p>Be wary of inaccurate property descriptions in your communication with guests. </p>
<p>For example, if the guest enquiring is talking about a city apartment and your property is a 5-bedroom villa, this could indicate they’re sending mass emails to property managers in the hope of catching someone in an Airbnb scam.</p>
<h3>2. Last-minute stays</h3>
<p>Last-minute bookings offer a great opportunity for property managers looking to fill gaps in their calendars.</p>
<p>However, <strong>fraudsters often take advantage of late bookings</strong> in the hope that you won’t have time to run effective <a href="https://superhog.com/what-is-guest-screening/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">guest screening</a> and security checks.</p>
<p>It’s essential to run all your security procedures and confirm the guest’s identification, even for last-minute reservations so that you can prevent a fraudulent booking.  <strong>With the right security measures and </strong><a href="https://superhog.com/short-term-rental-insurance-guide/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener"><strong>damage protection</strong></a><strong> in place, you can confidently accept late bookings and improve your occupancy.  </strong></p>
<h3>3. One-night bookings</h3>
<p>Again, single-night bookings can sometimes, but not always, raise alarm bells for fraudulent bookings and even parties. Guests who book for one night may also intend to commit other illicit activities within your listing.</p>
<p>However, there’s no need to turn down one-night bookings when you implement the correct risk management procedures. In fact, <strong>one-night bookings can also be a great additional revenue maker similar to last-minute bookings</strong> by filling gaps in your calendar. </p>
<p><strong>For more information on how to calculate your vacation rental performance, booking performance and revenue, check out our latest article on the </strong><a href="https://superhog.com/airbnb-kpis/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener"><strong>10 best KPIs to track as a vacation rental host.</strong></a><strong> </strong></p>
<h3>4. Requesting to pay by cash, cheque, or wire transfer</h3>
<p>Be extra cautious if a guest wants to pay by an unconventional payment method such as cash, cheque or wire transfer. It’s best to stick to your preferred payment method and use <a href="https://superhog.com/3d-secure-for-vacation-rentals/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">3D Secure payments</a> to make payments safer for you and your guests. </p>
<h2 id="five-steps"><strong>5 ways to prevent fraudulent bookings and guest chargebacks</strong></h2>
<p>Avoiding fraudulent bookings and chargebacks is complicated but not impossible. Knowing the right tools and strategies to help prevent vacation rental fraud is important so you can host confidently.</p>
<p>So, how do you go about protecting your short-term rental business? You must create a risk management process and ensure you’re building a portfolio of accurate listings. </p>
<p><strong>Here are our 5 tips for avoiding fraudulent bookings, Airbnb scams and chargeback fraud.</strong></p>
<h3>1. Create accurate listing descriptions </h3>
<p>Vacation rental fraud prevention as a host begins with an accurate listing description, which will help to:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Set expectations for your property</strong></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li><strong>Maintain your standards</strong></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li><strong>Attract the right guests</strong></li>
</ul>
<p>If your listing is true to your property, guests will not be able to claim later on that their stay was sub-par and that they were a victim of fraud. It will also make it easier for you to contest any guest chargebacks that occur.</p>
<p><strong>Your listing should </strong><a href="https://superhog.com/must-have-airbnb-amenities/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener"><strong>highlight your amenities</strong></a><strong>, have an accurate and detailed listing description and plenty of high-quality photos of your property</strong> – consider hiring a professional photographer to help truly capture the essence of your property. </p>
<p>It’s also essential to keep up with all property maintenance. This will help avoid unpleasant surprises for your guests during their stay, which could lead to complaints, negative reviews and guest chargebacks.</p>

</ul>
<ul>
<li><strong>Streamline the check-in process</strong></li>
</ul>
