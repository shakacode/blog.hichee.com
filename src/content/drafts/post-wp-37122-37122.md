<p><strong>Are you looking for ways to elevate your short-term rental business and secure direct bookings throughout the year to fill up those gaps in your calendar? In that case, you need to know how to attract the right guests, convert them from lookers to bookers and provide exceptional experiences to make them want to book again.  Our inside look at how to attract repeat guests is loaded with industry expertise to help build your strategies for success.</strong></p>
<p>Whether you’re a host with a single property or a property manager with an extensive portfolio, the goal remains the same – to attract guests, accept bookings and build revenue.</p>
<p>However, to be successful, many hosts believe that attracting new guests is the only way to continue to drive traffic to your short-term rental business. This, in fact, can:</p>
<ul>
<li>Take up a large proportion of your time</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>Be costly to you and impact your bottom line</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>We’re here to debunk the myth that new is always better</strong> and show you that there are other ways to build your business and fill those gaps in your calendar. </p>
<h2>Industry Insights</h2>
<p>According to AirDNA’s <a href="https://www.airdna.co/blog/2023-us-mid-year-short-term-rental-outlook-report" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">mid-market review</a> of the industry’s performance, although hosts are currently facing economic uncertainty, cost-conscious travellers and an evolving and changing market, <strong>the STR industry has defied slowdown predictions. </strong></p>
<p>We’re seeing travellers:</p>
<ul>
<li>Spend more nights in STRs than ever before</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>Consistent double-demand growth in the last 6 months</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>A shift in demand as traveller interest in shoulder season bookings increases</li>
</ul>
<p><span><strong>What does this mean for hosts?</strong></span></p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Travellers are hunting for value: </strong>Despite the economic climate demand is still rife for travel.  </li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li><strong>A shift in seasonal popularity:</strong> Bookings for ‘typical’ quieter months are increasing</li>
</ul>
<p>However, the current growth of supply, combined with the decrease in occupancy rates, also shows us the industry is flooded with short-term rental options – great for guests, not so much for hosts – and with hotels now inching their way into the short-term rental space, the market is even more competitive than before. </p>
<p>Travel consumers (potential guests) have to scroll through a multitude of options before making a booking decision. </p>
<p>So, what are you doing as a host to combat this?</p>
<p>Attention-grabbing imagery? Well-written listing descriptions? These are all great to help grab the attention of new guests, but what about guests who have already stayed with you? </p>
<p>Are your efforts targeted towards attracting them to book again? If not, fear not!  </p>
<p><strong>We’ve interviewed 6 industry experts to highlight the importance of direct bookings and all the ways you can foster guest loyalty and captivate your guests for endless return visits. </strong></p>
<p><span><strong>Who we asked:</strong></span></p>
<p>According to Airbnb Coach Tatianna, many hosts commonly ask about direct bookings and the opportunities they bring. </p>
<p>We will answer these questions through insights shared by our following industry experts:</p>

<h2>The ideal percentage of direct bookings vs third-party bookings</h2>
<p>For property managers and hosts, measuring your performance of bookings will not be a one size fits all. </p>
<p>Given the dominance of OTAs (Airbnb, Vrbo, Booking.com, Expedia) in the short-term rental market, aspiring for 100% direct bookings is overly-optimistic and may bring challenges, especially when first starting in the industry. </p>
<p><strong>Therefore,</strong> <strong>the ideal percentage of direct bookings, in comparison to third-party bookings, will depend on your distribution strategy and goals as a property manager. </strong></p>
<h3>Our experts said:</h3>
<p><em>“There is no right or wrong answer here. Any STR host/manager who is looking to generate more direct bookings can look at increasing numbers in very modest milestones. If that’s going from 0% to 1%, that’s fine. It’s what is right for you given your time and talent resources.</em></p>
<p><em>However, there are <strong>certain things that we should</strong> <strong>all be analysing when addressing the goal of increasing direct bookings:</strong></em></p>
<ul>
<li><em><strong>What is your current cost of acquiring direct bookings</strong> (think about your time, SEO, and paid advertising budgets)? This may be costing you more than you might think.</em></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li><em>Is the <strong>lack of data alongside your OTA bookings costing you your communication</strong> and relationship with guests?</em></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li><em><strong>Are you over-compromising on your T&#038;Cs</strong> (e.g. cancellation policy, deposits, payment terms)? </em></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li><em><strong>Are you reliant on 2/3 booking channels for the majority of your bookings?</strong> If so, what happens if your listings get pulled overnight without any reason or explanation? (It happens!)”</em></li>
</ul>
<p class="has-vivid-red-color has-text-color">–<strong> <em>Damian Sheridan, The Book Direct Show.</em></strong></p>
<p><em>“We’re advocates of using third-party OTAs to list your own property, especially if you are just starting out and do not have your own captive audience of new or repeat bookers just yet.</em></p>
<p><em><strong>As you grow your business, it’s great to see your calendar expand with direct bookings vs third-party, as it means you are becoming less reliant on the search algorithms of those third parties.</strong>”</em></p>
<p class="has-vivid-red-color has-text-color"><strong>– <em>Miles Hobson, Hospitable</em></strong></p>
<p><em><strong>“This is entirely down to the strategy of the property managers and hosts. </strong>Some property managers want to attract 100% direct. This can be for a variety of reasons; from reducing channel fees, maintaining control of booking and cancellation terms and conditions, wanting to build their brand, to an outright dislike of the channels.”</em></p>
<p class="has-vivid-red-color has-text-color"><strong>– <em>Kelly Odor, Bookster</em></strong></p>

<h2>The benefits of direct bookings and repeat guests</h2>
<p>Listing on multiple channels is a great way to help attract guests to your property, but what if they’ve already stayed with you? How are you ensuring these guests don’t just forget you after their stay? </p>
<p>Building a direct booking strategy can in fact help you nurture current and past guests which will help secure repeat bookings and fill those gaps in your calendar. </p>
<p><strong>The benefits of direct repeat bookings include:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Increase your direct booking performance percentage:</strong> Guests are more likely to book direct than through a third-party channel if they have had a previous booking with you where you have gone above and beyond in meeting guest expectations.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li><strong>Reduce third-party channel and booking fees: </strong>Say goodbye to paying fees for every booking you receive when you go direct. </li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li><strong>Build your brand and reputation: </strong>The more guests book again, the more memorable you are to them, which includes your branding and your reputation – don’t forget, consumers are 90% more likely to trust brands recommended by friends.</li>
</ul>
<h3>So, when do hosts typically turn to direct bookings?</h3>
<p><em>“Hosts with 1-5 units who have overcome the early daunting obstacles like creating their own website, forming a company, taking </em><a href="https://knowyourguest.superhog.com/damage-deposits/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener"><em>deposits</em></a><em> and payments and the </em><a href="https://knowyourguest.superhog.com/guest-screening/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener"><em>risk of unknown guests</em></a><em>, are usually ready to explore the many ways to generate direct bookings. </em></p>
<p><em>This usually speeds up at the point when the business becomes full-time.”</em></p>
<p class="has-vivid-red-color has-text-color"><strong><em>– Damian Sheridan, The Book Direct Show</em></strong></p>
<p>It’s useful to know when the shift towards direct bookings occurred. In the past few years, we can see that the Covid pandemic has had a huge impact on this. </p>
<p class="has-black-color has-text-color">“<em>Covid was clearly the seismic catalyst for many hosts around the world to begin exploring direct booking strategies. When several of the major OTAs were effectively closed for business (and enforcing cancellation refunds) many property managers who relied exclusively on them were left without any paths to revenue.”</em></p>
<p class="has-vivid-red-color has-text-color">– <strong><em>Damian Sheridan, The Book Direct Show</em></strong></p>
<h2>Attracting repeat guests</h2>
<h3 id="direct-booking-website">Using your direct booking website to attract repeat guests </h3>
<p>Repeat guests will typically skip the middle man (the OTAs) and come straight to your direct booking website when looking to make their next online booking<strong>. It’s important to ensure you make your website easy to use, with a simple booking process and attention-grabbing media to ultimately secure that repeat booking.</strong></p>
<p>Mark Simpson, CEO of <a href="https://boostly.co.uk/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">Boostly</a> said, <em><strong>“Your website content is a great way to connect with previous, potential and current guests.</strong> A regularly updated blog targeting specific keywords will do wonders for your SEO, but it also provides interesting content for guests to read too. At Boostly, we’ve recently integrated ChatGPT into our direct booking sites, so hosts can create unique optimised content for their site at a click of a button.</em></p>
<p><em>Remember, your booking engine should be easy-to-use and display real-time pricing and availability – the last thing you want is a guest jumping through all the hoops only to be met with ‘Sorry, these dates aren’t available ”</em></p>
<p><strong>It’s important to <a href="https://superhog.com/5-marketing-tips-for-direct-booking-websites/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">build trust</a> with your direct bookings. Guests won’t book direct when they don’t feel comfortable with your brand or website. </strong></p>
<p>“We believe that the best place to start with direct bookings is by marketing to individuals who already trust your brand and have stayed with you before. In StayFi’s case, guests are always providing this data when they log into the WiFi, and they are opting in for a vacation rental operator’s email marketing program”</p>
<p class="has-vivid-red-color has-text-color"><em>– <strong>Arthur Colker, StayFi</strong></em></p>
<p>Here are some of the <strong>key areas property managers and hosts should focus on</strong> to help build a website that converts:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>High-quality imagery:</strong> including every room and a detailed property description. Give them everything they need at their fingertips</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li><strong>A clear homepage: </strong>showcase your best properties to really grab their attention. Their top properties for a reason.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li><strong>FAQ page: </strong>cover all the frequently asked questions, this will save you and your guests time. You can even include a chatbot feature should they want to know more.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li><strong>Contact details:</strong> Make it easy for your guests to reach out. Communication is key in building and retaining relationships with guests.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li><strong>Trust</strong>: Create company about pages to show a human side to the business and ensure your website uses SSL encryption to help enforce the legitimacy of your website and prevent <a href="https://superhog.com/how-to-prevent-website-fraud-in-the-vacation-rental-industry/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">website fraud.</a> </li>
</ul>

<h3>The importance of targeting guests that have already stayed at your vacation rental</h3>
<p><strong>Converting a guest to book again directly will be easier if they have had an exceptional experience,</strong> in comparison to someone who is unfamiliar with your brand/company or had a sub-par experience. </p>
<p>“One issue in the vacation rental industry is that guests have a great deal of trust in the OTAs, but may never have booked directly with an operator before. Once the guest stays with you, we can eliminate the missing trust factor and, if marketed to properly, the guest will be much more likely to book again directly.”</p>

<h3 id="protecting-your-business">Are repeat guests risk-free bookings?</h3>

<h3 id="attracting-guests">6 tips you can use to help convert guests to repeat and direct bookers</h3>
<ol>
<li><strong>The guest experience:</strong> Look at the journey a guest goes through, from the moment they find your home and book, to the time that they return home. Be warm, welcoming and accommodating at every stage. If you know a child is staying or even a pet, leave little extras that will make their stay more comfortable. It really can be the little things that make all the difference.</li>
</ol>
<ol start="2">
<li><strong>Email marketing: </strong>Use short-term rental technology to<strong> </strong>capture email addresses from your guests and add them to marketing campaigns in Mailchimp or other email marketing software. A good email marketing strategy will automate emails so that you don’t have to worry about sending them – but you can easily track who has clicked through and booked as a result. </li>
</ol>
<ol start="3">
<li><strong>Multiple brand touchpoints before, during and after the stay: </strong>Reinforce your brand identity throughout the whole guest journey, including your website, social media, communication and in-home branded items (fridge magnets, coasters etc).</li>
</ol>
<ol start="4">
<li><strong>Loyalty programs</strong>: Offering exclusive benefits such as discounts, free upgrades, priority services, or other perks, loyalty programs create a sense of value and encourage guests to book again to take advantage of these rewards. For example, properties can provide extra rewards or discounts for guests who book multiple times within a specific period or reach certain milestones in their loyalty journey.</li>
</ol>
<ol start="5">
<li><strong>Stay active and up-to-date marketing your property on social media: </strong>Focus on growing an engaged community either through socials or community groups. Offer giveaway opportunities (the chance to win a free night etc) to help build social branding, reshares, reviews and your own community.</li>
</ol>
<ol start="6">
<li><strong>Communicate regularly with guests:</strong> Explore the potential of a Whatsapp business profile to engage with those in the ‘dreaming’ stage of their next holiday.</li>
</ol>
<h2>6 reasons why a guest won’t become a repeat booker</h2>
<ol>
<li><strong>Poor guest experience: </strong>The most common reason a guest won’t convert to a repeat booking is because of an average or poor experience. Travellers and guests want value for money – and they certainly don’t want to pay again for poor service.</li>
</ol>
<ol start="2">
<li><strong>No communication post-stay: </strong>If you’re trying to manually keep in touch with your guests before, during and after their stay, it’s going to be difficult to be super helpful and super responsive all the time – especially if you’re managing multiple properties at a time. Messaging automation and AI tools can help you keep your communication to a high standard.</li>
</ol>
<ol start="3">
<li><strong>Lack of cleanliness: </strong>A missed clean can really set you back. Ensure you’re utilising automated calendar scheduling with your short-term rental cleaners through a PMS, to not miss a clean.</li>
</ol>
<ol start="4">
<li><strong>Not visiting the area again: </strong>Sometimes, it is as simple as the guest not looking to visit the location again, rather than you missing the mark.</li>
</ol>
<ol start="5">
<li><strong>Lack of competitive advantage over others in the market, including OTAs and similar listings: </strong>This includes your pricing, availability, location and even <a href="https://superhog.com/must-have-airbnb-amenities/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">amenities</a>. </li>
</ol>
<ol start="6">
<li><strong>No optimised direct booking website:</strong> Make it as easy as possible for guests to book on your website, but make it secure. Guests need to feel secure when booking online. Are you telling your website visitors how their payments are completely safe? </li>
</ol>
