<p>Since 2007, the hotel industry disruptor, Airbnb, has well and truly fragmented the vacation rental sector. Airbnb caught fire, turning heads in the sphere and growing into the platform we know today. </p>
<p>But with <a href="https://news.airbnb.com/about-us/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">6 million active listings worldwide</a> and 4 million+ hosts and counting, how trustworthy can Airbnb truly be?</p>
<p>How do they monitor each and every listing? Do they <em>really</em> check every vacation rental for potential fraud? </p>
<p>The fraudulent Doll House listing showcased some pretty life-size concerns: is a 6ft water bottle really something you’d see every day?</p>

<h2><strong>What is The Doll House?</strong></h2>
<p>The Doll House is exactly that… a dolls house. One that features miniature furniture and interior design, but thanks to Youtubers Josh &#038; Archie and a few snaps later, the doll-sized rooms passed <strong>all</strong> Airbnb checks and became 1 of 6 million active listings on their vacation rental platform! </p>
<p>An active listing that quickly racked up bookings worth £3,000!</p>
<p>You can check out the process they took to get the dolls house live on Airbnb <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hropCxxehGo" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">here</a>.</p>
<p>Did you spot the water bottle? Did you spot the credit card ‘rug’ or even the airpod left on the piano?</p>

<h2><strong>Awareness of fraudulent activity</strong></h2>
<p>In such a rapidly growing industry with a global market size valued at USD $74.64 billion last year (2021) with a <a href="https://www.grandviewresearch.com/industry-analysis/vacation-rental-market" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">compound annual </a>growth rate of 5.3% from 2022 to 2030, the vacation rental industry is one that is susceptible to fraudulent activity. </p>
<p>Fraudulent hosts and fraudulent listings can be a cause of concern for both property managers and guests. </p>
<p>The Doll House scandal is proof of just one aspect of fraudulent activity in the industry. An aspect that can not only damage the trust guests have but can damage the reputation and authority of vacation rentals as a whole.</p>
<p>See just how easy it was to create a fraudulent listing on Airbnb.</p>

<h3><strong>The impact on hosts</strong></h3>
<p>Although the listing of The Doll House was a youtube experiment, it managed to shed light on how vacation rentals can be easily manipulated on the internet. </p>
<p>However, fraudulent listings aren’t always dollhouse schemes. In fact, the majority of fraudulent listings come from scammers duplicating listings without the knowledge of the real host. </p>
<p>Therefore, the impact of these types of fraud in the vacation rental industry doesn’t just affect guests, it’s hosts and property managers who suffer the consequences too! </p>
<p>What does this affect?</p>
<ul>
<li>Credibility can be waivered, diminishing the trust guests have with you, your listing, your business and even vacation rentals as a whole.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>Your business revenue</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>Your self-esteem and confidence in your business</li>
</ul>
<h3><strong>The impact on guests</strong></h3>

<ul>

<h2><strong>The solutions available</strong></h2>
<p>Vacation rental fraud is not to be taken lightly. Yes, it can be rare but it could also happen to anyone. </p>
<p>We all know a great photo sells better than lower-quality images. Make sure you watermark or copyright your images so that you reduce the risk of fraudulent listing duplications.</p>
<p>If you’re booking direct, ensure the website is ‘safe’ and ‘trusted’ – a padlock icon will display to the left of the website’s URL.</p>
