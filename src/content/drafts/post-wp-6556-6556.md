<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 10.0pt; margin-left: 0in; margin-right: 0in; margin-top: 10.0pt;">
<span style="font-family: " times new roman>We all know the links between top ratings<br />
and amount of money making potential a space has. Achieving top ratings as a<br />
host can be difficult, however, especially with so much competition and high<br />
guest standards to contend with. Ratings typically depend on key factors<br />
including the overall experience a guest has, cleanliness, accuracy, value,<br />
communication, arrival experience, and location. Here are a few ways you can up<br />
your game when it comes to a couple of these key factors: </span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 10.0pt;">
<b style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal;"><span style="font-family: " times new roman>Overall<br />
experience:</span></b></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 10.0pt;">
<span style="font-family: " times new roman>When it comes to the<br />
overall experience, think about what it might be like to arrive to your space<br />
with no prior knowledge of your neighborhood, home, culture, or space. Think of<br />
the experience of staying in your home as an moving event like a wedding with<br />
all the bells and whistles, instead of a stationary place. What would that full<br />
experience include? What is it like to walk to your space? Park for your space?<br />
Walk in the front door? How comfortable is your bed? Have you “tested” the full<br />
experience of staying in your space as a guest? Think about the last time you<br />
stayed at someone else’s home &#8211; what was missing? How did you feel? What would<br />
have made your stay better? Really analyzing your space from this perspective<br />
can help you create a full<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">  </span>five star<br />
experience from beginning to checkout. </span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 10.0pt;">
<span style="font-family: " times new roman>Sometimes little tokens<br />
also go a long way, such as welcoming signs, complimentary snacks, and extra<br />
pillows. Have a good coffee maker and leave a free supply of coffee for their<br />
first morning at least. If possible offer a Roku TV so guests can access their<br />
own YouTube, Amazon, and Netflix accounts. It is a good idea to learn about<br />
what other hosts are offering, and check our communities like <a href="https://www.facebook.com/freeproductsforhosts/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Free products for Hosts</a> to make it affordable to provide some of these amenities. </span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 10.0pt;">
<span style="font-family: " times new roman>You may not think of it<br />
as very important, but your tips can also make or break the experience your<br />
guest has in your area. Give tips about where locals go, brochures about local<br />
activities, a list of great cafes and which ones are close to the space, places<br />
with fire places or cool down spots, best restaurants or hot spots in popular<br />
nearby areas, best burger joints or wood-fired ovens, and new restaurants to<br />
check out, especially within walking distance. </span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 10.0pt;">
<b style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal;"><span style="font-family: " times new roman>Cleanliness:<br />
</span></b></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 10.0pt;">
<span style="font-family: " times new roman>This is probably the most<br />
criticized element of a vacation rental. You may think your cleanliness<br />
standards are high, but how can you get even better? Sometimes the quality of<br />
the items in your space can make all the difference. Towels and bed sheets are<br />
especially key. Research tips online to keep them fluffy or smooth and<br />
spotless. You can also create a checklist to make sure you don’t miss anything.<br />
In the end, sometimes going with highly-rated professional cleaners can make it<br />
worth your money, because the higher your ratings the more money you are likely<br />
to make.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">  </span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 10.0pt;">
<b style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal;"><span style="font-family: " times new roman>Communication:</span></b></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 10.0pt;">
<span style="font-family: " times new roman>Hosts who achieve<br />
superhost status or 5 stars are often amazing communicators both online and in<br />
person. Most are available 24-7, never miss any messages, and find a systematic<br />
way to stay in touch, like using the listing platform app. Always communicate over<br />
the platform you have your listing on so that there is a record (just in case)<br />
of what is said. When you provide check-in instructions have a friend or two<br />
test them out to make sure everything is crystal clear. Don’t forget to tell<br />
people how to turn the TV on, access wifi, or work any other device (i.e. heat)<br />
in your space. These small elements can make a huge difference to the comfort<br />
and ease of a guests’ stay. Overall, make everything easy and concise for<br />
guests by providing self-checkin, simple house rules in the original listing, a<br />
folder with wifi and any remote control directions. </span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 10.0pt;">
<span style="font-family: " times new roman>Also know how to tailor<br />
your communication, and the frequency of it, to your guests. Experienced<br />
younger travelers frequently don’t want very much interaction with hosts. Most<br />
often this demographic will arrive and never communicate after self checkin.<br />
Older, less experienced Airbnb guests may want to converse more, and so you<br />
will be able to meet their needs better if you understand individualized<br />
traveler types.</span></div>
