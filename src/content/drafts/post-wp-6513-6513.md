<p>Do you enjoy beach vacations and fun activities in the water? Saint Martin boasts 37 beaches, many of them ranked among the top-rated in the Caribbean. If you’re looking for pristine beaches, a lively nightlife, and plenty of opportunities to have fun in the sea, Saint Martin is a great destination.</p>
</div>
<div class="MsoSubtitle">
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
</div>
<p><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US;"><b>Surfing</b><o:p></o:p></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US;">The geographic<br />
position of Saint Martin on the edge of the Caribbean Islands makes many of its<br />
beaches ideal for surfing. The best season for surfing in Saint Martin is<br />
November through April, but the Atlantic coastline offers good conditions<br />
year-round. </span>Some beaches are ideal<br />
for surfers of all abilities, but others are only suitable for experts. Le<br />
Galion, south of Orient Bay, has a reef and coral beach that makes it suitable<br />
for all surfers. <span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US;">Wilderness is another<br />
beach suitable for all surfers. However, it is secluded and offers no services.<br />
It is also a 30-minute hike from the parking bay. When you get there, you’ll<br />
love the pristine pink sand, and Wilderness boasts the finest conditions for<br />
all abilities. </span>Experienced surfers<br />
can head to Cupecoy or Mullet Bay. Mullet Bay offers more services.</p>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">

<p><b>Windsurfing</b></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US;">Many beaches in Saint<br />
Martin are suitable for windsurfing. However, most of the locals head to Le<br />
Galion. The Le Galion Lagoon is perfect for beginners, and there are nice wave<br />
breaks for more experienced surfers on Le Galion Reef.</span>For a unique<br />
windsurfing experience, check out Maho Bay. The large jets taking-off from<br />
Juliana Airport generate a 40-knot offshore gust for one minute, providing<br />
windsurfers with a unique kick! Some of the other great windsurfing beaches<br />
include Cove Bay for beginners and Meads Bay and Barnes Bay for more experienced<br />
windsurfers.</div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
</div>
<div class="MsoSubtitle">
<span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US;"><b>Kite Surfing<o:p></o:p></b></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US;">Nettle Bay Lagoon is a<br />
wonderful stretch of calm water that is perfect for beginners. On the Caribbean<br />
side of Nettle Bay, the trade winds are ideal for more experienced kite<br />
surfers.<o:p></o:p></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US;">Orient Beach is the<br />
other popular kite surfing site on the island. It offers a flat and clear<br />
lagoon, sand bar and waves, and open ocean. There are three kite surfing schools<br />
in Saint Martin, two based in Nettle Bay and one in Orient Beach. <o:p></o:p></span></div>

<p><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US;"><b>Snorkeling</b><o:p></o:p></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US;">The warm,<br />
crystal-clear waters around Saint Martin are perfect for snorkeling adventures.<br />
Arguably the best snorkeling spot in Saint Martin is around Pinel Island on the<br />
French side. You can reach Pinel Island by a short ferry trip from Cul de Sac. What’s<br />
great about Pinel Island is that it’s at the heart of a protected Marine<br />
Reserve. </span>On the south side of<br />
the island, you’ll find the Pinel Island Marine Trail. This is a snorkeling<br />
trail marked with white buoys that leads you safely along the reef. The waters<br />
here are rich in marine life, including turtles, stingrays, and colorful reef<br />
fish. Other popular<br />
snorkeling destinations on the Dutch side are Cupecoy Beach, Dawn Beach, and<br />
Mullet Bay. And on the French side, there are Baie Rouge and Creole Rock.</div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US;"><br /></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<b>Scuba Diving</b></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US;">The coral reefs and<br />
crystal-clear waters make Saint Martin a fantastic location for scuba diving. Several<br />
equipment hire shops around the island can provide you with </span><span lang="EN-GB"><a href="https://www.outsidepursuits.com/best-scuba-mask-reviews/" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><span lang="EN-US" style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US;">diving mask</span></a></span><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US;">, flippers, and tanks, such as The Scuba Shop<br />
in Simpson Bay. However, for me, it’s the seven popular shipwrecks around the<br />
island that make the island perfect for scuba diving. </span><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US;">For example, HMS<br />
Proselyte was a 32-gun frigate serving in the British Royal Navy that hit a reef<br />
and sank in 1801. She now lies under 50 feet of water near Philipsburg. Sundry<br />
anchors, barrel hoops, ballast bars, and cannons remain scattered around the<br />
wreck, encrusted with coral. These features make this ancient warship a popular<br />
destination for scuba diving enthusiasts. </span>A less historic but<br />
still interesting shipwreck is the Carib Cargo off Cay Bay. She can be found 65<br />
feet under the waves on a sandy patch of the seabed. What’s interesting about<br />
this 1996 wreck is that stingrays love it. Because of the abundance of marine<br />
life and clear water, the Carib Cargo has become a popular destination for underwater<br />
photographers. <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;"> </span><span style="mso-spacerun: yes;"> </span></div>

<p><b>Kayaking and<br />
Stand-up Paddleboarding</b></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US;">Lots of visitors to<br />
Saint Martin have fun paddling around the bays or lagoons. The calm lagoons are<br />
perfect for beginners, while sea kayaking can provide more of a challenge for<br />
the adventurous. If you’re confident enough to paddle out to sea, you can reach<br />
some of the nearer shipwrecks and look down through the crystal-clear waters to<br />
the coral-encrusted ships below. </span>Kayaking is also an<br />
interesting way to reach the Marine Reserve at Pinel Island. It takes<br />
approximately 25 minutes to paddle from Cul de Sac. The water around the<br />
reserve is shallow, so you can look down and see the abundant marine life<br />
below. <span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US;">If you’re a nature<br />
lover, head over to Galion Beach where the surfers go. At the end of the bay is<br />
a wild mangrove. You’ll be delighted at the diversity of water plants, crabs,<br />
and birds all around you. And the calm lagoon behind the beach is great for<br />
paddling a </span><span lang="EN-GB"><a href="https://www.outsidepursuits.com/best-inflatable-paddle-board/" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><span lang="EN-US" style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US;">stand-up paddleboard</span></a></span><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US;">.</span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
</div>
<div class="MsoSubtitle">
<span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US;"><b>Fine Dining</b><o:p></o:p></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US;">Visitors who wish to<br />
combine their trip to the beach with gourmet food should head on down to Grand<br />
Case Bay. This quaint fishing village features a long beach with a selection of<br />
gourmet restaurants and small but popular traditional food outlets. The<br />
turquoise waters of the bay are clear and calm, and Grand Case is rated the<br />
TripAdvisor #3 destination in the Caribbean. <o:p></o:p></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
</div>
<div class="MsoSubtitle">
<span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US;"><b>Partying</b><o:p></o:p></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US;">Orient Bay in the<br />
northeastern part of the island is not only one of the most popular beaches<br />
during the day, but it is also a popular hotspot at night. With a naturist<br />
beach to the south of the bay, there is a libertine atmosphere around town. The<br />
beach restaurants and bars here are where you’re most likely to spot visiting<br />
celebrities. It’s commonly called the Saint Tropez of the Caribbean. <o:p></o:p></span><br />
