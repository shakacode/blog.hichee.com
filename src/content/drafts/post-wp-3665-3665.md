<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Is a vacation rental the right choice?</h2>



<p>Before we look at the various options regarding Airbnb, Booking (.com), VRBO, and the like, I just want to mention&#8230; Hotels.<br><br>There are certain instances where a hotel may be a better option.</p>



<p>If you are looking for a one or two-night stay, you may well get a better deal if you opt for a hotel instead of a short-term rental. Part of the price of any stay is the cleaning cost. Short-term rental hosts generally charge a cleaning fee (even if it&#8217;s absorbed into the overall price), and that fee will apply if you stay for one night or 7 nights. So the cleaning fee can be disproportionately high for a one or two-night stay.</p>



<p>Hotels, on the other hand, have full-time cleaning staff, and this overhead is divided by the number of rooms, so the costs per guest are generally lower.<br><br>Now, some of you will prefer the benefits of a rental as you often have the use of a kitchen. If you plan to prepare your own meals, the money saved compared to eating out can offset accommodation costs.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Space, Privacy, and Uniqueness</h2>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img decoding="async" width="64" height="64" src="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/vacation-rentals-offer-more-room-and-privacy.png" alt="" class="wp-image-9921"/></figure>



<p>As we all know, hotel rooms, in general, tend to be small and somewhat cookie-cutter in design. <br>Access is through shared spaces, and walls can be thin in some cases, so privacy can be an issue.</p>



<p>Short-term rentals come in all shapes and sizes. Individual rooms in shared houses can have privacy issues; some may even feel insecure. But whole rentals, where you rent an entire property, are generally larger than hotel rooms, and they have a degree of added privacy.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Location</h2>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img decoding="async" width="64" height="64" src="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/10/4975305_google_google-map_location_location-place_map_icon.png" alt="Location" class="wp-image-3818"/></figure>



<p>The location also comes into play here. Cheaper rentals and hotels can be found out of town, but transport costs and time spent traveling can soon add up. A centrally located rental or hotel will be more expensive, but the added convenience of being near attractions without paying for taxis or public transport could be a bonus.</p>



<p>When it comes to comparing prices, location can play a big part in the price. </p>



<p>Even for condos in the same block, with the exact same footprint, one with a sea view will often be more expensive than one without. </p>



<p>These are things that will need to be factored in before booking your accommodation.</p>

<h1 class="wp-block-heading">Reviews for stays</h1>



<p>Reviews are critical to help build trust on Airbnb—they’re an important way for Hosts and guests to give each other feedback, and help our community make informed decisions and understand what to expect when making plans. <h2 class="wp-block-heading">Who can leave a review on Airbnb</h2>



<p>Hosts and guests can only leave reviews for stays that have been booked and paid for on Airbnb.</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading">When can reviews be submitted and published</h2>



<p>Once checkout is complete, Hosts and guests have 14 days to submit their review. Guests and Hosts can also leave a review for certain reservations that are canceled on or after the day of check-in (12:00 AM in the listing’s timezone).</p>



<p>Reviews are only posted after:</p>



<ol>
<li>Both parties have submitted their reviews, or</li>



<li>The 14-day period has ended—whichever comes first.</li>
</ol>



<p>If a Host cancels a reservation before the day of check-in, the Host and guest won’t be able to leave a review for the stay.</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Are reviews moderated?</h2>



<p>Airbnb doesn’t moderate reviews before they’re published, and reviews are encouraged to be transparent.</p>



<p>However, reviews do need to be honest and relate to a genuine reservation and may be removed if they violate our&nbsp;<a href="https://www.airbnb.com/help/article/2673/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">Reviews Policy</a>. In short, this means that a review may be removed if:</p>



<ul>
<li>The review violates&nbsp;<a href="https://www.airbnb.com/help/article/546/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">Airbnb’s Content Policy</a>—for example, it includes private or confidential information, discriminatory, violent or threatening content</li>



<li>The review is biased—for example because the guest has been incentivized to leave a positive review in return for a free or discounted stay, or where there are signals that the reservation was made for the sole purpose of inflating a person’s overall rating</li>



<li>The review is irrelevant to the author’s experience on Airbnb—for example, because it doesn’t contain firsthand information about the Host, guest, or stay that would help other community members make an informed booking or hosting decisions</li>
</ul>



<p>We also have a detection system that proactively looks for signs that a review may not relate to a genuine stay. If our system detects that a review is likely to be fake—for example, because it doesn’t relate to a genuine stay on Airbnb or because it relates to a stay that was solely made to inflate a person’s ratings—it’ll be removed.</p>
