<h3><span class="ez-toc-section" id="International_Travel_Mistakes_To_Avoid_1"></span><span>International Travel Mistakes To Avoid # 1</span><span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>
<p><strong>Failing To Check The Expiry Date On Your Passport</strong></p>
<p><a href="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/102810_1844_Whattraveld1.jpg" data-rel="lightbox-image-1" data-rl_title data-rl_caption><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" class="aligncenter wp-image-261 size-full" title src="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/102810_1844_Whattraveld1.jpg" alt="travel documents" width="280" height="297" /></a></p>
<p><span>This is perhaps one of the most common mistakes people make, when getting ready to go on vacation. </span></p>
<p>They forget to check the expiry date on their 🛂 passport and/or on any other necessary documents, such as a – travel tourist visa. </p>
<p>In most cases, your 📰 passport must be valid for at least 6 months beyond the period of your extended stay, in the country you will be visiting.</p>
<p><span>Unfortunately, this has happened to friends of ours in the past. They were all set to head out for a 2 week 🏝 tropical vacation, only to discover when they arrived at the ✈ airport to check in, their passports had expired – 3 months previously. 😢</span></p>
<p>What a horrible way to ruin a long awaited vacation!</p>
<p><span><b>* Check the expiry dates on all your necessary documents, well in advance of your travel date.*</b></span></p>
<h3><span class="ez-toc-section" id="What_We_Should_NOT_Do_When_Travelling_2"></span><span>What We Should NOT Do When Travelling # 2</span><span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>
<p><strong>NOT Purchasing Travel Insurance</strong></p>
<p><a href="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/travel-insurance-3.jpg" data-rel="lightbox-image-2" data-rl_title data-rl_caption><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" class="aligncenter wp-image-19925 size-full" title src="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/travel-insurance-3.jpg" alt="travel insurance" width="600" height="315" srcset="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/travel-insurance-3.jpg 600w, https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/travel-insurance-300x158-1.jpg 300w" sizes="(max-width: 600px) 100vw, 600px" /></a></p>
<p><span>I’ve said this time and time again, if you can afford to travel, you can afford travel insurance. Be sure to check out my previous articles here on – travel insurance. </span></p>
<p>They can be found at – 📰 <a href="https://gr8traveltips.com/cheap-travel-insurance/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><i>Cheapest Insurance Not Always Best</i></a><span> and </span>📰 <a href="https://gr8traveltips.com/travel-insurance-tips-for-purchasing/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><i>Travel Insurance Coverage Tips</i></a><span> to learn the ins and outs of purchasing insurance for your protection.</span></p>
<p><a href="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/travel-vacation-injury.jpg" data-rel="lightbox-image-3" data-rl_title data-rl_caption><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" class="aligncenter wp-image-19936 size-full" title src="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/travel-vacation-injury.jpg" alt="travel vacation injury" width="590" height="443" srcset="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/travel-vacation-injury.jpg 590w, https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/travel-vacation-injury-300x225-1.jpg 300w" sizes="(max-width: 590px) 100vw, 590px" /></a></p>
<p><span>Don’t ever think nothing will ever happen to you or your family when traveling. It’s always when you least expect it, that something will happen. </span></p>
<p>Years ago, I almost broke my neck 🏄 wave boarding while on vacation in the 🏝 Dominican Republic! </p>
<p>Without travel medical insurance and should I have been hospitalized, it could have cost me 💰 thousands of dollars a day, out of my own pocket.</p>
<p><span><b>* In many countries, if you can’t prove you have insurance or the money to cover your bills, they won’t even admit you to 🏥 hospital or even just to receive ⚕ medical treatment.</b></span></p>
<h3><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Biggest_Travel_Problems_3"></span><span>Biggest Travel Problems</span><span> # 3</span><span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>
<p><strong>Overpacking or Packing Incorrectly</strong></p>
<p><a href="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/Overpacking-For-Vacation.jpg" data-rel="lightbox-image-4" data-rl_title data-rl_caption><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" class="aligncenter wp-image-19937 size-full" title src="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/Overpacking-For-Vacation.jpg" alt="Overpacking For Vacation" width="600" height="400" srcset="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/Overpacking-For-Vacation.jpg 600w, https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/Overpacking-For-Vacation-300x200-1.jpg 300w" sizes="(max-width: 600px) 100vw, 600px" /></a></p>
<p><span>I continue to be amazed by the amount people 🧳 pack for a typical – two week vacation. I’ve seen people check 🧳 multiple bags along with carry-on for their vacation. </span></p>
<p>The old saying goes among experts; </p>
<blockquote><p><center><i>“Only pack what you are – Sure to Wear, and then still remove half from your luggage.”</i></center></p></blockquote>
<p><span>It’s ok to wear your travel wear more than once when traveling. Most hotels and destinations do offer clothes 🧺 washing for your convenience. </span></p>
<p>My son and I traveled throughout India and Vietnam a few seasons ago. We had no trouble getting our clothes washed wherever we were.</p>
<p><span>Why have to lug around heavy luggage when there’s no need to, especially when airlines have strict weight rules and extra charges for overweight and extra baggage!</span></p>
<h3><span class="ez-toc-section" id="What_NOT_To_Do_As_A_Tourist_4"></span><span>What NOT To Do As A Tourist # 4</span><span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>
<p><strong>Failing To Mix With The Locals</strong></p>
<p><a href="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/Varanasi-India-Local.jpg" data-rel="lightbox-image-5" data-rl_title data-rl_caption><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" class="aligncenter wp-image-18658 size-full" title src="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/Varanasi-India-Local.jpg" alt="Varanasi India Local" width="600" height="305" srcset="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/Varanasi-India-Local.jpg 600w, https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/Varanasi-India-Local-300x153-1.jpg 300w" sizes="(max-width: 600px) 100vw, 600px" /></a></p>
<p><span>This is something that really 🤔 puzzles me as well, when traveling. </span></p>
<p>Isn’t the whole travel experience to new exciting destinations around the world, is to meet and mix with the locals and experience their culture? ❓</p>
<p>Don’t be afraid to approach locals and start a conversation. You will be amazed at what you can learn and discover, by mixing and enjoying interacting with the local people.  </p>
<p><span>We’ve discovered over the years of travel that it most cases, local people are eager to talk with foreigners, and learn more about where you’re from!</span></p>
<h3><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Worst_Travel_Mistakes_5"></span><span>Worst Travel Mistakes # 5</span><span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>
<p><strong>Failing To Notify Credit Card / Debit Card Institutions</strong></p>
<p><a href="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/credit-cards.jpg" data-rel="lightbox-image-6" data-rl_title data-rl_caption><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" class="aligncenter wp-image-7630 size-full" title src="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/credit-cards.jpg" alt="credit card theft" width="425" height="283" srcset="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/credit-cards.jpg 425w, https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/credit-cards-300x200-1.jpg 300w" sizes="(max-width: 425px) 100vw, 425px" /></a></p>
<p><span>Not a huge problem any more, but if you’re going to be away for extended periods of time, it’s a good practice to notify your 🏦 financial institutions. </span></p>
<p>You certainly don’t want your 💳 credit or debit cards blocked while away, especially when you’re trying to access cash from an 🏧 ATM.</p>
<p><span>Recently, I was told by one credit card company and to my surprise, that it was no longer necessary to notify them. </span></p>
<p>I guess unless there is some very unusual activity taking place with your card, they won’t block them anymore.❓ </p>
<p><span>Should they for some reason become blocked, you can always call their toll free customer service number found on the back of the cards.</span></p>
<h3><span class="ez-toc-section" id="How_NOT_To_Travel_6"></span><span>How NOT To Travel # 6</span><span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>
<p><strong>Not Sampling The Local Cuisine</strong></p>
<p><a href="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/Hello-Hong-Kong-Food-Tour.jpg" data-rel="lightbox-image-7" data-rl_title data-rl_caption><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" class="aligncenter wp-image-19491 size-full" title src="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/Hello-Hong-Kong-Food-Tour.jpg" alt="Hello Hong Kong Food Tour" width="600" height="615" srcset="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/Hello-Hong-Kong-Food-Tour.jpg 600w, https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/Hello-Hong-Kong-Food-Tour-293x300-1.jpg 293w, https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/Hello-Hong-Kong-Food-Tour-585x600-1.jpg 585w" sizes="(max-width: 600px) 100vw, 600px" /></a></p>
<p><span>This is one of the things I absolutely love to do, when traveling to new destinations!  </span></p>
<p>We thoroughly enjoy 🚶 Walking Food Tours when traveling, getting a chance to learn and experience the local cuisine. </p>
<p>Taking a guided food tour is the safest and most enjoyable way, to sample some of the local dishes. Many of the most popular eating establishments attended by locals in the area, that you would be hard-pressed to find on your own. </p>
