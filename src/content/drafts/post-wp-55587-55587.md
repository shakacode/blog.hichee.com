<h2><span data-color="transparent">Taxes in Arizona: What Can You Expect?</span></h2>
<p><span data-color="transparent">In the state of Arizona, Airbnb guests will need to pay the following taxes when they book a vacation rental for stays shorter than 30 days:</span></p>
<ul>
<li><span data-color="transparent">An Arizona Transaction Privilege Tax of 5.5% of the listing price (inclusive of cleaning fees)</span></li>
<li><span data-color="transparent">A county excise tax that can be anything from 0.28% to 6.5% of the listing price (inclusive of cleaning fees) </span></li>
</ul>
<p><span data-color="transparent">In addition to these taxes, guests will also be charged one or more local transient occupancy taxes that can include:</span></p>
<ul>
<li><span data-color="transparent">A Transaction Privilege Tax</span></li>
<li><span data-color="transparent">Transient Lodging Tax</span></li>
<li><span data-color="transparent">Bed Tax</span></li>
<li><span data-color="transparent">Hotel-motel Tax</span></li>
</ul>
<p><span data-color="transparent">The amount varies from one city to the next, but generally it ranges between 1.5% and 6%. </span></p>
<h2><span data-color="transparent">Short-term Rental Regulations in Arizona: What Does the Law Say?</span></h2>
<p><span data-color="transparent">As with other short-term rental markets in the United States, the regulations differ from one state to the next. On top of that, each city can also have its own local rules that you’ll need to plan around. </span></p>
<p><span data-color="transparent">For example, in Tucson, you’ll need to have a valid business license. As for regulations, there’s the Unified Development Code and the Land Use Code. Hosts who’ve owned their property since 2012 have the option of choosing which one of these codes they want to follow. </span></p>
<p><span data-color="transparent">Aside from these codes, you’ll also need to keep in mind the building and housing standards. Here, you’ll find more guidelines about minimum construction, design, and maintenance regarding residential and non-residential use. </span></p>
<p><span data-color="transparent">In Phoenix, the picture is similar. While no specific mention is made of a business license, all vacation rentals must be registered with the city. Upon successful registration, the city will then provide a vacation rental registration number. </span></p>
<p><span data-color="transparent">You’ll also need to adhere to the Short-term Vacation Rental Ordinance. Failure to do this can result in hefty penalties, starting at a few hundred dollars for the first offense.  </span></p>
