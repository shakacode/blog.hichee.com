<h2 class="uagb-heading-text"><strong>Top 10 guest damages Superhog team covered in 2024</strong></h2>
<p>Most damage is fairly small and caused by guests being careless or not following your house rules. </p>
<p>You can cover the cost of <a href="https://superhog.com/guides/count-small-guest-damage-expenses/">small guest damage</a> using our <a href="https://knowyourguest.superhog.com/damage-waiver/">damage waiver</a>, which allows you to collect a small, non-refundable fee from each guest which waives their liability. </p>
<p>So, what items do guests most commonly break in your Airbnb?</p>
<p>Here are the most common incidents, according to Tanya.</p>
</p>
<h3 class="uagb-heading-text">Make-up-smeared linens and towels</h3>
<p>Tanya says stained linens and towels are among the most frequently occurring guest damages.</p>
<p>Tanya explains that “<em>Makeup, hair dye, and fake tan</em>” were all common culprits for ruining towels and sheets.</p>
<p>She adds that it’s a common practice for the hosts to change their linens every year, but Superhog covers the cost of replacing stained linens sooner in no time compared to the OTAs.</p>
</p>
<h3 class="uagb-heading-text">Pets behaving badly </h3>
<p>Property managers also face damage caused by pets. Dogs and other pets may scratch furniture, break vases, or leave excessive mess. </p>
<p>“<em>We can only cover damage caused by unauthorized pets</em>,” she clarifies. </p>
<p>If the guests haven’t obtained permission to bring pets, the property manager can claim for damage caused.</p>
</p>
<h3 class="uagb-heading-text">Broken locks</h3>
<p>Fixing a damaged lock is always a property manager’s first priority. </p>
<p>Many property managers reported guests losing keys, not returning them after their stay, or breaking the locks with force.“ In case of a broken lock, we’ll need to see a report from a locksmith to prove that the damage is caused by guests, not just wear and tear,” Tanya explains.</p>
</p>
<h3 class="uagb-heading-text">Smoking indoors</h3>
<p>Smoking inside a property can be a common complaint, though Tanya explains that hosts must be able to show concrete proof that smoking occurred.</p>
<p>“Ashes or discarded cigarettes in the rubbish, for example,” she says.</p>
<p>If a host can prove that the guest smoked, Superhog tends to cover Ozone cleaning to eliminate the smell.</p>
</p>
<h3 class="uagb-heading-text">Wrecked beds</h3>
<p>Tanya deals with many bed-related incidents, including broken bed frames, torn upholstery on the headboard, and stains on the mattress.</p>
</p>
<h3 class="uagb-heading-text">Patching carpet catastrophes</h3>
<p>Carpet stains are another common complaint, as well as damage to hard flooring. </p>
<p>Often, Superhog will cover the cost of fixing the damage rather than replacing the entire carpet. </p>
<p>Tanya explains that Superhog works closely with specialist repair services to fix the damage without redoing the whole carpet or floor.</p>
</p>
<h3 class="uagb-heading-text">Smashed TVs</h3>
<p>TVs are easily broken.</p>
<p>“You can spill liquid on the electronics, or you can crack or even completely smash a screen,” Tanya says. </p>
<p>Superhog will generally cover the cost of repairing the TV.</p>
</p>
<h3 class="uagb-heading-text">Malfunctioning hot tubs</h3>
<p>Hot tubs are one of the top, <a href="https://superhog.com/blog/must-have-airbnb-amenities/">must-have amenities for Airbnb</a> that can convince guests to book your property over competitors.</p>
<p>Tanya explains that hot tubs are susceptible to damage.</p>
<p>“<em>Sand or stones can get into the tub and quickly damage the filter. Sometimes, guests play around with settings, which can lead to problems with the pump</em>.”</p>
<p>Because hot tubs are so popular, property managers must get any issues fixed quickly.</p>
</p>
<h3 class="uagb-heading-text">Blocked toilets</h3>
<p>Blocked toilets are unpleasant for both guests and hosts. </p>
<p>“<em>It can be hard to hold guests liable for a blocked toilet because they can blame it on bad plumbing or a build-up over time,</em>” said Tanya. </p>
<p>Some property managers are lucky, though. Tanya explained that if you’ve recently had the plumbing checked, it would be easier to prove that your guests caused the problem.</p>
</p>
<h3 class="uagb-heading-text">Maintenance issues</h3>
<p>Finally, all kinds of maintenance issues with appliances are a common source of trouble. </p>
<p>Garbage disposal units are especially tricky. Guests often put too much into the garbage disposal, or insert things that aren’t supposed to go there.  </p>
<p>Leaks and floods are another typical problem, which may or may not be the guests’ fault.</p>
<p>It’s important to explain how to use appliances clearly to reduce the chances of damage.</p>

<h2 class="uagb-heading-text"><strong>5 of the wildest incidents Superhog has dealt with </strong></h2>
<p>While most guest damage is mundane, occasionally, the incidents team comes across absurd stories of guests acting up. </p>
<p>Here are some of the most bizarre reports Tanya and her team have dealt with over the years.</p>
</p>
<h3 class="uagb-heading-text">New Year’s Eve party</h3>
<p>Hosts should have heard alarm bells ringing when a 22-year-old rented a huge property, in his hometown, for one night – New Year’s Eve!</p>
<p>“<em>It’s down to hosts to look at the guest screening report and weigh up the pros and cons of accepting such a high-risk booking</em>,” said Tanya. </p>
<p>The property managers were left with thousands of dollars worth of damage, however, they weren’t penalised by Superhog for not spotting the red flags.</p>
</p>
<h3 class="uagb-heading-text">Bite mark in an ornamental apple</h3>
<p>“<em>A property manager tried to claim for a damaged ornamental apple with a bite mark on it!</em>”</p>
<p>Tanya explained that this bizarre incident was rejected after listing photos showed that the bite mark had been there before the guest arrived.</p>
</p>
<h3 class="uagb-heading-text">Truck destroys the garden</h3>
<p>Security cameras at a vacation rental property caught a man reversing a truck out of the driveway, destroying the garden in the process. “<em>It was all caught on video,</em>” laughs Tanya. “<em>The guy was struggling to reverse his truck and tore up the whole garden, smashing up rocks and stones</em>.”</p>
</p>
<h3 class="uagb-heading-text">Hosts accuse guests of running a brothel </h3>
<p>A couple of years ago, hosts accused the guest of running a brothel.</p>
<p>“<em>It wasn’t used as a brothel it was just the guests’ friends paying them frequent visits</em>,” says Tanya. </p>
<p>However, this case shows how it can sometimes be difficult to know the difference between parties and illicit activity, and guests innocently inviting local friends to the property during their stay.</p>
</p>
<h3 class="uagb-heading-text">Treasured tree cut down</h3>
<p>Mature Japanese Maples are rare and beautiful trees with colour-changing foliage.</p>
<p>Property managers at one vacation home were aghast when they had to cut down this stunning tree in their backyard due to guest damage.</p>
</p>
<h2 class="uagb-heading-text"><strong>Wear and tear vs guest damage</strong></h2>
<p>Before we get into the most common incidents, knowing the difference between <a href="https://superhog.com/blog/guest-damage-prevention-strategy/">guest damage in vacation rentals</a> and normal wear and tear can help you avoid unnecessary disputes. </p>
<p>When you rent your property regularly, you’ll experience some wear and tear, and will occasionally need to replace things.</p>
<p>However, when guests are careless and ignore your house rules, you can face the unexpected cost of fixing or replacing broken items. </p>
<p>Superhog’s damage protection plan, like most damage coverage packages, doesn’t cover standard wear and tear, but it does protect against accidental or deliberate damage to your property.</p>
