<h2 class="uagb-heading-text"><strong>Can you spot troublesome guests in advance?</strong></h2>
<p>Unfortunately, there’s no guaranteed way to spot guests most likely to cause damage.</p>
<div class="wp-block-uagb-image uagb-block-07bdb5b5 wp-block-uagb-image--layout-default wp-block-uagb-image--effect-static wp-block-uagb-image--align-none">

</div>
<p>There are some red flags to watch out for, such as:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Bad guest reviews</strong> — This is the most reliable indicator that guests may cause trouble, so check reviews. Hosting guests with no previous reviews can also be risky.</li>
<li><strong>Bending the rules</strong> — Guests who try to dispute your house rules or convince you to make an exception (for example, to allow pets if you have a policy against it) may be problematic.</li>
<li><strong>Payment difficulties</strong> — When guests don’t pay on time, their cards get declined, or they want to pay through alternative methods, this could mean trouble.</li>
<li><strong>Odd-looking email address</strong> — A strange email address with random numbers could be created for the purpose of creating a new Airnb account, therefore might be suspicious. </li>
<li><strong>Poor grammar</strong> — If your guest has difficulty communicating or uses “text” language, this could indicate a scammer. However, this might be because your guests are communicating in a second language.</li>
<li><strong>Live locally</strong> — If your guest’s home address is nearby, this should raise your suspicions. They might be looking to rent a property to throw a party.</li>
</ul>

<h2 class="uagb-heading-text"><strong>Does Airbnb cover damages?</strong></h2>
<p>When you list on Airbnb, you’ll be covered by the OTA’s Aircover insurance. This cover is free of charge and available for all hosts.</p>
<p>Aircover includes:</p>
<ul>
<li>Up to $3M damage protection, with special cover for art &#038; valuables, auto &#038; boat, damage caused by pets, loss of income, and deep cleaning.</li>
<li>$1M liability insurance to cover guest injury, damage, or theft of guest property.</li>
</ul>
<p>Learn more about <a href="https://www.airbnb.com/aircover-for-hosts">what Aircover offers</a>.  </p>

<h2 class="uagb-heading-text"><strong>What happens if a guest damages your property?</strong></h2>
<p>When John’s housekeeping team entered the house, they were shocked at the damage they found. </p>
<p>The guests had sneaked dogs into the property without paying the pet fee, and there was dog mess everywhere. Trash cans were overflowing, towels and bedsheets were badly stained, and appliances like hairdryers were missing. </p>
<p>“<em>There was dirt everywhere. Every single pair of sheets was destroyed, all the towels were damaged – it was very disrespectful</em>.”</p>
<p>Airbnb isn’t the quickest way to get Airbnb damage covered. First, you have to document the before and after to prove that the damage happened during the stay. Then, you have to keep in mind the strict time frames. Airbnb takes a week to a month to process your claim so make sure you’re not out cash.</p>
<p>If you ever find yourself in a similar situation with Airbnb damage, here’s what to do: </p>
<ul>
<li>Document the damage by taking photos and videos. Get testimonials from cleaning staff if they were first to enter the property.</li>
<li>Communicate with guests directly through Airbnb to see if they are willing to pay for damages caused.</li>
<li>If this fails and guests refuse to pay Airbnb damages, submit a claim through Airbnb’s Resolution Center. Select “Request compensation for damages” and fill out all the details.</li>
</ul>
<p>You have 14 days to report an incident to Airbnb, and the sooner you report, the more chance you have of being able to claim damages.</p>
<p>After you’ve submitted the request, guests will have 3 days to pay. If you don’t receive the money, you’ll need to contact Airbnb support. You must do this within 30 days of the damage.</p>
<p>John decided to report the incident to Airbnb immediately to give himself the best chance of winning a claim, and help prevent the same thing happening to other hosts. </p>
