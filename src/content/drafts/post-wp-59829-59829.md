<p><strong>As a property manager or host, you need to make informed decisions about who you’re letting into your property to keep your community safe. Host requirements are changing along with short-term rental regulations and legislation, so Know Your Guest by Superhog now provides the tools to perform an up-to-date Sex Offender Background Check on guests. </strong></p>
<p>With our risk management platform, you can host safely and prevent a registered sex offender from staying in your holiday rental home. </p>
<p>In this product article, we’ll explain <a href="https://superhog.com/blog/sex-offender-background-check/#how-it-works">how our sex offender product works</a>, <a href="https://superhog.com/blog/sex-offender-background-check/#setup">how to set it up</a> and how it can <a href="https://superhog.com/blog/sex-offender-background-check/#why-SO-checks">help property managers and hosts</a> accept bookings confidently. </p>
<h2 class="wp-block-heading">The ever-tightening legal requirements for vacation rentals </h2>
<p>While sex offender checks may not be legally mandated in all areas, many responsible property managers voluntarily adopt these checks as part of their due diligence to ensure the safety and security of their guests and the surrounding community. </p>
<p>In a world where short-term rentals are being put under more scrutiny, it is important for property managers to keep abreast of any legal developments, or changes in their jurisdiction, that may impact guest screening requirements or obligations.</p>
<p>We recommend consulting with local authorities or signing up with short-term rental associations to ensure compliance with applicable laws and regulations and adopting best practices in screening guests for vacation rental properties.</p>
<p><strong>Short-term rental regulation discussions are currently at the forefront of industry news and changes to come. </strong>Formulating a compliance strategy will ensure the safe and successful operation of your short-term rental business. </p>
<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="why-SO-checks">Why should property managers consider implementing sex offender checks into their screening journey? </h2>
<p>With the continued rise of the use of short-term rentals, tighter restrictions and regulations will continue to come into play regarding the safety of communities.</p>
<p>Take Arizona. As of February 2023,<strong> </strong><a href="https://www.scottsdaleaz.gov/news/city-council-adopts-new-rules-for-short-term-rentals" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener"><strong>it is against the law for you, as a property manager or host, to continue with a booking if the lead guest is a suspected match on the sex offender register</strong></a><strong>. </strong></p>
<p><strong>It is also illegal for anyone to book a property for a registered sex offender, even if they are part of a group booking.</strong> In this case, it will be the lead guest making the booking who is breaking the law.</p>
<p><strong><em>We predict guest screening and sex offender checks will no longer be a ‘nice to have’ feature but a requirement by law across the industry and condition of use for short-term rental operators.</em></strong> </p>
<p>But, besides tighter regulations, there are some other compelling reasons to consider implementing sex offender checks for your guests. This includes: </p>
<ol>
<li><strong>Property Protection:</strong> Screening guests for potential sex offences will help protect your property. It minimises the chances of any illicit activities or incidents occurring within your vacation rental, which can prevent damage, theft, or other undesirable situations.</li>
</ol>
<ol start="2">
<li><strong>Reputation Management</strong>: Prioritising guest safety through sex offender checks enhances your reputation as a responsible property manager/host. It shows that you are proactive in ensuring a secure environment for your guests and can even attract more bookings from safety-conscious travellers.</li>
</ol>
<ol start="3">
<li><strong>Community Relations</strong>: By taking measures to screen guests for sex offences, you contribute to the well-being of the local community. This can foster positive relationships with neighbours, local authorities, and regulatory bodies and demonstrate your commitment to being a responsible member of the community.</li>
</ol>
<p>While sex offender screenings are not legally mandated everywhere, there are definitely pros to incorporating them as part of your guest screening process. </p>
