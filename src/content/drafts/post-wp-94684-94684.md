<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Does Booking.com verify guests?</strong></h2>
<p>All travelers need to provide a verified email address and credit card information to reserve a property on Booking.com. Guests who have used the platform before must have a good history, with no complaints or bans from any properties.</p>
<p>Hosts can also add additional guest requirements. For example, you can adjust your settings so that guests must provide a valid phone number and home address to book.</p>
<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Can you screen guests on Booking.com?</strong></h2>
<p>In 2022, <a href="https://skift.com/blog/booking-com-to-test-verifying-guest-ids-and-offering-payment-alternatives/" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">Booking.com began developing risk management solutions </a>using AI and machine learning to increase safety for hosts on the platform. </p>
<p>According to Booking.com, their screening process includes:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Background checks</strong> to screen guests against global sanctions watchlists.</li>
<li><strong>Fraud scoring</strong> with machine learning models to detect fraud when guests are booking, and measures to protect your reservations.</li>
<li>We conduct r<strong>eservation risk assessments</strong> after guests make a booking in order to take action against bookings that will be canceled.</li>
<li><strong>Guest blocking</strong> to prevent guests with a problematic history from making reservations on Booking.com.</li>
</ul>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>How does Booking.com protect hosts?</strong></h2>
<p>Despite the screening process, property managers listing on Booking.com can still be at risk of expensive damage. </p>
<p>To reduce this risk, Booking.com offers <a href="https://admin.booking.com/partner-liability-insurance.html" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">Partner Liability Insurance</a> to all owners and property managers listing on the platform, for no extra cost. </p>
<p>The insurance, provided by Zurich, offers $1,000,000 in primary liability coverage for all stays booked through the platform. It protects against third-party lawsuits or liability claims for bodily injury or property damage incurred during any stay.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Can you block a guest on Booking.com?</strong></h2>
<p>Another way Booking.com protects hosts is through guest blocking. </p>
<p>The channel encourages all hosts and property managers to <a href="https://partner.booking.com/en-gb/help/first-steps/first-reservations/reporting-guest-misconduct" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">report guest misconduct</a>. You can also report incidents up to seven days after check-out. This gives you time to accurately assess damages. </p>
<p>Once you’ve reported a guest for misconduct, you can choose to block the guest from booking any of your properties in the future. </p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Boosting security on Booking.com</strong></h2>
<p>Here are some things you can do to boost security on the Booking.com:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Turn on Request to Book</strong> — This means guests cannot instantly book your properties, and you can check guests’ profiles before you confirm a booking. </li>
<li><strong>Make your own rules for your properties </strong>on Booking.com. Guests must agree to these rules before booking.</li>
<li><strong>Communicate with guests</strong> — Chat to guests through the Booking.com message platform to get to know the people who’ll be staying in your property, and make sure they understand the house rules. </li>
<li><strong>Report all misconduct</strong> — Booking.com encourages property managers to report all incidences of misconduct to reduce the risks for all hosts. </li>
</ul>
