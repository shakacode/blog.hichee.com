<p class="has-larken-font-family has-text-xxl-font-size">What better way to banish the winter blues than with a bit of last-minute travel? Whether you’re on a tight budget or fancy splashing out on some long-haul winter sun, these destinations are sure to get the year off to an exciting start.</p>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity" />
<h2 class="wp-block-heading has-xl-font-size">1. Dubai</h2>
<p><strong>Glitz, glamour and that coveted winter sun make Dubai an excellent choice for a last-minute January trip.</strong></p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Things to do in Dubai in January</h3>
<ul>
<li>Slide through shark infested waters at <strong><a href="https://www.atlantis.com/dubai/atlantis-aquaventure/aquaventure-waterpark" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">Atlantis Aquaventure</a></strong>: the world’s largest waterpark</li>
<li>Soak up the sun on one of the city’s many <strong>beautiful beaches</strong> – the average high temperature in January is a lovely 24°C</li>
<li>Go <strong>adventuring in the desert</strong>. There are lots of tour companies offering the chance to hit the dunes on quad bikes, 4x4s or even on a camel</li>
<li>Sip cocktails in one of the city’s many <strong>rooftop bars</strong></li>
<li>Get a taste of Middle Eastern culture in <strong>Deira market</strong>. Haggle for jewellery in the gold souk, restock your pantry in the spice souk or discover your new signature scent in the perfume souk.</li>
</ul>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity is-style-wide" />
<h2 class="wp-block-heading has-xl-font-size">2. Bangkok</h2>
<p><strong>You’ll need more than a couple of nights in Bangkok to get to grips with Thailand’s buzzing capital.</strong></p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Things to do in Bangkok in January</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Celebrate Lunar New Year</strong>. Yaowarat (Chinatown) is famous for its exuberant parades with acrobatic performances, dragon dances and firecrackers</li>
<li>Take a longtail boat trip along the <strong>Chao Phraya River</strong>. The tour takes you past modern skyscrapers and ancient temples, giving you a unique viewpoint of the city</li>
<li>Party with backpackers on the infamous <strong>Khao San</strong> <strong>Road</strong></li>
<li>Visit the city’s iconic temples and pay respect to the Buddha. <strong>Wat Pho</strong> is home to the enormous Reclining Buddha, and the nearby <strong>Wat Phra Kaewand</strong> is home to the famous Emerald Budda</li>
<li>Pick up some bargains (and delicious street food) at <strong>Chatuchak Weekend Market</strong></li>
<li>Treat yourself to an invigorating <strong>Thai massage</strong>.</li>
</ul>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading has-xl-font-size">3. Tenerife</h2>
<p><strong>Known as the ‘island of eternal spring’ thanks to its year-round mild climate, Tenerife is a great choice for some last-minute travel in the January sunshine.</strong></p>
<figure class="wp-block-gallery has-nested-images columns-2 is-cropped wp-block-gallery-7 is-layout-flex wp-block-gallery-is-layout-flex">

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Things to do in Tenerife in January</h3>
<ul>
<li>Bask on the <strong>black sand beaches</strong>. The average high temperature in January is 20°C, which is more than pleasant enough for a little bit of sunbathing</li>
<li>Admire the view  from the top of Spain’s tallest mountain, <strong>Mount Teide</strong>. You can either take the cable car or hike to the summit, but either way you’re likely to encounter snow at this time of year – a fun contrast if you’re spending the rest of your trip on the beach</li>
<li>Take a <strong>wine-tasting</strong> tour and discover the island’s unique volcanic wines, sometimes made from ancient grape varieties that were wiped out by a virus on mainland Europe</li>
<li>Go <strong>whale watching</strong>. You can do this in Tenerife year round, but from November to February you’re likely to see migrating species like humpback whales, blue whales and fin whales</li>
<li>Head to the rugged coast and <strong>swim in the rockpools</strong>. These naturally formed swimming pools were created by ancient lava flows, and now provide a unique place to swim that’s a bit calmer than the open ocean.</li>
</ul>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading has-xl-font-size">4. Barcelona</h2>
<p><strong>Get the perfect combination of beach and city with some last-minute travel to Barcelona, Catalonia’s cool capital.</strong></p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Things to do in Barcelona in January</h3>
<ul>
<li>Enjoy one of the most iconic views of the city at <strong>Park Güell. </strong>This UNESCO World Heritage Site is famous for its colourful mosaics and playful architecture, designed by Modernista legend Antoni Gaudí</li>
<li>Pick up a picnic at <strong>La Boqueria</strong>, the sprawling food market just off La Rambla. There are more than 200 stalls selling everything from fresh fruit smoothies to melt-in-the-mouth jamon Iberico</li>
<li>Take a tour of the largest unfinished Catholic church in the world, <strong>La Sagrada Familia. </strong>Gaudí’s masterwork is one of the best things to see in Barcelona, with striking architecture drawing upon natural shapes and Art Nouveau</li>
<li>Temperatures sit at around 15°C in January – a little too cool for sunbathing and swimming, but absolutely ideal for a stroll along <strong>Barceloneta Beach</strong>. Follow it with a bite to eat in one of the many beach bars</li>
<li>Go for a <strong>tapas crawl </strong>in Poble Sec. This small enclave in the shadow of Montjuïc Hill is famous for its pintxo bars, modern restaurants and traditional eateries.</li>
</ul>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading has-xl-font-size">5. İstanbul</h2>
<p><strong>With its unique blend of cultures and wealth of diverse attractions, ultra-chic Istanbul is another top destination for last minute travel this January.</strong></p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Things to do in İstanbul in January</h3>
<ul>
<li>İstanbul straddles Europe and Asia, so hop on a <strong>commuter ferry across the Bosphorus</strong> and visit Asia for the day. Most boats stop in the stylish Kadıköy neighbourhood with its stylish bars and colourful street art</li>
<li>Visit spectacular mosques like the <strong>Hagia Sophia</strong>. Originally a church, it was converted to a mosque, then a museum, and is now a mosque again. Another important building is the <strong>Blue Mosque</strong>, famous for its intricate tilework</li>
<li>Treat yourself to a spa day. Istanbul is famous for its <strong>hammams and Turkish baths</strong>, where you can treat your skin to a traditional body scrub and massage</li>
<li>Come face to face with Medusa in the <strong>Basilica Cistern. </strong>This underground attraction was built in the 6th century to store water for İstanbul’s palaces, and is famous for its mesmerising columns, particularly two column bases featuring Medusa’s upside-down head</li>
<li>Start your day properly with <em>kahvalti – </em>a full <strong>Turkish breakfast</strong>. This veritable feast usually includes cheeses, cold meats, crusty <em>simit</em> bread, jam, honey as well as veggie bits like olives, cucumber and tomato. It’s all washed down with dark Turkish tea, served black in tulip-shaped glasses.</li>
</ul>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Things to do in Paris in January</h3>
<ul>
<li>Treat yourself to designer markdowns in the famous <strong>Paris winter sales</strong>. Labels like Chanel, Valentino and Hermés are known to cut prices by up to 80 per cent towards the end of the month</li>
<li>Admire some of Beyoncé and Jay Z’s favourite sculptures and paintings on a special <strong><a href="https://www.louvre.fr/en/explore/visitor-trails/beyonce-and-jay-z-s-louvre-highlights" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">Louvre Highlights</a></strong> tour of the pieces seen in the power couple’s <em>‘APES**T’</em> music video</li>
<li>Get a glimpse of the city’s macabre side. Tour the ornate <strong>Père Lachaise Cemetery</strong> (the final resting place of musician Jim Morrison and playwright Oscar Wilde) and the <strong><a href="https://www.catacombes.paris.fr/en" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">Paris Catacombs</a></strong> – a labyrinth of bone-lined tunnels underneath the city</li>
<li>Watch the famous <strong>Eiffel Tower</strong> illuminations and light show. It takes place every hour on the hour from dusk until 11:45pm. As the sun sets around 5:20pm in mid-January, you won’t have to stay up too late to see the sparkles</li>
<li>Get local insights into the best places to eat on a <strong>guided food tour</strong>. France is world famous for its gastronomy, from sweet treats and wine to gourmet cheese.</li>
</ul>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading has-xl-font-size">7. Amsterdam</h2>
<p><strong>With its eclectic museums, boutique shopping and famous café culture, there’s good reason why Amsterdam is a popular choice for a last minute trip.</strong></p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Things to do in Amsterdam in January</h3>
<ul>
<li>Immerse yourself in art at<strong> Fabrique des Lumières </strong>– an exhibition using light projections and music to bring to life iconic pieces by artists like Gustav Klimt (of <em>The Kiss </em>fame)</li>
<li>Pay your respects at the <strong><a href="https://www.annefrank.org/en/museum/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">Anne Frank Museum</a></strong>, and see the tiny annexe where Anne and her family hid from the Nazis</li>
<li>Live like a hipster in <strong>De Negen Straatjes (the Nine Streets). </strong>This trendy corner of the Jordaan is home to indie shops, vintage boutiques and plenty of cute cafés where you can enjoy an artisanal lunch</li>
<li>Tuck into street food at <strong><a href="https://foodhallen.nl/amsterdam" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">Foodhallen</a>.</strong> This former tram depo in the Oud-West district is home to 21 street food stalls and bars, selling local delicacies like bitterballen and international favourites like dim sum and tacos</li>
<li>Learn the history of Amsterdam’s most famous beer at the surprisingly fun, interactive <strong><a href="https://www.heinekenexperience.com/en/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">Heineken Experience</a></strong>.</li>
</ul>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading has-xl-font-size" id="h-ready-to-book-your-next-trip">Ready to book your next trip?</h2>
