<p>It&#8217;s important for you to have the right emails sent to your guests at the right time so you can get reviews like this:</p>
<p><em>“Oh my god! Excellent communications”</em></p>
<p>But is there too much communication? or too little? In today&#8217;s episode, I&#8217;ll talk about “Communications” in your Airbnb listing and the five essential emails to send to your guests. I&#8217;ll also discuss how to set them up on Airbnb, and how to set them up on IGMs, which is the property management platform that I use for my listings.</p>

<h3>Email #1: Upon Booking Email</h3>
<p>Let me tell you exactly what you have to say in your 1st email.  Once your guests book, you want to welcome and thank them. You can say like, “thank you for your reservation”.</p>
<p>My HOA requests, the names of guests over 16 years old so I make that request immediately on that first email. My next paragraph is “Please note, my HOA request this, can you please send it to me?” Give them a specific amount of time. For me, I will always ask them 48 hours from that email, because then if they take a little longer, I could tap them again. I don&#8217;t leave it open-ended.</p>
<p>So if you have any requests, like they need to sign some contract, or they need to give you information, ask them in the first email and also give them a time limit.</p>
<p>In the 3rd paragraph, let them know and remind them about the “Check-In and Check-Out” information, like the time of the check-in. Also, tell them that you will provide them with arrival information at a later time.</p>
<h4>Pro Tip: <span>One of the reasons why I don&#8217;t give the address nor arrival information this early is just in case they cancel. </span></h4>
<p>Also, request for an email address as part of your 4th paragraph. One of the reasons for this is in case anything happens with the booking and your account, you have their information. However, not everybody gives their email address, and it&#8217;s okay.</p>
<p><strong>Note:</strong> Once they gave their email address, put it in another document and not leave it on the Airbnb platform. Airbnb will block that information after your guest checks out.</p>
<p>The 5th paragraph gives them a little reminder of the further comfort you provide in the house.</p>
<p>This is how I write my first email right after they book and they get it immediately. And now I use <a href="https://thehostingjourney.com/igms">IGMs</a> for automation.</p>
</div>
</div>
<div class="et_pb_module et_pb_text et_pb_text_6  et_pb_text_align_left et_pb_bg_layout_light">
<div class="et_pb_text_inner">
<h3>Email #2: Before Arrival </h3>
<p>The second email should be right before arrival. I send it in 3 days before guest check-in.  You can give them the name of the listing, and address because sometimes it&#8217;s hard to find the listing address on Airbnb.</p>
<p>Make sure to let them know the number of guests that are coming or the number of guests that are booked for. One of the reasons for that is in case they have more people coming, or more people were added especially if you have guests limitations or you are charging per guest. So you want them to know like, “Oh, this is the number of people you booked”.</p>
<p>Also provide, the checking information again. Now you can provide all the arrival information. You can also let them know the instructions on how to open up the lock.</p>
<p>Let them know about any HOA restrictions, and give them driving instructions. Also, in both emails (1st and 2nd), give them links to your social media accounts for the listings.</p>
</div>
</div>
<div class="et_pb_module et_pb_text et_pb_text_7  et_pb_text_align_left et_pb_bg_layout_light">
<div class="et_pb_text_inner">
<h3>Email #3: After Arrival</h3>
<p>The third email should be right AFTER arrival (the next day). You can send this email after the guests check in the next day at 8:00 in the morning. Your guest gets up and sees an email like, “Hey, I hope you had it tonight. Do you have any questions? I hope you looked at the house manual with all the information”.</p>
<h3>Email #4: Before Checkout.</h3>
<p>The 4th Email should be right BEFORE checkout. Give them the big details, like what to do with the trash, and what to do with the sheets. I tell my guests, “You&#8217;re still on vacation, no need to strip the bed or make it”. People like to make the bed and some people like to strip the bed. I do not want my guests to do that just in case there were any stains. It is easier to see them on a made bed than on a crumpled-up sheet.</p>
<p>I also let them know what to do with the passes and things like that. That&#8217;s the email right 24 hours before the checkout. I also provide this checkout information in the <a href="https://www.thehostingjourney.com/thehousemanual/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">house manual</a>.</p>
<h3>Email #5: After Checkout</h3>
<p>The 5th Email should be 3 Days AFTER the checkout. A couple of days after your guest leave, you can send out a, “Hey, thank you for leaving the house great”. If they do!</p>
<p>And then you can give them a five-star review or if they don&#8217;t leave the house perfectly, don&#8217;t send the 5th email. Even though it&#8217;s automated. What you can do is stop it from going, which is the reason why I have it in the system to be set up and sent about three days after checkout.</p>
<p>And these are your 5 emails!</p>
<p>You need to have amazing communication with your guest. And yes, if my guests ask me questions, I do answer them. It&#8217;s not like I&#8217;m not talking to them, but these are emails that are automated and sent out immediately.</p>
