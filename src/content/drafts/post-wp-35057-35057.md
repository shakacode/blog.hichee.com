<p>Most hospitality hosts want more direct bookings. If you are reading this, you almost certainly want more direct bookings. Direct bookings bring more control and possibly higher profits.<br><br>I&#8217;ve been creating short-term rental sites for 19 years, and in this article, I will share the things that will help your website shine and open the floodgates to direct bookings.</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Introduction</h2>



<p>Before looking at the various ways to increase your traffic, we must understand the marketplace and what we are up against in the form of competition. No matter where your property is located, you are in an over-saturated marketplace.</p>



<p>If I search the term &#8220;short-term rental websites&#8221;, Google returns 22 million results. If I narrow the search to a location, like &#8220;short-term rental websites in Florida&#8221;, Google returns over 83 million results.</p>



<p>Even if we dial in the search, we still see huge competition. If I search &#8220;vacation rental Anna Maria Island&#8221; Google returns well over 3 million results. The island is only 7 miles long and only 2 miles wide at its widest point! According to <a href="https://www.hometogo.com/anna-maria-island/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">HomeToGo</a>, there are just under 5,000 vacation rentals on the island.<br><br>I&#8217;m not sharing the figures to paint a picture of doom and gloom. I&#8217;m just highlighting what we are all up against regarding online marketing. </p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading">What Is Google?</h2>



<p>If you were to ask people &#8220;what is Google&#8221;, nearly everyone would say that it&#8217;s a search engine. But if you want more traffic to your website you need to look at Google differently.<br><br>Yes, Google serves up search results, but it&#8217;s understanding why and how it does this that&#8217;s key to getting more traffic. </p>



<p>Google&#8217;s mission is <strong>to organize the world&#8217;s information and make it universally accessible and useful</strong>. Google want to deliver the most relevant and reliable information available.</p>



<figure class="wp-block-pullquote"><blockquote><p>The takeaway here, is that Google isn&#8217;t searching for answers &#8211; <br>It&#8217;s organizing answers</p></blockquote></figure>



<p>Google actually takes into account over 200 ranking factors that it uses to organize web pages in its search results. </p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Ranking factors without the geek speak.</h2>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Age before beauty</h3>



<p>Let&#8217;s imagine that I&#8217;m a tourist, and I&#8217;ve just arrived on Anna Maria Island, and I fancy a beer. </p>



<p>I&#8217;ll ask the first person that I meet where I can find the best bar on the island. Unfortunately, the first person I meet is a young boy, so he&#8217;s not much help. Luckily the next person to come along is in his 40s, and he gives me directions to his favorite bar.</p>



<p>I was looking for an answer to my question, and age played a part in getting a better answer. This simple premise is one of Google&#8217;s primary ranking factors. </p>
