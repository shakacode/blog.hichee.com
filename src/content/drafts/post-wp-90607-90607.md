<p class="has-larken-font-family has-text-4-xl-font-size">Although more and more travellers are choosing electric car rental, some people remain anxious about doing so. These EV hacks should help you hit the road with confidence on your next trip.</p>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity is-style-wide" />
<h2 class="wp-block-heading has-text-xxl-font-size">Electric car rentals are surging  </h2>
<p>Did you know the share of people buying electric vehicles (EVs) globally has more than tripled in recent years – from 4% in 2020 to 14% in 2022?*</p>
<p>We’re noticing the difference too. The number of travellers hiring EVs via Skyscanner over a petrol or diesel car has tripled over the past 12 months.<br />Many rental companies are shifting their focus to offer lower-emitting options, with some setting themselves ambitious environmental targets involving growing the number of EVs in their fleet.</p>
<p>However, hiring an electric car abroad can still be a little daunting for many, for reasons like where and how to charge it.So here are six helpful tips for hiring an EV successfully on your next trip.</p>
<p class="has-text-sm-font-size">*<a href="https://www.iea.org/reports/global-ev-outlook-2023/trends-in-electric-light-duty-vehicles">IEA 2023 report</a></p>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity is-style-wide" />
<h2 class="wp-block-heading has-text-xxl-font-size">1. Pick the right options between a full EV or hybrid</h2>
<p>Before hiring your rental car, think about what type of EV would suit you and the kind of trip you’re planning – a fully electric vehicle or a hybrid model.</p>
<p>Make sure to check the car’s range. If you’re driving longer distances, then choosing a car which can travel further between charges would be a good idea.</p>
<p>Skyscanner lets you filter by low emissions vehicles that include both EVs and Hybrids, so you can easily find the right one for you</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity is-style-wide" />
<h2 class="wp-block-heading has-text-xxl-font-size" id="voxblock">2. Check your rental company provides access to a charging network</h2>
<p>If your car rental company has partnered with a network of charging stations, this will make life easier – especially if you’re visiting from another continent.</p>
<p>Some companies in Europe, for example, give their customers access to 300,000 charging stations across the continent. It’s always good to check what your rental company offers because in Europe, now, there isn’t one single company which provides access to every charging station – it’s a bit more patchwork.</p>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity" />
<h2 class="wp-block-heading has-text-xxl-font-size">3. Map out your trip in advance</h2>
<p>A little forward planning can save a few headaches later on in the holiday. Check out all the charging sites on your route using maps such as Charge Map or Zapmap to see what’s available and where.</p>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity is-style-wide" />
<h2 class="wp-block-heading has-text-xxl-font-size">4. Ensure your electric car rental has fast charging</h2>
<p>If you want to spend a less time as possible charging your car, then make sure it’s equipped with DC fast charging. This will also allow you to access the increasing number of fast chargers popping up across the network.</p>
<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" src="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/12/Electric-Car-Hire-Article-1.jpg" alt="Person charging an electric vehicle while looking at their phone " class="wp-image-9FD4EB50-FAAD-487B-8FB7AE6C2645BADF" /></figure>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity is-style-wide" />
<h2 class="wp-block-heading has-text-xxl-font-size">5. Check your accommodation has a charging point</h2>
<p>It can really pay to check ahead to see if your accommodation provides EV charging. Recharging your vehicle overnight helps to reduce the overall cost and hassle on your trip. Plus, some hotels will allow you to charge your car for free.</p>
