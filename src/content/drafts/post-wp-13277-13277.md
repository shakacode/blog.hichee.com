<p>A few seasons back, our son finally recovered his lost travel 🧳 baggage, that had been missing in action for over 3 months.
</p>
<p>
So I thought, this would make an excellent post offering a few – 🧳 Lost Luggage Travel Tips, and on how to help prevent your luggage from going missing in the first place. 
</p>
<p>
If it should disappear, here are a few ways to possibly recover that missing bag as well.</p>
<p><strong>This goes for any form of travel, either by; <em>✈ air, 🛳 sea and or 🚍 land</em>.</strong></p>
<p>First, check out this short  📹 video on some of the new high tech gadgets, that make recovering luggage so much easier!</p>

<h3><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Ways_To_Prevent_Lost_Luggage_-_Our_Lost_Luggage_Story"></span>Ways To Prevent Lost Luggage – Our Lost Luggage Story<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>
<p><a href="https://gr8traveltips.com/lost-travel-luggage-tips/airport-bus-shuttle/" rel="attachment wp-att-16651"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" class="aligncenter wp-image-16651 size-full" title="Airport Bus Shuttle" src="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/02/Airport-Bus-Shuttle.jpg" alt="Airport Bus Shuttle" width="600" height="369" srcset="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/02/Airport-Bus-Shuttle.jpg 600w, https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/02/Airport-Bus-Shuttle-300x185-1.jpg 300w" sizes="(max-width: 600px) 100vw, 600px" /></a></p>
<p>Our son was on his way home from the United Kingdom, after spending a term as an exchange student in 🏫 University.
</p>
<p>
He had two pieces of heavy 🧳 luggage with him. One piece made it home, the other was discovered lost, after he stepped off his bus upon arrival at the ✈ airport in the UK.</p>
<p>It was obvious, the 🚌 bus driver had removed one of his 🧳 bags at the first of two airports {Gatwick &#038; Heathrow} in the London area, while en-route.
</p>
<p>
The driver was immediately notified by my son on his stop, and after a search at the previous airport, the bag was – Gone! 
</p>
<p>
A claim was made with the bus company right away, and my son boarded his plane for home, less one bag. 🙁</p>
<p><a href="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/02/lost-bags.jpg" data-rel="lightbox-image-0" data-rl_title data-rl_caption><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" class="aligncenter wp-image-9927 size-full" title src="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/02/lost-bags.jpg" alt="Lost Travel Luggage Tips" width="272" height="164" /></a></p>
<p><b>Lost Travel Luggage Tips – * Note *</b></p>
<p>For ✈ air travel, airlines are not required by law, to reimburse passengers for delayed luggage. Most however, do allow a daily allowance, to assist in purchasing clothing, incidentals and so forth.
</p>
<p>
So it’s important to read the airlines 📖 policies and procedures carefully, before you travel.
</p>
<p>
This is basically the same when traveling by 🚢 sea, and/or taking any form of 🚉 land transportation as well.</p>
<blockquote class="instagram-media" data-instgrm-permalink="https://www.instagram.com/p/BVitULllhni/?utm_source=ig_embed&amp;utm_campaign=loading" data-instgrm-version="12" readability="-5.2090163934426"></blockquote>
<p>Another thing to keep in mind, is that when traveling by land such as 🚍 bus transportation, always keep an 👀 eye on your luggage at 🚏 bus stops if possible.
</p>
<p>
Bus companies can always use the excuse that – someone else must have removed your bag, – that looked the same as yours!</p>
<p><b>Back to the story …</b></p>
<p><a href="https://gr8traveltips.com/lost-travel-luggage-tips/story-telling-cartoon/" rel="attachment wp-att-16652"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" class="aligncenter wp-image-16652 size-full" title="Story Telling Cartoon" src="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/02/Story-Telling-Cartoon.jpg" alt="Story Telling Cartoon" width="400" height="222" srcset="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/02/Story-Telling-Cartoon.jpg 400w, https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/02/Story-Telling-Cartoon-300x167-1.jpg 300w" sizes="(max-width: 400px) 100vw, 400px" /></a></p>
<p>After numerous 📧 emails and ☎ telephone calls to the bus company, the bag could not be found.
</p>
<p>
To add insult to injury, the claim for the bag was denied and as mentioned, because of the possibility that another person must have taken the bag from the curb.
</p>
<p>
A great way out for the bus company and also taking the blame away from the driver.</p>
<h3><span class="ez-toc-section" id="What_To_Do_When_Your_Luggage_Goes_Missing"></span>What To Do When Your Luggage Goes Missing<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>
<p><a href="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/02/Airport-Lost-and-Found.jpg" data-rel="lightbox-image-1" data-rl_title data-rl_caption title><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-29077" src="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/02/Airport-Lost-and-Found-600x386-1.jpg" alt="Airport Lost and Found" width="600" height="386" srcset="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/02/Airport-Lost-and-Found-600x386-1.jpg 600w, https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/02/Airport-Lost-and-Found-300x193-1.jpg 300w, https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/02/Airport-Lost-and-Found-768x494-1.jpg 768w, https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/02/Airport-Lost-and-Found-1200x773-1.jpg 1200w, https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/02/Airport-Lost-and-Found.jpg 1280w" sizes="(max-width: 600px) 100vw, 600px" /></a></p>
<p>Needless to say, without naming the bus company involved, we will never recommend this company to any of our family or friends in the future.
</p>
<p>
Very poor customer service, as it was the driver who removed the bags from his bus as each 🛑 stop.
</p>
<p>
After thinking about this over a period of time, it then occurred to me that the majority of ✈ airports around the world have a – <em><b>🧥 Lost and Found Department.</b></em></p>
<p>Knowing that my son’s bag;</p>
<p><b>Had some – Identifiable Features;</b></p>
<p><b>Had the – Name of the Manufacturer and Label – on the baggage;</b></p>
<p><b>Knew the – Color and Material.</b></p>
<p>I then began to search on the internet for the two airports lost and found departments. 
</p>
<p>
As luck had it, these departments were so organized, that it was easy to track the; day in question, type of article and so on.
</p>
<p>
This made a search for the 🧳 luggage very easy for them and low and behold, there it was, sitting at the wrong airport for 3 months! 😯</p>
<p>To make a long story short, we then contacted the 🚍 bus company in question, and they finally accepted blame for the lost luggage. It was then promptly 📦 shipped home by courier, right to our door.</p>
<h3><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Help_Prevent_and_Recover_Lost_Luggage"></span>Help Prevent and Recover Lost Luggage<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>
<p><a href="https://gr8traveltips.com/lost-travel-luggage-tips/canada-luggage/" rel="attachment wp-att-16650"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" class="aligncenter wp-image-16650 size-medium" title="Canada Luggage" src="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/02/Canada-Luggage-300x300-1.jpg" alt="Canada Luggage" width="300" height="300" srcset="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/02/Canada-Luggage-300x300-1.jpg 300w, https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/02/Canada-Luggage-250x250-1.jpg 250w, https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/02/Canada-Luggage.jpg 600w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a></p>
<p>✔ Take a photograph of your luggage with your cellphone or camera;</p>
<p>✔ Ensure your bag has a name tag inside and outside the bag! (Name, email and/or phone number only);</p>
<p>✔ Add some form of easily identifiable unique feature. (Colored straps, lock, decorative cover and so on);</p>
<p>✔ Know the bags manufacturer name and if a label is attached;</p>
<p>✔ Know the color of the bag and material (cloth or hard case);</p>
<p>✔ Immediately report the lost bag to the appropriate officials and obtain a claim number. </p>
<p>I always try to pack a few necessities and a change of clothing appropriate for my final destination in my <em>🧳 <a title="Air Travel Carry On Luggage Regulations" href="https://gr8traveltips.com/carry-on-baggage-restrictions/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><span>Carry-On Luggage</span></a></em>. 
</p>
<p>
If your travel plans are a week or more, then I would strongly suggest including some form of {travel insurance coverage,} that includes a – 🧳 lost luggage policy. One that offers prompt lost/delayed baggage and contents replacement allowance coverage.</p>
<blockquote class="instagram-media" data-instgrm-permalink="https://www.instagram.com/p/BOFwP1aDg6L/?utm_source=ig_embed&amp;utm_campaign=loading" data-instgrm-version="12" readability="-5.3333333333333"></blockquote>
<p>Imagine arriving on your fabulous <em>🌅 <a title="All Inclusive Vacations Are A Great Choice" href="https://gr8traveltips.com/cheapest-all-inclusive-vacations/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><span>All Inclusive Vacation</span></a></em> to some beautiful 🏝 Caribbean Island, wearing and having only the clothes on your back!
</p>
<p>
No shorts, no bathing suit, no nothing!
</p>
<p>
This is a prime example and depending on our travel destination, why I pack a few clothes in my carry on, especially when taking a beach vacation. 😎
</p>
<p>Losing your luggage and all your belongings can certainly cause a great deal of; stress and anxiety for many people. </p>
<h3><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Lost_Luggage_Travel_Tips"></span>Lost Luggage Travel Tips<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>
<h3><span class="ez-toc-section" id="On_Average_10000_Bags_A_Day_Go_Missing"></span>On Average 10,000 Bags A Day Go Missing<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>
<p><a href="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/lost-luggage.jpg" data-rel="lightbox-image-2" data-rl_title data-rl_caption><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" class="aligncenter wp-image-9929 size-full" title src="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/lost-luggage.jpg" alt="Lost Travel Luggage Tips" width="434" height="244" srcset="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/lost-luggage.jpg 434w, https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/lost-luggage-300x169-1.jpg 300w" sizes="(max-width: 434px) 100vw, 434px" /></a></p>
<p>That’s right! 🙁</p>
<p>Around the world daily, a staggering amount of luggage does not arrive with their owners at their final destination. Luckily, most people’s luggage is returned within {2 days,} to their rightful owners.
</p>
<p>
This is only because most bags are; properly marked, identified and easily tracked. </p>
<h3><span class="ez-toc-section" id="Simple_Steps_To_Prevent_Lost_Travel_Luggage"></span>Simple Steps To Prevent Lost Travel Luggage<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>
<p><a href="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/02/Lost-Luggage-Tips.jpeg" data-rel="lightbox-image-3" data-rl_title data-rl_caption title><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-23085" src="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/02/Lost-Luggage-Tips-600x399-1.jpeg" alt="Lost Luggage Tips" width="600" height="399" srcset="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/02/Lost-Luggage-Tips-600x399-1.jpeg 600w, https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/02/Lost-Luggage-Tips-300x200-1.jpeg 300w, https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/02/Lost-Luggage-Tips.jpeg 640w" sizes="(max-width: 600px) 100vw, 600px" /></a></p>
<p>In concluding this article, here are a few extra tips to help prevent lost or delayed luggage.</p>
<p><em>➡   If you are a frequent traveler, especially by air, do consider some of the new <strong>high tech tracking travel gadgets</strong> available on the market as outlined in the video above.</em></p>
<p><em>➡   Be sure to <strong>remove all previous destination labels, tags and or stickers</strong> as to prevent any confusion in the baggage handling areas.</em></p>
<p><em>➡   Again, <strong>immediately report and complete a lost luggage claim</strong> at the appropriate counter, whether traveling by land, sea or air. Always obtain a claim and contact number before leaving. This will be necessary for insurance purposes as well as for reclaiming your lost luggage in the future.</em></p>
<p><em>➡   Continue to <strong>check with lost and found departments</strong> where it may be possible your luggage has arrived.</em></p>
<p><em>➡   Make your luggage <strong>easily identifiable</strong> and be creative!</em></p>
<p><em><strong>➡   Take a photo</strong> of your luggage. With today’s cell phones and digital cameras, there is no reason not to!</em></p>
<p><em>➡   If you have expensive items and clothing in your lost luggage, then it is advisable to <strong>keep a detailed list</strong> of each item in the bag and its value. Again, this will be necessary for insurance companies and/or dealing with your travel provider for compensation.</em></p>
<p>
<a href="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/11/Final-Thoughts-Banner.png" data-rel="lightbox-image-4" data-rl_title data-rl_caption title><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" src="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/11/Final-Thoughts-Banner.png" alt="Final Thoughts Banner" width="500" height="155" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-33185" srcset="https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/11/Final-Thoughts-Banner.png 500w, https://blog.hichee.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/11/Final-Thoughts-Banner-300x93-1.png 300w" sizes="(max-width: 500px) 100vw, 500px" /></a>
</p>
<p>
Losing luggage is a drag, but above all, try not to let it ruin that long awaited vacation. Remember, it happens to thousands of people each and every day! 
</p>
<p>
Taking a few steps to help prevent loss and prepare yourself ahead of time in the event of loss, will make things a little bit easier to deal at the time.</p>

<p><b>Lost Travel Luggage Tips – Update:</b></p>
<p>Below, are statistics gathered showing the main reasons luggage gets lost during air travel.
</p>
<p>
Not surprising, a whopping 45% of lost bags are when travelers are taking direct or stop over flights!
</p>
<p>
This is when your bags have to be taken off the first aircraft and then found their way onto your next flight.</p>
<p>Other reasons include baggage tagging errors, overweight bags and so forth.</p>
