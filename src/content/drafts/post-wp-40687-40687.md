<h1 class="wp-block-heading">How Can Vacation Rentals Be $40,000+ More To Book On One Platform Than Another?</h1>



<p>I&#8217;ve been in the vacation rental business for over 20 years, but I still see things that shock me. <strong>What you are about to read will shock you too.</strong><br><br>Here at the HiChee blog, we write about some of the best vacation rentals in the world. We&#8217;re lucky. We list 13 million rentals, so we have many beautiful rentals to write about. It&#8217;s a fantastic job looking at the best of everything, like majestic castles, stunning beachside properties, perfectly formed tiny houses, glam glamping options, amazing treehouses, and other unique properties.</p>



<p>As we&#8217;re primarily a vacation rental price comparison website, it&#8217;s also eye-opening to see the price differences that come up. It&#8217;s not unusual to find price differences between platforms of $5,000 for a two-week stay.</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Hold on tight</h2>



<p>We just created a couple of articles on <a href="https://blog.hichee.com/the-most-expensive-airbnbs-in-the-caribbean/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">The Most Expensive Airbnbs In The Caribbean</a> and <a href="https://blog.hichee.com/9-of-the-most-expensive-airbnb-getaways-in-europe/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">The Most Expensive Airbnbs in Europe</a>, and the price differentials are jaw-dropping.<br><br>When writing the Caribbean article (just two weeks ago), one property was  $112,000 more on Airbnb than on VRBO. That property is now $7,213 less on Airbnb than on VRBO. It&#8217;s listing madness.<br><br>The most expensive Airbnbs in Europe article also highlighted a property that was $102,000 more on Airbnb than on VRBO. That price differential between platforms is now $112,000 more on Airbnb than VRBO.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><br>So, what&#8217;s going on?</h2>



<p>These huge price differences could be down to one or more reasons. The following thoughts apply to all listing price differences across online travel agencies.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Dynamic pricing gone rogue?</h3>



<p>The vast majority of potential guests have no idea that some hosts are using dynamic pricing software. This software adjusts prices depending on market supply and demand, seasonal and day-of-week trends, special events and holiday predictions, and the number of days left to book. Prices can be constantly adjusted up or down depending on various factors.</p>



<p>Is this software or the parameter constraints entered by hosts responsible for these pricing differentials?</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Guest demographics</h3>



<p>During Airbnb&#8217;s early years, the company was often described as a media darling. The press showered us with stories about the sharing economy, innovation, and Airbnb&#8217;s fantastic growth. But, as often happens, as the company matured and it became super successful., the press focused on more negative stories. We were inundated with articles about parties, damage, and noise-related issues.</p>



<p>Like it or not, whether it&#8217;s warranted or otherwise, some of that bad press has stuck with hosts and managers. Some operators perceive Airbnb guests as more problematic than those of VRBO. VRBO indeed markets to older, multi-generational groups of guests.</p>



<p>So, in the same way that young drivers pay a higher car insurance premium because they are more likely to have an accident, short-term rental (STR) hosts and management companies may be loading their prices because their perception or experience tells them that Airbnb guests may cause more issues. <a href="http://blog.hichee.com/dont-get-scammed-on-airbnb-find-out-the-ugly-truth-and-save-big-money/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">You can read more about this here</a>.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Traffic jacking</h3>



<p>All e-commerce businesses rely on web traffic. In the case of short-term rental hosts, if no one comes across their properties &#8211; no one can book them. It&#8217;s as simple as that.</p>



<p>Airbnb gets around 3 times as much web traffic as VRBO, so are clever management companies and smart owners using Airbnb as a tool that helps with brand discovery? Even if they don&#8217;t potentially want or expect a booking!</p>



<p>Travelers are getting smarter. They find a property on a platform and then conduct an internet search for other booking options. We see tens of thousands of potential guests comparing prices on rentals every day on HiChee alone. </p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">A lack of (other) brand awareness</h3>



<p>Let&#8217;s face it, Airbnb rules the STR space. According to SEMrush, Airbnb is searched for 9.1 million times a month, whereas vacation rental is only searched for 12,100 times a month (both numbers are for the US). Not only has Airbnb become THE brand, it&#8217;s also become a verb.</p>



<p>Many travelers only use Airbnb. Some travelers are brand loyal to VRBO. Maybe they are just unaware of other booking platforms. So maybe savvy operators are just loading prices as most potential guests will never price-check other booking options. <br><br>Maybe, don&#8217;t ask, don&#8217;t get, is coming into play.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Recouping the commission</h3>



<p><br>Another thing to bear in mind is that Airbnb&#8217;s service fee is in the region of $36,000 for a $180,000 booking. No host or management company is going to absorb that amount of commission. It&#8217;s being passed on to the guest in every case, even though it&#8217;s hidden from them.<br><br>Saying that, both Booking.com and VRBO are also charging commissions, so it doesn&#8217;t explain all of the inconsistent pricing.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Conclusion</h2>



<p>Yes, these are extreme cases, but let&#8217;s face it, very few people would read an article where one listing was $25 more on one listing site than another. But that&#8217;s not the point. We are writing articles daily, and it&#8217;s quite unusual to find listings that are the same price on two or more sites. </p>



<p>The general public, Joe and Jane travelers, don&#8217;t realize that properties are often listed on multiple platforms and at different price points.</p>



<p>It&#8217;s ironic that those same travelers will price-check the cost of flights to their chosen destination with Skyscanner, but they don&#8217;t price-check the big ticket item of the accommodation itself.</p>
