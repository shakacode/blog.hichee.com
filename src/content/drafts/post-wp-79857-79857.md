<p>One of the world’s most quintessential <a href="https://greenglobaltravel.com/wildlife-conservationists-animal-rights-activists/"><strong><span>wildlife</span></strong></a> experiences, the annual Great Migration in Africa is an event like no other.</p>
<p>Millions of Zebras, Wildebeest, and other antelope species make this <a href="https://www.nationalgeographic.org/media/wildebeest-migration/">incredible journey</a> across <a href="https://greenglobaltravel.com/exploring-tarangire-national-park-tanzania/"><strong><span>Tanzania</span></strong></a> and <a href="https://greenglobaltravel.com/meru-national-park-george-joy-adamson/"><strong><span>Kenya</span></strong></a> every year, facing Crocodile- infested waters and terrestrial predators (Lions, Leopards, etc) along the way.<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
<p>To witness such an event in the birthplace of humanity is an emotional experience that could never be fully captured by mere photos or words.</p>
<p>Africa’s Great Migration takes place in some of the most wildlife-rich natural areas in the world, making the experience all that more rewarding.</p>
<p>This is one of those <a href="https://greenglobaltravel.com/category/world_travel_bucket_list/"><strong><span>world travel bucket list</span></strong></a> items that we all dream of experiencing during our lifetime.</p>
<p>Here’s a brief guide on where, when, and why every nature lover should see the Great Migration in Africa!</p>

<h2><b>Why Africa’s Great Migration is Special</b></h2>
<p>Consisting of millions of <a href="https://greenglobaltravel.com/weird-animals-around-the-world/"><strong><span>animals</span></strong></a>, East Africa’s Great Migration is the largest terrestrial <a href="https://www.livescience.com/10235-animals-migrate.html">mammal migration</a> in the world.</p>
<p>Its stage encompasses some of the most breathtaking ecosystems on Earth, including the most well-known reserve on the continent (<a href="https://greenglobaltravel.com/serengeti-national-park-wildlife-safari-photos/"><strong><span>Serengeti National Park</span></strong></a>).</p>
<p>The migration is an endless circuit in which the circle of life is showcased in its purest form.</p>
<p>Like the hands on a clock, a seemingly never- ending stream of fauna make their way across Africa’s landscape in search of food and water, never knowing how much time they may have left.<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
<p>The Great Migration is a famously treacherous journey in which the animals must not only find food to sustain themselves, but also give birth to the future generations that will keep the annual migration alive.</p>
<p>It’s a classic game of survival of the fittest, and its contenders are some of the most majestic and resilient creatures Africa has to offer.</p>

<h2><b>Where to See the Great Migration in Africa</b></h2>
<p>The Great Migration is unlike other natural events you might find elsewhere in the world.</p>
<p>The unpredictable journey’s timing and exact locations are constantly changing, evolving based on current conditions.<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
<p>Rain (and the lack thereof) is the main ingredient that dictates where and when the herds will be during a specific period of the year.</p>
<p>The only thing you can rest assured of is the fact that the event will take place across Tanzania and Kenya.<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
<p>But let’s explore some of the many East African National Parks and reserves that offer the best chance to see this natural spectacle:</p>

<h3><b>Great Migration in </b><b>Ngorongoro Conservation Area (Tanzania)</b></h3>
<p>Located in Tanzania’s north, the <a href="https://greenglobaltravel.com/ngorongoro-conservation-area-tanzania/"><strong><span>Ngorongoro Conservation Area</span></strong></a> is the place to see newborn Wildebeest and Zebras during calving season.</p>
<p>The abundance of young prey also means you might have a greater chance of experiencing Lion, Leopard, and other predator attacks during your visit.</p>

<h3><b>Great Migration in </b><b>Serengeti National Park (Tanzania)</b></h3>
<p>This is definitely one of the best places to witness the Great Migration, as large herds of Wildebeest and Zebra begin to congregate.</p>
<p>The Grumeti River makes for an imposing barrier which the animals need to gather up the courage to cross.</p>
<p>It’s also here where the Wildebeest will begin to mate, hopefully offsetting their population losses during the migration.</p>

<h3><b>Great Migration in </b><b>Mara River (Tanzania &#038; Kenya)</b></h3>
<p>Animals that survive the Grumeti River crossing now face an even more daunting task.</p>
<p>Crossing the turbulent Mara River will give them access to the fertile grasslands of the Maasai Mara.</p>
<p>But the threat of crocodile attacks here increases considerably, often causing great panic among the herds.</p>
<p>This makes the Mara River a great place to witness dramatic attacks, as well as the triumphant herds that manage to make it safely to the other side.</p>

<h3><b>Great Migration in </b><b>Maasai Mara National Reserve (Kenya)</b></h3>
<p>The animals that make it to these feeding grounds can now fatten up before returning to the Ngorongoro to give birth again.</p>
<p>But they also face one of East Africa’s greatest densities of lions.</p>
<p>The survivors will make their way south in a few months, leaving behind the big cats that preyed upon them to defend their home territories.</p>
<p>The <a href="https://greenglobaltravel.com/maasai-mara-conservancies-kenya/"><span><strong>Maasai Mara</strong></span></a> also offers excellent sightings of <a href="https://greenglobaltravel.com/facts-about-elephants-world-elephant-day/"><strong><span>Elephants</span></strong></a> and <a href="https://greenglobaltravel.com/facts-about-giraffes/"><strong><span>Giraffes</span></strong></a>, as well as rarer species such as the Topi.</p>

<h3><b>January-March</b></h3>
<p>At this time of year the great herds of Wildebeest and Zebra are spread out across the southern Serengeti and Ngorongoro Conservation Area.</p>
<p>The majority of females will give birth during a 2-3 week window.</p>
<h3><b>April-May</b></h3>
<p>The herds will now move on to the Moru Kopjes and Seronera areas, en route to the Serengeti’s Western Corridor.</p>
<p>This is not a great time or place to witness the Great Migration, as heavy rains and flooded roads make reaching the herds very difficult.</p>

<h3><b>June-July</b></h3>
<p>The herds will now begin to congregate and attempt to safely cross the Grumeti River. Mating also occurs during this time.</p>
<p>Easily accessible camps along the river offer incredible safari options to witness the migration in progress.</p>
<h3><b>August-October</b></h3>
<p>The herds will cross over from the northern border of Serengeti National Park into the Maasai Mara Reserve during this period.</p>
<p>They will face their hardest challenge, crossing the Mara River, which offers incredible wildlife viewing.</p>
<p>Safaris on the Maasai Mara are wonderful for viewing not only the migration, but also a wide variety of local wildlife.<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>

<h3><b>November-December</b></h3>
<p>Now the herds will begin to make their way south through Loliondo. They attempt to time their arrival on the southern Serengeti plains as the much-needed rains finally begin to fall. The lucky ones will once again give birth to the next generation of East Africa’s migratory wanderers.<span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span><b><i>–by Megan Jerrard courtesy of Green Travel Media</i></b></p>
